\paragraph{Replication Stability.}
To assess the consistency of LLM-generated difficulty estimates across independent runs,
we computed Spearman rank-order correlations between all pairs of the three replications
for each prompt--model--temperature condition.
Across seven conditions with extractable predictions,
the mean pairwise inter-replication correlation was $\bar{\rho} = 0.84$
(range $0.72$--$0.98$).
The \textsc{teacher} prompt exhibited the highest stability
($\rho_{\mathrm{inter}} = 0.98$ for screening; $0.97$ for Gemini),
while more elaborate reasoning prompts such as \textsc{cognitive\_load}
showed moderate replication noise ($\rho_{\mathrm{inter}} = 0.78$ screening;
$0.72$ Gemini).
On average, $30.3\%$ of items shifted rank by more than 20~positions
(out of 140) between any two replications,
with the teacher baseline showing the least rank displacement
(median maximum displacement of 2.0~positions).

Crucially, averaging predictions across three replications consistently
improved ground-truth correlations relative to any single run:
the best screening prompt (\textsc{prerequisite\_chain} at $t{=}0.5$)
rose from single-rep $\rho = 0.63$--$0.67$ to an averaged
$\rho = 0.69$.
This confirms that the three-replication design adopted throughout
our experiments provides a meaningful noise-reduction benefit,
and that inter-replication variance is a predictable property
of the prompting strategy rather than a threat to the validity
of the rank ordering.
