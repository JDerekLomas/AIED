\paragraph{Replication Stability.}
We computed pairwise Spearman correlations between the three independent replications for each condition. The mean inter-replication correlation was $\bar{\rho} = 0.84$ (range 0.72--0.98), but stability varied systematically with prompt complexity. The teacher-role prompt was highly stable ($\rho_{\mathrm{inter}} = 0.97$--$0.98$; only 7--11\% of items shifted rank by $>$20 positions). Structured prompts were substantially noisier: cognitive\_load showed $\rho_{\mathrm{inter}} = 0.72$--$0.78$, with up to 50\% of items shifting $>$20 rank positions between replications.

This presents a clear tradeoff. The teacher prompt produces consistent but less accurate estimates ($\rho_{\mathrm{GT}} = 0.55$); structured prompts produce more accurate but noisier estimates ($\rho_{\mathrm{GT}} = 0.67$--$0.69$ when averaged). Averaging across three replications consistently improved ground-truth correlations: prerequisite\_chain rose from single-rep $\rho = 0.63$--$0.67$ to averaged $\rho = 0.69$. For practical use, our results indicate that structured prompts \emph{require} multi-replication averaging---a single run is unreliable.
