\paragraph{Response Parsing Transparency.}
Each LLM response was parsed by extracting the final numeric value (a probability
in $[0,1]$) from the model's free-text output.  We report completion and parse
rates for all experimental conditions in Table~\ref{tab:parse-rates}.

In Phase~1 (prompt screening, Gemini~2.0 Flash), all 14{,}700 expected responses
were received and successfully parsed (100\% completion, 100\% parse rate across
35 prompt$\times$temperature configurations).

In Phase~2 (model survey), file-level completion varied by model: API-accessed
models (Gemini, GPT-4o, Claude) achieved 100\% completion on attempted prompts,
while locally-hosted models showed 82--93\% completion due to occasional
generation failures.  Among received responses, parse rates exceeded 98\% for
most models.  Two notable exceptions were GPT-4o's \textit{contrastive} and
\textit{simulation} prompts (42--44\% parse rate), where the model produced
extended narrative responses without a final numeric estimate.

Overall, 30{,}178 of 32{,}760 expected response files were present (92.1\%),
and 29{,}613 of those were successfully parsed into numeric predictions (98.1\%).
Correlations reported in the paper are computed only over successfully parsed items;
unparseable responses are treated as missing data rather than imputed.
