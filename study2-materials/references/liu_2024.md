# Liu et al. 2024

## Citation
Liu, Y., Bhandari, S., & Pardos, Z. A. (2024). Leveraging LLM-Respondents for Item Evaluation: a Psychometric Analysis. arXiv preprint arXiv:2407.10899.

## Summary
Tests whether six LLMs (GPT-3.5, GPT-4, Llama 2, Llama 3, Gemini-Pro, Cohere Command R Plus) can serve as synthetic respondents for item calibration. Each model generated 150 responses to 20 College Algebra items from OpenStax, and the resulting IRT parameters were compared to human-calibrated parameters. Finds that no single LLM mimics the human ability distribution, but ensemble combinations with resampling closely approximate human-calibrated item parameters.

## Key Numbers
- GPT-3.5 item difficulty correlation with human: Pearson r = 0.83, Spearman rho = 0.87
- GPT-4: Pearson r = 0.72, Spearman rho = 0.78
- Cohere: Pearson r = 0.65, Spearman rho = 0.72
- Gemini: Pearson r = 0.68, Spearman rho = 0.61
- Llama 3: Pearson r = 0.18, Spearman rho = 0.32
- Llama 2: Pearson r = -0.01, Spearman rho = -0.07
- Best augmentation (50 human + 50 resampled LLM): Spearman rho = 0.93 (vs. 0.89 human-only)
- 20 items, ~50 human respondents, 150 LLM responses per model

## Relevance to Our Work
Most directly comparable -- they also compare multiple LLMs for item parameter estimation. However, they use a single domain (College Algebra), a single prompting approach, and focus on IRT parameter calibration rather than prompt strategy comparison. Our work extends this by systematically varying 16 prompts across 6 models and 3 datasets. Their finding that model choice matters more than ensemble strategies complements our finding about prompt-model interactions. Their wide range of correlations across models (r = -0.01 to 0.83) supports our argument that systematic evaluation is needed.
