**Analysis of Likely Errors:**

Given the student profile (Class 7, Indian government schools, economically weaker sections, limited English, average pass rate ~29%), here's what we can expect:

**1. Language Misunderstandings & Vocabulary Issues:**

*   **"Paragraph":** This word itself might be a barrier. Some might interpret it as just a sentence, or a list of points. They might not understand the concept of interconnected sentences forming a cohesive block of text.
*   **"Hobby":** While "hobby" is a fairly common word, some might confuse it with "game," "sport," or even "subject." They might write about their favourite sport, or even their favourite school subject if they don't fully grasp "hobby" as a leisure activity.
*   **"Mention":** They might not understand that "mention" means explicitly state or describe. They might *imply* the hobby rather than stating it clearly.
*   **"When you do it":** This requires understanding temporal phrases (e.g., "every evening," "on weekends," "after school"). They might struggle with constructing these correctly.
*   **"Why you enjoy it":** This demands expressing reasons and feelings. Students might know *why* they enjoy something in their native language but struggle to translate those sentiments into English. Common simpler reasons like "it is fun," "I like it" might be the extent of their expression.

**2. Grammatical & Syntactical Errors (Attempt but Fail):**

*   **Sentence Structure:** Students will likely struggle with forming grammatically correct sentences, leading to run-on sentences, sentence fragments, or awkwardly constructed phrases.
*   **Tenses:** They might mix tenses (e.g., "I play cricket yesterday and now I enjoy it").
*   **Subject-Verb Agreement:** "He like it" instead of "He likes it."
*   **Prepositions:** Incorrect use of "in," "on," "at."
*   **Articles:** Omitting or misusing "a," "an," "the."
*   **Word Order:** Awkward word order ("Enjoy I it very much").

**3. Not Meeting Paragraph Requirements (Attempt but Fail):**

*   **Sentence Count (4-5 sentences):** Many might write only 1-2 sentences, or a long list of fragmented ideas, failing to meet the length requirement for a "short paragraph."
*   **Cohesion:** Even if they write multiple sentences, they might not flow logically, appearing more as a list of independent statements rather than a cohesive paragraph.
*   **Addressing All Prompts (Hobby, When, Why):** This is a critical point for the rubric.
    *   Some might identify the hobby but fail to mention *when* they do it.
    *   Others might mention the hobby and when, but not *why* they enjoy it, or provide a very generic "I like it."
    *   The "why" is often the hardest part to elaborate on for students with limited vocabulary for emotions and reasons.

**4. Not Attempting at All:**

*   **Lack of Confidence:** Students who feel completely overwhelmed by the English, the concept of a paragraph, or the requirement to express personal thoughts might simply leave it blank.
*   **Understanding Zero:** If they don't understand "hobby" or "paragraph" at all, they will have no starting point.

The most common wrong answers will likely demonstrate *some* attempt but fall short in multiple areas:

*   **Example 1 (Too short/incomplete):**
    > "My favourite hobby is playing cricket. I play it." (Misses "when" and "why," too short)

*   **Example 2 (Grammatically weak, incomplete):**
    > "Cricket is my favourite hobby. I play everyday. I very like it." (Weak grammar, short, "why" is very basic)

*   **Example 3 (Misunderstanding "paragraph"/list format):**
    > "1. My hobby is drawing.
    > 2. I draw in the evening.
    > 3. I enjoy because it is good." (Not a paragraph, fails on cohesion, basic "why")
