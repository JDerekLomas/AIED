Let's break down the likely errors for Class 6 students in Indian government schools, keeping in mind their background and the nature of the question.

**Analysis of Likely Errors:**

1.  **"Which line from the poem..."**:
    *   **Misconception/Misunderstanding:** The students might not understand "line" in the context of poetry. They might think of a physical line drawn, or a "line" as in a queue. They might also not understand "from the poem," especially if they struggle with reading comprehension of the poem itself.
    *   **Attempt/Failure:** They might attempt to recall something from the poem but not specifically a "line." They might just summarize the poem, or pick a word.

2.  **"...do you like the most..."**:
    *   **Misconception/Misunderstanding:** "Like" is a common word, but "most" might be challenging. They might just pick *a* line they like, without understanding the comparative aspect of "most." Some might misunderstand "you" and try to guess what the teacher likes.
    *   **Attempt/Failure:** Most would attempt to express preference, but the "most" might be missed.

3.  **"...and why?"**:
    *   **Misconception/Misunderstanding:** This is the most challenging part.
        *   **Lack of Causal Reasoning:** Explaining *why* requires a higher-order thinking skill (causal reasoning, justification) that is often underdeveloped at this age, especially in a second language and with limited exposure to such analytical tasks.
        *   **Limited Vocabulary for Justification:** Even if they understand the need to explain, their English vocabulary might be insufficient to articulate a reason beyond very basic statements ("because it is good," "because I like it").
        *   **Cultural Context:** In some educational settings, students are more accustomed to rote learning and direct answers rather than subjective interpretation and justification.
    *   **Attempt/Failure:** Many would likely *attempt* to answer this, but fail significantly. They might give a very generic, uninformative "why," or completely omit it.

4.  **Overall Language Misunderstandings and General Challenges:**
    *   **Reading Comprehension of the Poem:** The prerequisite for answering this question is understanding the poem itself. If they don't understand the poem, selecting a meaningful line or explaining why they like it becomes impossible. Given the average pass rate of 29%, it's likely many struggle with basic comprehension.
    *   **Vocabulary:** Words like "denotes," "mentions," "rubric" (though for the grader, not student) and "proportion" are not relevant to the student, but *within the question*, "line," "most," and "why" all pose difficulties in their specific context.
    *   **Sentence Structure/Grammar:** Even if they have the right idea, forming a grammatically correct sentence to express their chosen line and justification will be difficult.
    *   **Fear of Error/Non-attempt:** Some students, especially those struggling significantly, might not attempt the question at all, fearing a wrong answer. They might leave it blank or just write a single word from the poem.

**Most Common Wrong Answers Would Look Like:**

*   **Partial Answer (Line only):**
    *   "The sun is bright." (If that's a line from the poem)
    *   "I like the blue sky."
    *   "Red bird."
*   **Partial Answer (Why only, or very weak justification):**
    *   "Because it is nice."
    *   "I like it good."
    *   "It is good."
*   **Summary of Poem:**
    *   "The poem is about a bird and a tree."
*   **Picking a random word:**
    *   "Bird."
    *   "Tree."
*   **Irrelevant answer/Confusion:**
    *   "My favorite color is red."
    *   "I like to play."
*   **Leaving it blank.**
*   **Copying the entire poem (if short enough), or a large chunk of it.** (This indicates they haven't understood the "line" part or the "most" part).
