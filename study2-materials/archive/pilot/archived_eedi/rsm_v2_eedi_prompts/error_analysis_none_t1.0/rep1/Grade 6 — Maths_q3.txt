    *   **Place Value Confusion (Most Likely Major Issue):** This is the biggest hurdle. Students often misinterpret the "length" of a number as its value. A three-digit number (736) might *look* shorter and therefore "smaller" to some, but to others, they might think more digits mean larger, or vice versa, without a solid understanding of place value.
        *   *Specifically:* A student might see 736 and think, "It has fewer digits, so it's less significant than a four-digit number," leading to it being placed last, or randomly.
        *   *Alternatively, but less likely for "smallest":* Some might focus on the *first digit* only, but even then, 7 (in 736) is larger than 3 (in 3367). However, if they *only* compare the first digit of *all* numbers (ignoring length), it gets tricky.
    *   **Understanding "Ascending Order":** While "ascending" is a common term, some students might confuse it with descending, or simply not grasp the concept of "smallest to largest." They might arrange randomly or in descending order.
    *   **Comparison Skills:** Even with a grasp of place value, comparing multiple numbers, especially when some have different numbers of digits, requires a systematic approach. Students might compare two numbers correctly, then get lost when adding a third or fourth.
