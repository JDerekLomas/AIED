**Analysis of Likely Errors:**

*   **Mathematical Misconceptions:**
    *   **Forgetting the 1/2:** This is the *most common* error in triangle area problems. Students might simply multiply base and height (8 * 6 = 48).
    *   **Confusing area with perimeter:** Less likely given the explicit "area" in the question, but possible for very weak students.
    *   **Incorrect operation:** Some students might add (8 + 6 = 14) or subtract, though less probable for a direct "find the area" question.
    *   **Inability to perform multiplication:** 8 * 6 = 48 or 48 / 2 = 24. While these are basic multiplications, some students from economically weaker sections might have weaker foundational arithmetic skills.
    *   **Not knowing the formula at all:** This would lead to an empty response or a random number.

*   **Language Misunderstandings:**
    *   **"Base" and "height":** While these are fairly common mathematical terms, some students might not fully grasp their meaning, especially if their exposure to geometry in English is limited. However, the numbers 8 and 6 are clearly associated with them.
    *   **"Find the area":** This phrase is direct and usually well-understood in a mathematical context.
    *   **"cm":** The unit is unlikely to cause an error in finding the numerical value, but some might omit it in the answer. The rubric focuses on the formula/calculation, not the units.

*   **Attempt Rate:**
    *   This is a relatively straightforward calculation once the formula is known. Most students will likely *attempt* it, even if they get it wrong. Empty responses would probably be from students who have no idea about triangles or area.

*   **Common Wrong Answers:**
    *   **48 (cm²):** This is by far the most anticipated incorrect answer, resulting from `base × height` without the `1/2`.
    *   **14 (cm):** From adding base and height.
    *   **Random numbers:** From students guessing or having no clue.
    *   **Empty response:** From total lack of knowledge.
