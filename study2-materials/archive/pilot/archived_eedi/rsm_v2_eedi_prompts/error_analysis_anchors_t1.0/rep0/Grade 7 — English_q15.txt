Here's an analysis of likely errors and the estimated pass rate for the 'My Favourite Hobby' question:

**Analysis of Likely Errors:**

*   **Misconceptions about English/Language Misunderstandings:**
    *   **"Paragraph" understanding:** Many students might struggle with the concept of a "paragraph" as a cohesive unit of 4-5 *sentences*. They might list words, short phrases, or unconnected sentences as seen in the "My Favorite Season" example ("Winter\nSweater\nBaju...").
    *   **Sentence structure:** Difficulty forming grammatically correct, complete sentences. Subject-verb agreement, tense consistency, and basic sentence construction are common issues.
    *   **Vocabulary limitations:** Students might know their hobby but lack the vocabulary to express *why* they enjoy it ("relaxing," "creative," "fun," "learn new things"). They might resort to very simple, repetitive language ("I like it because it is good").
    *   **"Hobby" vs. "Activity":** While less likely to completely derail, some might confuse a hobby with a one-off activity or something they *have* to do, rather than something they do for leisure.
    *   **Prepositional phrases/time expressions:** Expressing "when" they do it (e.g., "in the evening," "on weekends," "after school") can be challenging.

*   **Attempt vs. Non-Attempt:**
    *   **High attempt rate:** This is a very common, relatable topic. Almost all students will likely attempt to write something, even if it's very brief or disjointed. It's a personal question that doesn't require factual recall from a textbook.
    *   **Failure to meet length/cohesion criteria:** Many will attempt but fail to produce 4-5 *sentences* that form a coherent paragraph. They might write 2-3 sentences or a list of words.

*   **Common Wrong Answers/Patterns of Errors:**
    *   **Listing, not paragraphing:** "My hobby is cricket. I play cricket. I like it." (3 short sentences, not a cohesive paragraph, might not fully explain *why* it's enjoyable beyond "I like it").
    *   **Fragmented sentences/incomplete thoughts:** "Playing games. After school. Fun."
    *   **Very short and simple sentences, lacking detail:** "I like drawing. I draw everyday. It is good." (Might not meet the '4-5 sentences' implicitly required for a "short paragraph" that also addresses all prompts thoroughly).
    *   **Grammatical errors:** Frequent errors in tense, articles, prepositions, and spelling which might make sentences difficult to understand or cause them to fall below the scoring threshold if too pervasive.
    *   **Missing one or more elements:** A student might mention the hobby and when they do it, but struggle to articulate *why* they enjoy it beyond "it is good" or "I like it," which might not be considered a sufficient explanation for the "why" component.
    *   **Redundancy:** Repeating the same simple idea multiple times instead of elaborating.
