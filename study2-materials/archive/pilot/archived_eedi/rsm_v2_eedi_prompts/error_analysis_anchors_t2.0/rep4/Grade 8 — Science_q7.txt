    *   **Lack of awareness about composting benefits:** While the rubric *only* asks if changes are noticed, the follow-up questions about "reasons" and "beneficial for soil health" would expose deeper science gaps. Even if they get the first part right, they might fail on subsequent parts. (For this specific sub-rubric, it's about acknowledging *any* "significant change").

2.  **Language Misunderstandings:**

    *   "**Reasons and evaluate if this is beneficial for soil health**": This part is more demanding and might require explaining biological processes or using specific terminology like "decomposition," "nutrients," "manure," "compost."

3.  **Attempt vs. No Attempt:**
