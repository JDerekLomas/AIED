Let's break down the likely errors for the question "Describe the poet's writing style."

**Likely Errors and Analysis:**

*   **Misunderstanding "writing style":** This is a relatively abstract concept, especially for students with limited English exposure. They might confuse it with:
    *   **The poet's *mood* or *feelings*:** (e.g., "The poet is sad," "The poet is happy") rather than *how* the poem is written.
    *   **The *story* or *content* of the poem:** (e.g., "The poem is about bargaining," "It's about happiness").
    *   **Literary devices without description:** They might say "figurative language" but not *describe* the style, or just list a device.
*   **Limited Vocabulary:** Even if they understand "writing style," they might lack the English vocabulary to describe it accurately. Words like "simple," "descriptive," "informal," "direct," "figurative," "philosophical," "evocative" might be beyond their regular working vocabulary. They might struggle to find a precise adjective.
*   **Difficulty with Abstraction:** Indian government school students from economically weaker sections often have rote learning approaches and struggle with analytical or abstract questions that require higher-order thinking and sophisticated vocabulary. Describing a *style* is more abstract than recalling facts (like fundamental rights or parts of a story).
*   **Generic/Vague Answers:** They might try to answer but give extremely vague responses like "good," "nice," "easy to understand" which may not meet the "suitable and relevant" criteria for a full score. "Easy to understand" could sometimes pass, but depends on context.
*   **Translating Incorrectly:** They might try to translate a nuanced Hindi concept into English and lose precision.
*   **Attempt but Fail:** This is a strong possibility. Many students will *attempt* this question because it seems relatively short, but their attempts will likely be off-target due to the reasons above. Unlike an empty response, an attempt signals engagement but misunderstanding.
*   **Not Attempting:** Some students will likely leave it blank if they simply don't understand "writing style" or have no vocabulary to answer.

**Common Wrong Answers Would Look Like:**

*   "The poet talks about finding job." (Focus on content)
*   "Poet feels sad." (Focus on emotion)
*   "He is Rabindranath Tagore." (Irrelevant information)
*   "Good." / "Nice." / "Very beautiful." (Too vague, not descriptive of *style*)
*   "Simple." (Could be correct, but depends on poem and might be given without real understanding of what it means as a style)
*   "About freedom." (Theme, not style)
