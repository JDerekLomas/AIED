The question asks for a short paragraph about 'My Favourite Hobby', including what it is, when it is done, and why it is enjoyable. The maximum score is 1, indicating that any complete and relevant answer earns the full score.

**Likely Errors and Analysis:**

*   **Understanding the prompt (Language Misunderstanding):**
    *   **"Short paragraph (4-5 sentences)":** This is a key structural requirement. Students might struggle to write a cohesive paragraph of that specific length. Many might write just a list of sentences, one-liners, or very short fragments, rather than a flowing paragraph.
    *   **"My Favourite Hobby":** While "hobby" is a common word, some students might not grasp its full meaning or distinguish it from "work" or "routine tasks." They might pick a very general activity rather than a specific hobby.
    *   **"Mention what it is, when you do it, and why you enjoy it":** This breaks down the required content. Students might cover only one or two points, missing others. For example, they might state the hobby but not why they enjoy it, or state it but not when they do it.

*   **English Language Proficiency:**
    *   **Sentence Construction:** Many students in this demographic struggle with forming grammatically correct, coherent sentences. Simple sentences are likely to be present, but complex sentences with correct conjunctions, prepositions, and verb tenses might be rare or flawed.
    *   **Vocabulary:** Limited vocabulary might lead to repetitive language or difficulty expressing *why* they enjoy something beyond "I like it" or "It is good."
    *   **Spelling:** Common words might be misspelled, though often comprehensible enough not to disqualify the answer if the content is there.
    *   **Punctuation and Capitalization:** Frequent errors in commas, periods, and capitalization at the beginning of sentences are common.
    *   **Cohesion/Flow:** Linking sentences together to form a true paragraph (as opposed to just a collection of sentences) is a significant challenge for students with limited writing practice.

*   **Content Generation:**
    *   **Lack of a "Hobby":** Some students, particularly from economically weaker sections, might not have many defined "hobbies" beyond helping with household chores or playing simple games. They might invent one or pick something generic like "playing" or "reading."
    *   **Expressing Enjoyment:** Explaining *why* they enjoy something requires slightly more abstract thinking and vocabulary than simply stating *what* it is or *when* it is done. This part is often the weakest.

*   **Attempt vs. Failure:**
    *   Most students will attempt this question because "My Favourite..." prompts are common and relatively approachable.
    *   However, many attempts will likely fail due to not meeting all the rubric requirements (e.g., too short, not a paragraph, missing one of the three required elements, severe grammatical errors making it unintelligible).
