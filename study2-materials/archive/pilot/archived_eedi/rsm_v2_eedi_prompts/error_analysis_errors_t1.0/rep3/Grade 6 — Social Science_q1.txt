**Analysis of the Specific Question:** "Name any two major mountain ranges located in India."
Rubric: Names one correct mountain range. Max score: 0.5 (This means partial credit for just one correct answer).

**Likely Errors and Their Causes:**

1.  **"Empty Response" / Non-Attempt (High Probability):**
    *   **Lack of Knowledge:** Many students simply might not have been taught or retained this specific geographical fact. Geography might be a weaker area for some, or the curriculum coverage was insufficient.
    *   **Language Barrier:** While "Name any two major mountain ranges" is relatively simple English, for students with limited English, it might still pose a hurdle. "Major" or "located" could be misunderstood, leading to confusion and abandonment.
    *   **Overwhelm/Anxiety:** Given the overall low pass rates, many students might be prone to giving up quickly if they don't immediately know the answer, especially if they struggled with earlier questions.
    *   **Common Wrong Answer:** "Empty Response"

2.  **Misconceptions / Incorrect Information (Moderate Probability):**
    *   **Confusing with Rivers/Plains/Deserts:** Students might recall other geographical features and list them instead (e.g., "Ganga," "Thar Desert," "Deccan Plateau"). They know *something* about India's geography but not specifically mountains.
    *   **Listing States/Cities:** A general lack of geographical understanding might lead them to list familiar place names (e.g., "Delhi," "Mumbai," "Rajasthan").
    *   **Listing Neighboring Country Mountains:** If they have some general knowledge of mountains, they might list mountains from nearby countries, not specifically *in India*.
    *   **Common Wrong Answer:** "Ganga River," "Thar Desert," "Delhi," "Mount Everest" (if they don't specify it's not *fully* in India's major ranges for the rubric).

3.  **Attempt but Fail to Recall/Spell Correctly (Moderate Probability):**
    *   **Partial Recall:** They might remember "Himalaya" but struggle with "Aravalli" or "Vindhya." Since only one is needed for partial credit, this is less of a complete failure for the rubric but still an error in getting *two*.
    *   **Severe Misspellings:** Similar to "Ashok seamb," they might attempt to spell "Himalayas" as "Himaleah" or "Aravalli" as "Aravli." The rubric states "Names one correct mountain range," implying a recognizable attempt at the name. Minor spelling errors are usually tolerated for "naming," but major ones might not be.
    *   **Common Wrong Answer:** "Himaleya," "Arvali," "Vinda."

4.  **Providing Only One Correct Answer (High Probability, for *partial* credit):**
    *   Many students might know "Himalayas" due to its prominence but struggle to recall a second one like Aravalli, Vindhya, Satpura, etc.
    *   **Impact on Score:** For this specific rubric (0.5 points for one correct), listing only one is a "pass." This is crucial.
