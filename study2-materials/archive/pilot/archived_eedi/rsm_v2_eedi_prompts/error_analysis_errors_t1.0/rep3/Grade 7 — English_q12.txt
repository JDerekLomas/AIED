**Analysis of Likely Errors for "Change the following sentence from Active Voice to Passive Voice: The wind blew the leaves away."**

**1. Misconceptions about English / Language Misunderstandings:**

*   **Lack of understanding of "Active Voice" and "Passive Voice" concepts:** This is the most fundamental barrier. Many students will simply not know what these terms mean, even if they can vaguely understand the individual words. The grammatical terminology itself is complex.
*   **Difficulty identifying Subject and Object:** To change voice, one needs to correctly identify the subject ("the wind") and the object ("the leaves"). This might be a challenge.
*   **Confusion with Tense:** The original sentence is in Simple Past. Students might struggle to maintain the correct tense in the passive voice (using "were blown" instead of "are blown" or "is blown").
*   **Incorrect use of "be" verb:** Understanding that the passive voice requires a form of "to be" (is, am, are, was, were) plus the past participle is a major hurdle. They might just add "by the wind" without changing the verb form.
*   **Incorrect use of Past Participle:** "Blown" is an irregular verb. Students might incorrectly use "blew" again or create a non-existent past participle like "blowed."
*   **Prepositional phrase "by the wind":** While some might grasp this, others might omit it or use an incorrect preposition.
