Let's break down the likely errors for the question: "What are the main occupations of people in the rural areas of Rajasthan? Describe any one in brief."

**Analysis of Likely Errors:**

Given the student demographic (economically weaker sections, limited English, Class 7) and common errors from similar Social Science questions, here's a detailed breakdown:

1.  **Empty Response/No Attempt (Very High Probability):**
    *   **Reasoning:** Similar to the 9-12% pass rates on other questions, a significant portion will simply leave it blank. This could be due to:
        *   Lack of knowledge about Rajasthan's specific economy.
        *   Difficulty understanding the question in English.
        *   Feeling overwhelmed by the "describe any one in brief" part.
        *   General disengagement or lack of confidence.
    *   **Likely Impact:** This will be the single biggest factor pulling down the pass rate.

2.  **Misconceptions about Social Science/General Knowledge (High Probability):**
    *   **Focus on Urban/Industrial Occupations:** Students might list occupations they are familiar with from their own urbanizing environments or from general media, such as "factory work," "shopkeeping" (though shopkeeping *can* exist in rural areas, it might not be a *main* occupation compared to the expected answers), "construction" without specifying its rural context.
    *   **Generic Occupations:** They might list very generic occupations that aren't specific to rural Rajasthan, e.g., "doctor," "teacher" (while present, not *main* occupations defining the rural economy in the way agriculture is).
    *   **Lack of Specificity:** Even if they get close, they might say "work" or "job" without naming specific occupations.
    *   **Mixing up States/Regions:** They might list occupations relevant to other parts of India (e.g., fishing, if they know about coastal areas).

3.  **Language Misunderstandings (High Probability):**
    *   **"Occupations":** While this is a common word, some students might struggle with it. They might interpret it as "daily activities" rather than "ways of earning a living."
    *   **"Rural areas":** Some might not fully grasp the distinction between "rural" and "urban," leading them to list urban jobs.
    *   **"Rajasthan":** The lack of specific geographical knowledge about Rajasthan (its climate, topography, traditional economy) will directly impact their ability to name relevant occupations. They might only know it's a "desert state" but not translate that into economic activities.
    *   **"Describe any one in brief":** This part requires synthesis and expression, which is challenging for students with limited English. Even if they name an occupation, describing it briefly will be an additional hurdle. Many might only list, failing the second part of the question.

4.  **Attempt but Fail (Moderate to High Probability):**
    *   **Partial Correctness:** A student might correctly name "farming" but struggle to name a second, or fail to describe one. The rubric requires *at least two* *and* a description.
    *   **Incorrect Description:** Even if they name an occupation, their description might be vague, inaccurate, or simply a repetition of the name.
    *   **Listing without Understanding:** They might recall a word like "agriculture" but not truly understand what it entails or why it's main in Rajasthan.

5.  **Most Common Wrong Answer:**
    *   **"Empty Response":** This is almost guaranteed to be the most frequent.
    *   **Partially correct/vague list:** "Farmers, jobs, shops." (Fails to meet "two main occupations" clearly and describe one.)
    *   **Incorrect occupations:** "Factories, offices."
    *   **Single correct occupation, no description:** "Agriculture." (Fails on the second part of the rubric.)
