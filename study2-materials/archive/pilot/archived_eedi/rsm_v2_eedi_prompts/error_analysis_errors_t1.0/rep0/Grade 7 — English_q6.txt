Let's break down the likely errors for the question "Describes the poet's writing style." given the context of Class 7 Indian government school students from economically weaker sections with limited English.

**Common Errors & Misconceptions:**

1.  **Misunderstanding "Describes"**:
    *   **"I am reading."**: This wrong answer is a classic example of students interpreting "describes" as "What are you doing?" or "What activity are you engaged in related to the poet/poem?" They might focus on *their own action* rather than the *description of an attribute*.
    *   **Focus on the act of reading/studying**: They might think the question is asking them to *engage with* the poem rather than *analyze* it.

2.  **Misunderstanding "Poet's"**:
    *   They might confuse "poet's" with "poem's" theme or general content.
    *   They might think it's asking about the poet's life or personal details, not their artistic choices.

3.  **Misunderstanding "Writing Style"**: This is the biggest hurdle.
    *   **Lack of vocabulary for literary analysis**: Students at this level, especially with limited English, are highly unlikely to possess the specific vocabulary needed to describe a writing style (e.g., "simple," "descriptive," "informal," "direct," "figurative," "narrative," "lyrical," "metaphorical," "flowery," "concise"). They might not even have equivalents for these terms in their native language in an analytical context.
    *   **Abstract concept**: "Writing style" is an abstract concept that requires meta-cognition about text. They are more accustomed to factual recall or direct comprehension tasks.
    *   **Confusion with "theme" or "story"**: As seen with the "Last Bargain" example, students often default to describing the plot or the main idea rather than the *how* of the writing. They might say something about what the poem is *about*, not how it's *written*.
    *   **Literal interpretation**: They might think "style" refers to physical attributes of the writing (e.g., "neat handwriting," "clear words").

4.  **No Attempt/Empty Response**: This is highly probable given the difficulty of the vocabulary and the abstract nature of the question. If they don't understand *what* is being asked, they won't even know how to begin formulating an answer. The 4% pass rate for this exact question supports this.

5.  **Partial understanding leading to irrelevant answers**:
    *   Similar to "My Favourite Hobby" where they list games or food, they might list words they associate with "poem" or "reading" but are not style descriptors (e.g., "nice poem," "good words," "beautiful," "long"). These are evaluative or general descriptors, not stylistic ones.
    *   They might pick out random words from the poem itself if they don't understand "style" as a concept.

*   **"Empty Response" / No Attempt**: Highly likely due to complete lack of understanding.
*   **"I am reading." / "I like the poem." / "It is a good poem."**: Focusing on their own action or a general evaluation rather than analysis.
