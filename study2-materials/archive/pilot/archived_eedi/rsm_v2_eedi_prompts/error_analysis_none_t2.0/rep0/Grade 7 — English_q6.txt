**Analysis of Errors and Misconceptions:**

Given the student profile (economically weaker sections, limited English, average pass rate 29%):

*   **Misconceptions about English:**
    *   **Vocabulary Gap:** The most significant issue will be understanding "describes," "poet's," "writing style." They might mistake "style" for "type" (e.g., of poem) or "fashion."
    *   **Grammatical Structure:** Even if they recognize some words, combining them into a coherent understanding of the question ("Describe the *writing style* of the poet") will be challenging. They might struggle with the possessive "poet's" or the noun phrase "writing style."
    *   **Ambiguity of "Style":** "Style" has multiple meanings. They might think of it as "how well" the poet writes (good, bad) rather than "how" they write (descriptive, simple).

*   **Language Misunderstandings:**
    *   **"Describes":** This verb might be misunderstood. They might think it means "tells a story about" or "is about" rather than "gives characteristics of."
    *   **"Writing style":** This is the core term. Many will likely not grasp its meaning. They might interpret it as:
        *   **The subject matter of the poem:** What the poem is about (e.g., "nature," "love," "patriotism").
        *   **The feeling of the poem:** Happy, sad, angry.
        *   **The type of poem:** Story poem, short poem.
        *   **The physical appearance of the writing:** Neat, messy (less likely, but possible confusion with "style" in penmanship).
        *   **A personal characteristic of the poet:** Good, kind.

*   **Attempt vs. Not Attempt:**
    *   Given the single-word answer requirement, many *will attempt*. They won't want to leave it blank, and a one-word answer feels achievable even if guessed.
    *   However, their attempt will often be based on a misunderstanding of the question.

        *   **Random vocabulary they know related to poetry:** "Rhyme," "stanza."
        *   **Simply rewording part of the question as an answer:** "Poet," "writing." (Unlikely for a 1-word answer, but reflects confusion).
        *   **An attempt to say "good" or "beautiful"** if they've inferred it's a positive assessment of the poet.
