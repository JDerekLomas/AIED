Given the student profile (Class 8, Indian government schools, economically weaker sections, limited English, 29% average pass rate), this question presents significant hurdles.

1.  **Direct Vocabulary Barrier:**
    *   **"Define":** Many students might not understand "define" beyond providing a direct translation (if they even know it). They might be used to answering direct questions rather than providing a *definition*. They might interpret it as "What is a fable?" but then still struggle with the *description*.
    *   **"Fable":** This is the core vocabulary word.
        *   **Unknown Word:** The most common issue will be simply not knowing what a "fable" is. Even if they've encountered fables in their local language, they might not know the English term.
        *   **Partial Knowledge:** They might know *a* fable (like the Crow and the Pitcher or the Hare and the Tortoise) but not the *definition* of the genre itself. They might try to describe a specific story instead of defining "fable."
        *   **Confusion with Similar Words:**
            *   **Story/Tale:** They might use "story" as a generic synonym, but that's not specific enough for a "define" question.
            *   **Myth/Legend:** Less likely given Class 8, but possible if they have a broader "old story" category in their minds.
            *   **Poem/Rhyme:** Unlikely for "fable" but possible if they simply don't understand the question and default to another literary form.

2.  **English Language Comprehension and Production:**
    *   **Grammar/Syntax Issues:** Even if they *know* the elements of a fable (animals, moral), constructing a grammatically correct sentence or two to convey this will be extremely challenging for students with limited English.
        *   Incorrect verb tenses ("it is animals used")
        *   Missing articles ("story for animal")
        *   Incorrect prepositions ("story by moral")
        *   Sentence fragments.
    *   **Limited Lexicon for Explanation:** They might not have the English words for "moral lesson," "imparts," "typically uses," "characters."

    *   **Difficulty with Abstraction:** Defining a literary genre requires abstract thought about its characteristics, which is harder than recounting a specific story.
