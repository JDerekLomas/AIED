## Contrastive Analysis of the Question:

**"A gardener has collected waste of plants, vegetables and fruits in a pit. Does gardener notice any change after some days? Give reasons and evaluate if this is beneficial for soil health"**

Let's break down the factors influencing the difficulty for Class 8 Indian government school students with limited English proficiency:

**1. Prior Knowledge Assumed:**

*   **Decomposition:** This is the core concept. Students need to understand that organic matter breaks down. This is usually covered in Class 6-8 science, often in chapters on ecosystems, waste management, or the carbon cycle. They might know terms like "manure," "compost," or "khad" (Hindi).
*   **Microorganisms:** A basic understanding that tiny living things are responsible for decomposition is crucial.
*   **Benefits of Manure/Compost:** Knowledge that decomposed organic matter enriches soil is fundamental to the "beneficial for soil health" part.
*   **Basic vocabulary:** "gardener," "collected," "waste," "plants," "vegetables," "fruits," "pit," "change," "reasons," "evaluate," "beneficial," "soil health." Most of these are fairly common, but "evaluate" might be a slight stretch for some.

**2. Language Demands:**

*   **Relatively straightforward language:** The question uses simple sentence structures and common words. There aren't many complex technical terms that would be specific to advanced science.
*   **Concrete scenario:** The scenario of a gardener collecting waste is relatable and visualizable, which helps understanding even with limited English.
*   **"Evaluate":** This term, as mentioned, can be a bit more demanding than simply "describe" or "explain." It requires them to form a judgment and justify it.
*   **Length:** The question is not excessively long, which reduces cognitive load from reading comprehension.

**3. Cognitive Demands (Recall, Application, Reasoning):**

*   **Recall (moderate):** Recalling the process of decomposition and the concept of microorganisms, as well as the benefits of organic matter for soil.
*   **Application (low-moderate):** Applying the recalled knowledge to a specific, simple scenario (waste in a pit).
*   **Reasoning (moderate):**
    *   **Part 1 ("Does gardener notice any change?"):** Requires observation/prediction based on prior knowledge. This is relatively easy.
    *   **Part 2 ("Give reasons"):** Requires explaining *why* changes occur (decomposition, microorganisms). This is slightly harder, requiring a causal explanation.
    *   **Part 3 ("evaluate if this is beneficial for soil health"):** This is the highest cognitive demand. It requires forming a judgment (beneficial) and justifying it with scientific reasoning (adds nutrients, improves soil structure, etc.).

**4. Difficulty Compared to Typical Textbook Exercises:**

*   **Comparable to moderate textbook exercises:** This question is similar to a short-answer or explanation question often found at the end of a chapter on waste management, soil, or agriculture.
*   **More demanding than simple recall:** It's not a fill-in-the-blank or a one-word answer. It requires constructing a coherent explanation.
*   **Less demanding than complex problem-solving:** It doesn't involve calculations or multi-step experimental design.
