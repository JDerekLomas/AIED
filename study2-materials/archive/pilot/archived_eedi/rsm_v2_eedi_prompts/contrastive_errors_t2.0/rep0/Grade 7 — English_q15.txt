Let's break down the question "Write a short paragraph (4-5 sentences) about 'My Favourite Hobby'. Mention what it is, when you do it, and why you enjoy it." using the provided context.

**Contrastive Analysis:**

*   **Prior Knowledge Assumed:**
    *   **Concept of a hobby:** This is generally understood, even with limited exposure, as an activity done for pleasure. It's a very common concept introduced early in language learning.
    *   **Basic vocabulary related to common hobbies:** Students likely know words for cricket, drawing, reading, singing, etc., in English or can easily transliterate from Hindi.
    *   **Basic time expressions:** "in the evening," "on weekends," "after school."
    *   **Basic expressions of enjoyment:** "I like it because," "it makes me happy."
    *   **Understanding of "paragraph" and "sentence":** This is where some students might struggle, confusing a paragraph with just a few disconnected sentences or not adhering to the sentence count.

*   **Language Demands for Students with Limited English:**
    *   **Simple Sentence Construction:** The prompt asks for direct information ("what it is," "when you do it," "why you enjoy it"). This allows for very simple subject-verb-object structures. For example: "My hobby is playing cricket. I play it every evening. I enjoy it because it is fun."
    *   **Cohesion and Coherence:** While asking for a "paragraph," the prompt's clear bullet points (what, when, why) intrinsically guide the student to structure their response, even if their linking words are weak or absent. This makes it less demanding than a general topic essay.
    *   **Vocabulary:** The vocabulary required is highly personal and common. Students choose their own hobby, so they can use words they are familiar with. They are not forced to describe a complex abstract idea or analyze a poem.
    *   **Grammar:** Present simple tense ("I play," "I enjoy," "it is") will suffice. Complex tenses are not needed.
    *   **Conjunctions/Connectors:** Basic connectors like "and," "because" are useful but not strictly necessary for conveying the core information. Simply stating "My hobby is X. I do it at Y time. I enjoy it because Z." can get them credit.

*   **Recall, Application, or Reasoning?**
    *   This is primarily **Application** with elements of **Recall**. Students are recalling personal information about their hobby and applying it to the given structure. It requires very little abstract reasoning or interpretation of complex text, unlike the poem-related questions.

*   **Difficulty Compared to Typical Textbook Exercises:**
    *   This type of prompt ("Write a few sentences about yourself/your favourite thing") is a *very common introductory writing exercise* in English language textbooks at elementary and early secondary levels.
    *   It is significantly **easier** than questions requiring analysis of writing style (4% pass rate), understanding abstract themes of a poem (5% pass rate), or even the common errors on *this very prompt* show.

The key difference here is the explicit mention of "4-5 sentences" and the breakdown "what it is, when you do it, and why you enjoy it" provides scaffolding that a simpler "write about your hobby" might lack, potentially helping students meet the rubric more closely *if they understand those explicit instructions*.
