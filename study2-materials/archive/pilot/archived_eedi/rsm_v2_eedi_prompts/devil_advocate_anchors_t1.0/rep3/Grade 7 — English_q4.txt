The question asks students to connect the king's questions from the passage to real life and provide one reason. The king's questions are: "the right time to do everything, the right thing to do, and the right people to work with."

2.  **Connect to Real Life:** This is an application/inference step. Students need to understand the practical relevance of these questions.
3.  **Provide a Valid Reason:** This requires basic reasoning and the ability to articulate that reason in English.

**Challenges:**
*   **Limited English:** While the king's questions are stated, explaining *why* they are important in "real life" might be difficult for students with limited vocabulary or grammatical structures. They might grasp the concept but struggle to express it.
*   **Abstractness:** "Importance in real life" can be a bit abstract. Students might struggle to bridge the gap from a story to their own experiences or general human experience.
*   **"Why" questions:** "Why" questions often have lower pass rates because they require reasoning and explanation, not just recall.
*   **Passage Dependence:** If a student didn't understand the original passage well enough to identify the king's questions (as indicated by the 50% recall rate), they certainly won't be able to explain their real-life importance.
