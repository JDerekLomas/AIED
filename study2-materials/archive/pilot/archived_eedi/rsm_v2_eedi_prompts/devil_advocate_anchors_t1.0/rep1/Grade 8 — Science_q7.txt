I'll break down the thought process for estimating the pass rate for the "gardener" question.

1.  **Analyze the Question:**
    *   "A gardener has collected waste of plants, vegetables and fruits in a pit." - This sets the context: composting.
    *   "Does gardener notice any change after some days?" - Direct question about observation.
    *   "Give reasons" - Requires explanation of *why* changes occur.
    *   "and evaluate if this is beneficial for soil health" - Requires an evaluative judgment and linking it to soil health.

2.  **Deconstruct the Rubric:**
    *   "The user suggests that the gardener will notice significant changes." - This is the core requirement for earning points.
    *   Max score: 0.5 - This implies it's a relatively low bar to get *some* credit, but not a full understanding. The prompt is only asking for the *proportion correct* (meaning at least 0.5 points).

3.  **Consider the Target Audience (Class 8 Indian Government School Students):**
    *   **Limited English:** This is crucial. While "gardener," "plants," "vegetables," "fruits," "pit," "days," "change" are fairly common, "beneficial" and "evaluate" might be slightly harder. The overall sentence structure is manageable.
    *   **Economically Weaker Sections:** Often, these students have less exposure to science concepts outside of school, and less home support for academics.
    *   **Average Pass Rate (29%):** This is a key anchor. It tells us that most questions are challenging for these students.
    *   **Exam Anxiety / Blank Responses:** A significant factor. Many students will just leave it blank.
    *   **"Basic" Concepts Aren't Basic:** Composting, decomposition, and soil health are taught in science, but retention and the ability to articulate them in English are different.
