*   **Understanding "Paragraph" and Sentence Structure (Fundamental English Skills):**
    *   **Misconception:** Students might not grasp the concept of a "paragraph" as a cohesive unit of related sentences. They might write isolated sentences or a list.
    *   **Misconception:** "4-5 sentences" might be interpreted literally without connecting ideas, leading to disconnected statements or just counting words.
    *   **Common Error:** Writing single, fragmented sentences. e.g., "My hobby cricket." or "Play everyday."
    *   **Common Error:** Writing a list of hobbies/likes rather than focusing on *one* favourite hobby and describing it. (Seen in provided examples).
    *   **Common Error:** Starting multiple sentences with "My favourite hobby is..."

*   **Grammar and Vocabulary Limitations:**
    *   **Misconception:** Difficulty with verb tenses (especially present simple for regular hobbies).
    *   **Misconception:** Limited vocabulary to describe *why* they enjoy it. They might resort to very simple, repetitive words or struggles to articulate emotions.
    *   **Common Error:** Grammatical errors in simple sentence construction (subject-verb agreement, articles, prepositions). e.g., "I enjoying cricket." "I play cricket school ground."

    *   **Common Error:** Word choice issues that obscure meaning.

*   **Addressing All Parts of the Prompt ("what," "when," "why"):**
    *   **Language Misunderstanding:** While "what" is relatively straightforward, "when you do it" and "why you enjoy it" require more complex sentence structures and vocabulary.
    *   **Cognitive Load:** Trying to juggle three specific requirements while struggling with basic English. Students might only address one or two.
    *   **Common Error:** Mentioning the hobby but failing to state when they do it or, more commonly, why they enjoy it. The "why" is often the hardest part.
    *   **Common Error:** Giving a vague "when" (e.g., "everyday") without more detail or failing to give specific times/situations.

*   **Effort and Attempting vs. Not Attempting:**
    *   **Attempt but Fail:** Most students will attempt this question as it relates to personal experience, making it seem accessible. However, their attempts will be riddled with the errors mentioned above.
    *   **Not Attempt:** Some students might still leave it blank if their English proficiency is extremely low and they feel overwhelmed. However, compared to more abstract questions, the "My Favourite Hobby" question encourages attempts.
