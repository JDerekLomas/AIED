**The Question:** "Which line from the poem do you like the most and why?"

**Rubric:** "Denotes a line from the poem and mentions why." (Max score: 1.5 - suggesting some partial credit opportunities)

**Analysis considering common errors and teacher overestimation:**

1.  **"Which line from the poem..."**:
    *   This requires identifying a *specific line*. This might be hard for students struggling with basic reading comprehension, even if they've generally understood the poem. They might give a summary or a favorite *idea* instead of a literal "line."
    *   Referring back to the poem requires active recall or looking back, which can be challenging under exam conditions.
    *   Some might copy the first line they remember, even if they don't particularly "like" it, just to fulfill the "line" requirement.

2.  **"...do you like the most..."**:
    *   This asks for a personal opinion/preference. While seemingly simple, students from very different educational backgrounds might struggle with articulating personal preferences in a formal exam setting, especially in a foreign language. They might not understand *why* their liking is relevant to the question.
    *   "Empty Response" is a very real possibility here if they can't easily formulate a "like."

**Challenging initial instincts:**

My first instinct might be: "It's just about personal preference, that's easy."

But then I remember:

*   **Limited English:** Can a student with "My hobby is cricket play" articulate *why* they like a specific poetic line? Unlikely. They might understand "good" but not have the words for "it made me feel hopeful" or "it beautifully described..."
*   **"Basic" concepts aren't basic:** Expressing and justifying personal opinions in a formal written setting is a relatively advanced skill, especially when syntax and vocabulary are limited. It's not "basic" for struggling learners in a foreign language.
*   **Exam anxiety:** "Empty Response" is a prevalent issue. If they can't easily retrieve a line or immediately think of a *why*, they might panic and leave it blank, or give a minimal, incorrect response.
*   **Never practiced this exact question type:** Even if they've learned about poems, a specific question asking for personal preference *and* justification might be new.
