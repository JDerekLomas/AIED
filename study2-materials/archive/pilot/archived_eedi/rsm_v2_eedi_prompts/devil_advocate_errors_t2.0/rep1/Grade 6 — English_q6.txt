Let's break down the question and common student difficulties to arrive at a calibrated estimate.

**Understanding the Question and Rubric:**
*   **Part 1: "Which line from the poem do you like the most..."** This requires students to identify a specific line. They need to recall or find it in the provided text.
*   **Part 2: "...and why?"** This is the more challenging part. It requires students to articulate a reason for their preference. This involves:
    *   Comprehension of the chosen line.
    *   Formulating an opinion/reason in English.
    *   Expressing that reason coherently.

*   **Rubric: "Denotes a line from the poem and mentions why."** This means both parts *must* be present and understandable for *any* score, even if minimal.

**Specific Difficulties for This Question:**

*   **Locating a line:** While seemingly simple, students might struggle with attention, rapid reading, or recalling specific phrases.
*   **Comprehension:** They need to understand the *meaning* of the line to genuinely "like" it for a reason, not just pick it randomly.
*   **Expressing Personal Preference:** This involves more than just reciting facts or rephrasing text; it requires introspection and linguistic tools to convey it.
*   **Formulating "Why":** This is the highest hurdle. It requires:
    *   Using "because."
    *   Constructing a grammatically sound sentence/phrase to explain the reason.
    *   The reason itself needing to be intelligible. (e.g., "I like this line because it's good" might not get full marks if "good" isn't elaborated, and some might only manage "It is good" without the preceding phrase).
