**Rubric: "Denotes a line from the poem and mentions why."**

*   "Mentions why": Requires articulation of a reason, however simple. This is where most students will fail based on the examples.

**Applying the Common Errors and Teacher Overestimation Principles:**

*   **Limited English:** Many students will understand "line" and "like," but struggle significantly with "why." They might pick a line, but then say something generic or unrelated for the "why."
    *   Example likely incorrect answer: "I like this line: 'Walk on water' (the poem talks about this line). Because I like the water." (Does not adequately explain *why they like this line in the poem*).

    *   Example likely incorrect answer: They copy a line, then write "Empty Response" for the "why."

*   **Exam Anxiety:** Could lead to copying a line and then writing nothing else for the "why."
*   **Never Practiced This Question Type:** Many exams focus on recall (Who is the poet? What happened in stanza 3?). This question demands a personal, reflective response, which is a higher-order thinking skill and requires different English structures.
