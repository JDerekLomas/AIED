Let's break down the question and the context for Class 8 Indian government school students.

**Understanding the Question:**

*   **"A gardener has collected waste of plants, vegetables and fruits in a pit."** This sets up the scenario: composting. Most students would understand "gardener," "waste," "plants," "vegetables," "fruits," and "pit."
*   **"Does gardener notice any change after some days?"** This asks about the *observable* outcome of composting.
*   **"Give reasons..."** Requires understanding *why* the change happens (decomposition).
*   **"...and evaluate if this is beneficial for soil health."** This asks for the *purpose/benefit* of composting.

**Challenging Initial Assumptions & Considering Constraints:**

1.  **English Language:**
    *   "Collected waste," "pit," "notice any change," "after some days," "give reasons," "evaluate," "beneficial," "soil health."
    *   While individual words might be known, the phrasing "Does gardener notice any change... Give reasons and evaluate if this is beneficial..." is a multi-part, somewhat formal question. Students with limited English might struggle to grasp the full scope or nuance.
    *   "Evaluate" can be a challenging verb for many.

2.  **"Basic Concepts":**
    *   **Composting/Decomposition:** This is a core topic in science (often biology or environmental science) for Class 7/8. They *should* have been taught it.
    *   **Microorganisms:** The underlying reason for decomposition (bacteria, fungi) might be known in theory, but linking it directly to the "change" in the pit and articulating it as a "reason" might be harder.
    *   **Benefits for Soil Health:** Concepts like "manure," "nutrients," "fertility" are also taught.

3.  **Exam Anxiety/Question Type:**
    *   This is not a simple multiple-choice or fill-in-the-blank. It requires a descriptive answer, explaining cause and effect, and then an evaluation. This is a higher-order thinking skill.
    *   Many students might give a one-word answer for "change" (yes) and "beneficial" (yes) but struggle with "reasons" and "evaluate."
    *   "This exact question type" - while decomposition is common, this specific phrasing with all its parts might be new.

4.  **Absenteeism/Prior Knowledge:**
    *   Given the average pass rate of 29%, it's highly likely that a significant portion of students either missed this topic, didn't fully grasp it, or struggle with recall and articulation.
    *   Practical experience with composting is probably very low, even for those from agricultural backgrounds, as "gardener" implies a somewhat systematic approach rather than just discarding waste.

**Estimating the Proportion:**

*   **Yes, they will notice change (and say yes):** A good portion will likely get this first part correct by saying "yes." Maybe 60-70%.
*   **Giving reasons (decomposition):** This is where it gets harder. Some might say "it rots" or "it decays." Fewer will correctly mention microorganisms. Maybe 30-40% will provide a passable "reason."
*   **Beneficial for soil health (and evaluation):** Many will likely say "yes, it's beneficial." Fewer will clearly explain *why* (adds nutrients, makes soil fertile, acts as manure). Maybe 35-45%.
*   **Connecting all parts coherently:** This is the biggest challenge. A student needs to answer all three parts (change, reasons, beneficial) with reasonable accuracy and detail.
