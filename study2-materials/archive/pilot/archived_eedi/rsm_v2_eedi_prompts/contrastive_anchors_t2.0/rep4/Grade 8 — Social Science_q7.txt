## Analysis of Question Difficulty

**Question:** How did the Battle of Plassey in 1757 contribute to the expansion of British power in India?

**Rubric:** For explaining that the victory at Plassey led to the establishment of British political dominance/control over Bengal (e.g., placing Mir Jafar as a puppet nawab). Max score: 1

**Prior Knowledge Assumed:**
*   **Battle of Plassey:** Students need to know about the Battle of Plassey itself â€“ its occurrence in 1757, that it was a British victory, and the key players (Clive, Siraj-ud-Daulah). This is standard Class 8 Social Science history curriculum in India.
*   **British East India Company:** Students should understand that the British East India Company was operating in India and seeking to expand its influence.
*   **Concepts of Political Dominance/Control:** Understanding what "political dominance" or "control" means in the context of one power over another is crucial.
*   **Puppet Rulers:** The concept of a "puppet nawab" (like Mir Jafar) is a key outcome and requires understanding of political manipulation.

**Language Demands:**
*   **Vocabulary:** "Contribute to," "expansion," "dominance," "control," "political" are moderately challenging. "Puppet nawab" might be new to some, but often explained in textbooks.
*   **Sentence Structure:** The question is a direct "how did X contribute to Y" type, requiring an explanatory answer rather than a simple recall of facts. This requires some synthesis and articulation in English.
*   **Abstract Concepts:** Discussing "expansion of power" and "political dominance" requires handling somewhat abstract historical concepts.

**Cognitive Demand:**
*   **Recall:** Students need to recall facts about the Battle of Plassey and its immediate aftermath.
*   **Reasoning/Application:** This is not purely recall. It requires students to understand the *causal link* between the victory at Plassey and the *subsequent expansion of British power*. It's a "how did it contribute?" question, implying an understanding of consequences and implications, not just the event itself. This pushes it beyond simple recall.
*   **Synthesis:** To articulate the contribution, students need to synthesize information about the battle's outcome and its political implications.

**Comparison to Typical Textbook Exercises:**
*   Textbook exercises often ask for definitions, dates, names, or causes/effects in a more straightforward manner. A question like "What was the significance of the Battle of Plassey?" is similar. "How did it contribute?" is slightly more open-ended and requires a clear logical flow in the answer.
*   Students might easily recall that "Plassey was a British victory" but struggle to articulate *how* that victory specifically led to *political expansion* beyond a general sense of British strength. The "puppet nawab" detail, if remembered, makes the explanation much stronger.
