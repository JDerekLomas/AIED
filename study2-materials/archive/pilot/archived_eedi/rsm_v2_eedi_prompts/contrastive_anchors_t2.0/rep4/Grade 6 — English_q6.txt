This question requires students to:
1. **Understand the question:** "Which line from the poem do you like the most and why?" This is relatively straightforward English, common in simple comprehension tasks.
2. **Recall/Identify a line from a *known* poem:** The assumption is that students have read and at least somewhat comprehended the poem "The Last Bargain." This poem, by Rabindranath Tagore, is likely part of their Class 6 curriculum, meaning they would have been taught it in class, potentially with explanations in their native language (Hindi).
3. **Express a preference:** "do you like the most." This asks for a personal opinion, not a factual recall.
4. **Justify their preference:** "and why?" This requires a simple explanation.

**Contrastive Analysis:**

*   **Prior Knowledge:** Assumes students have read "The Last Bargain" and have a basic understanding of its content. This is a reasonable assumption for a Class 6 curriculum-aligned test. It also assumes a basic understanding of subjective preference (liking something).
*   **Language Demands:**
    *   "Which line...": Easy.
    *   "...from the poem...": Easy.
    *   "...do you like the most...": Easy.
    *   "...and why?": Easy.
    *   The core difficulty lies in articulating *the line itself* in English (if they copy it or rephrase it) and then explaining *why* they like it, also in English. Students with limited English might struggle to express nuanced reasons, but they can often provide very basic reasons (e.g., "it is good," "it teaches freedom," "it is about happiness").
*   **Cognitive Task:** This is primarily an application/reasoning task based on personal interpretation, not just recall. It's more open-ended than recalling facts. However, the 'why' can be very simplistic to meet the rubric (max score 1.5, implying even a short, basic reason could get credit).
*   **Difficulty compared to typical textbook exercises:** Questions like "What is your favorite..." or "Why do you like/dislike..." are very common in early language learning and literature comprehension. It's less demanding than summarizing a theme or differentiating concepts.

**Conclusion:**

The question asks for a simple personal preference tied to a poem and a basic justification. The English required for both the line (copying it) and the "why" can be minimal (e.g., "I like this line because it is about freedom"). Given that the poem is likely taught in their curriculum (potentially in Hindi), students will have an understanding of its content. The hurdle is primarily articulating their preference in basic English. Compared to "My favorite season" (30%), this requires less sustained writing but is more constrained by the poem.
