An adjective *phrase* would be a group of words that functions like an adjective (e.g., "a bird *very tiny in size*" or "a bird *with bright red feathers*"). "A tiny bird" is a noun phrase, where "tiny" is an adjective. So, if a student correctly identifies "tiny" as an adjective, they technically haven't identified an *adjective phrase* according to the grammatical definition. However, if they interpret "adjective phrase" broadly to mean "the word that functions as an adjective," or if they are trained with simplified definitions, they might pick "tiny."
