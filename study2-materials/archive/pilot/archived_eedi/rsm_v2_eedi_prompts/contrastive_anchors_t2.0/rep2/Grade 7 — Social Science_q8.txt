## Contrastive Analysis: "Main occupations in rural Rajasthan"

**Difficulty Factors for this SPECIFIC question:**

*   **Prior Knowledge:** This question directly assesses knowledge of specific regional geography/social studies content relevant to India.
    *   **Assumed:** Students should have been taught about different types of occupations (primary, secondary, tertiary) and perhaps specific regional characteristics in India, including those of Rajasthan. Class 7 social science textbooks in India *do* cover different types of livelihoods, often including examples from various regions. Rajasthan is a prominent state, so its general characteristics (desert, agriculture, animal husbandry) are usually touched upon.
    *   **Potential Gaps:** While general knowledge of occupations might exist, linking them specifically and accurately to "rural Rajasthan" could be challenging if the curriculum didn't emphasize this particular state or if retention is poor.
*   **Language Demands:**
    *   The question itself ("What are the main occupations of people in the rural areas of Rajasthan? Describe any one in brief.") uses relatively straightforward English.
    *   "Occupations," "rural areas," "Rajasthan," and "describe in brief" are common vocabulary.
    *   The challenge would be in recalling the correct terms for occupations in English (e.g., "agriculture," "animal husbandry," "handicrafts"). If taught in Hindi, students would need to translate accurately.
    *   Describing one in brief might require a simple sentence or two, which could be an issue for those with very limited English writing skills. However, the rubric (max score 1) suggests that simply naming two is enough to score, or a very minimal description is acceptable for full credit.
*   **Cognitive Task (Recall/Application/Reasoning):** This is primarily a **recall** question. Students need to remember facts about Rajasthan's economy. There's a slight application component in linking the general concept of occupations to a specific region, but it's largely rote recall.
*   **Comparison to Typical Textbook Exercises:** This type of question is very common in social science textbooks and exams. It's a factual recall question about regional characteristics, which are standard curriculum topics. The "describe in brief" part adds a minor element, but given the 1-point rubric, the expectation is low.
