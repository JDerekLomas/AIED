**Difficulty Compared to Typical Textbook Exercises:**

*   **Elapsed Time:** Calculating elapsed time is a common Class 4-6 topic. The difference here is crossing the "noon" barrier from am to pm (10 am to 4 pm), which can be slightly trickier than, say, 10 am to 2 pm (without going past noon).
*   **Rate Problems (Simple):** Very simple rate problems are introduced around Class 5-6, often involving speed or "cost per item." This problem is structurally similar, just with temperature.
*   **English Language Barrier:** This is the most significant differentiating factor. Textbook exercises in Hindi medium would be in Hindi, or simpler English if it's an English class. A full word problem like this, *in English*, would be considerably harder for many government school students who might primarily use Hindi for math. Many might grasp the numbers ($30^0C$, $2^0C$, $10$ am, $4$ pm) and some action word but miss "increases by" or "per hour."
*   **Multi-step:** It's a two-step problem: calculate duration, then calculate temperature change. This is standard for Class 6 but can be an obstacle for students with weak comprehension.
