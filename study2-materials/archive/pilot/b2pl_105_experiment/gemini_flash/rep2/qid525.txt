**Analysis:**
*   **The "Add across" trap (D):** Students who add the whole numbers ($2+2=4$), then add the numerators ($7+1=8$) and denominators ($10+4=14$) will arrive at $4 \frac{8}{14}$. This is a very common error at Below Basic and Basic levels.
*   **The "Improper Fraction" trap (A):** The answer $4 \frac{19}{20}$ is equivalent to $\frac{99}{20}$. A student who converts to improper fractions correctly ($\frac{27}{10} + \frac{9}{4} = \frac{54}{20} + \frac{45}{20} = \frac{99}{20}$) but fails to read the instruction "Leave your answer as a mixed number" will choose A.
*   **The "Common Denominator Error" (C):** This usually comes from multiplying denominators ($10 \times 4 = 40$) but losing track of the numerators or failing to simplify.
*   **The Correct Path (B):** $2 + 2 = 4$. For the fractions: $\frac{7}{10} + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{14}{20} + \frac{5}{20} = \frac{19}{20}$. Result: $4 \frac{19}{20}$.
