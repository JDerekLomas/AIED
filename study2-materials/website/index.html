LLM-Based Synthetic Student Research

Can Large Language Models authentically simulate student misconceptions?

Research Overview

This research investigates whether LLMs can serve as "synthetic students" for educational applications—pretesting items, simulating learner populations, and validating instructional interventions.

Key Finding: The Reasoning Authenticity Gap

In pilot data (N=208 errors across 7 models), we find a gap of 41.3 percentage points: LLMs select target distractors 50% of the time but show matching reasoning only 8.7% of the time.

This suggests LLMs mimic error outcomes without authentic error cognition.

Theoretical Framework

We ground our work in three cognitive science traditions:

Knowledge Component Theory

Koedinger et al.

Specify what the student knows and doesn't know

Misconception-as-Theory

Chi et al.

Specify the flawed belief system the student holds

Buggy Procedures

Brown & Burton

Specify the step-by-step algorithm the student executes
