Step 1: Specific procedural steps
1. Recall the definition of pasteurization as a thermal treatment process.
2. Identify the specific sequence of operations: Heating followed by Rapid Cooling.
3. Identify the specific medium (Milk).
4. Identify the purpose (Preservation/killing microbes).
5. Translate these conceptual components into a coherent English sentence or structured description.

Step 2: Buggy rules and systematic errors
1. "Temporal Disconnection": Describing heating but forgetting the critical "quick cooling" step (the most common procedural omission).
2. "Purpose Confusion": Confusing pasteurization with "boiling" (a common household practice in Indian homes), leading to the error that milk must be boiled for a long time.
3. "Step Reversal": Reversing the order (cooling then heating).
4. "Incomplete Retrieval": Identifying that it kills germs but failing to describe the *process* as requested by the prompt.
5. "Linguistic Substitution": Using vague Hindi-influenced English terms (e.g., "making it hot and cold") which may not meet the scientific "describe" criteria depending on the strictness of the evaluator.
