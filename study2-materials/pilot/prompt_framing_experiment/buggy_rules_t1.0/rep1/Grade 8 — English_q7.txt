Step 2: Systematic Buggy Rules
1. **The "Last Word of First Line" Bug:** Students assume the first word of the first line and the last word of the last line must be the answer, regardless of sound.
2. **The "Orthographic Matching" Bug:** Students look for words that *look* alike (same ending letters) rather than sound alike (e.g., they might reject "rain/grain" if they over-focus on the 'r' vs 'g' but might incorrectly pair words like "tough/though").
3. **The "Semantic Association" Bug:** Students pair words that are contextually related rather than phonologically related (e.g., "rain/shelter" or "grain/borrow").
4. **The "Grammar Fixation" Bug:** Correcting the text's grammar ("He had [to] repay") instead of performing the rhyming task.
