Step 1: Specific procedural steps
1. **Linguistic Decoding**: Translate the English prompt into Hindi/native conceptual framework, identifying "adaptations" as physical/behavioral traits.
2. **Concept Retrieval**: Access the specific mental schema for "desert plants" (e.g., Cactus) from long-term memory.
3. **Attribute Selection**: Isolate one specific functional trait (roots, leaves, or stems) that relates specifically to water conservation or acquisition.
4. **Functional Linking**: Connect the trait to its purpose (e.g., "spines" to "preventing water loss").
5. **English Encoding/Production**: Formulate a written response in English that sufficiently conveys this causal link to satisfy the rubric.

Step 2: Identified "Buggy Rules"
1. **Category Over-generalization**: Identifying a desert animal trait (e.g., "hump for fat") instead of a plant trait.
2. **Feature Isolation without Function**: Listing a feature (e.g., "Cactus has thorns") without explaining the adaptation/benefit (saving water), which may fail the "Explain" command.
3. **Transliteration Error**: Using a Hindi term (e.g., *jad*) but failing to provide the English equivalent or the specific qualifier (e.g., "long").
4. **Keyword Association Bug**: Writing everything known about deserts (hot, sand, camels) rather than specific plant adaptations.
5. **Reverse Logic**: Stating a trait but misattributing the function (e.g., "thick leaves to absorb sunlight").
