Step 2: Buggy Rules / Cognitive Errors
1. Linguistic/Translation Bug: Misinterpreting "thrice" as "plus three" (additive instead of multiplicative reasoning) or "twice" due to phonetic confusion/limited English.
2. Temporal Additive Bug: Calculating both future ages (17 and 41) but adding them together ($17 + 41 = 58$) or adding 5 to the current difference ($24 + 5 = 29$).
3. Misinterpreting the Goal: Providing the father's future age (41) as the final answer instead of the difference.
4. "Age Gap Growth" Misconception: The intuitive but incorrect belief that as people get older, the gap between their ages changes (failing the invariance principle).

Many students will correctly find 36. However, a significant portion will either stop there, add 5 to 36 and provide 41, or add 5 to the difference and provide 29. The linguistic barrier of the word "thrice" and the conceptual trap of the temporal shift significantly lower the success rate.
