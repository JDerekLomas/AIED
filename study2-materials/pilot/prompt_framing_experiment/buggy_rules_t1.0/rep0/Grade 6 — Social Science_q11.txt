Step 2: Buggy Rules (Systematic Errors)
1. "The One-Reason Bug": Providing only one reason because the cognitive load of the second half of the question displaces the "two reasons" constraint.
2. "Tautological Reasoning": Writing "It is important to follow rules so people follow rules" (failure to identify external impact).
3. "The Translation Slip": Thinking in Hindi (e.g., "Challan se bachne ke liye") but failing to find the English vocabulary, leading to omission.
4. "The List-to-Paragraph Mismatch": Listing rules (e.g., "Wear a helmet") instead of explaining *why* they are important or how they help the *community*.
5. "The Rubric Paradox Bug": Student sees 1 mark and provides a minimalist answer, failing to address the "two reasons" and "community" components required by the prompt structure.

Open-ended "Why/How" questions are significantly more difficult than "What" questions for this group. The disconnect between a 1-mark maximum and a multi-step prompt often leads students to underserve the answer.
