Step 1: Procedural Steps
1. Comprehend the word problem and identify the relationship (1 unit = 10 units, therefore 5 3/4 units = $10 \times 5 3/4$).
2. Convert the mixed fraction $5 3/4$ into an improper fraction ($23/4$).
3. Set up the multiplication: $10 \times (23/4)$ or $(10 \times 5) + (10 \times 3/4)$.
4. Execute the multiplication: Multiply numerator by 10 ($230/4$) or simplify/cancel common factors ($5 \times 23 / 2 = 115/2$).
5. Convert the result into a decimal (57.5) or a mixed number ($57 1/2$) for the final distance.

Step 2: Buggy Rules/Systematic Errors
- Improper conversion: $5 3/4 \to 5 \times 3 + 4 = 19/4$ or $5 \times 4 + 3 = 23/3$ (swapping numerator/denominator).
- Fraction as digits: Treating $5 3/4$ as $5.3$ or $5.34$.
- Operator selection: Attempting to divide ($10 \div 5 3/4$) due to keyword confusion or "larger number must be dividend."
- Whole-number-only: Multiplying $10 \times 5 = 50$ and ignoring the fraction.
- "Add numerator": $10 \times 5 3/4 = (10 \times 5) + 3/4 = 50 3/4$.
