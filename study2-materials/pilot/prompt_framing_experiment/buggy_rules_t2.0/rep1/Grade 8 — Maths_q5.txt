Step 1: Specific Procedural Steps
1. Pattern Recognition: Identify $9/16$ as the common factor across two terms.
2. Factoring (Inverse Distribution): Rewrite the expression as $\frac{9}{16} \times [\frac{4}{12} + (-\frac{3}{9})]$.
3. Integer Operation: Process the addition of a negative fraction ($+$ and $-$ becoming $-$).
4. Fraction Simplification (Pre-computation): Ideally simplifying $4/12$ to $1/3$ and $3/9$ to $1/3$.
5. Rational Arithmetic: Find a common denominator to subtract the fractions inside the parentheses.
6. Zero Property: Recognize that $1/3 - 1/3 = 0$.
7. Multiplicative Property of Zero: Compute $\frac{9}{16} \times 0 = 0$.

Step 2: Buggy Rules & Systematic Errors
1. Partial Distribution: Attempting to multiply first (as per BODMAS/PEMDAS habits) rather than using the property, leading to high computational load and calculation errors.
2. Sign Confusion: Treating $+\ (-\frac{3}{9})$ as an addition of a positive, or incorrectly applying "negative times negative" rules to an addition problem.
3. Common Factor Mismanagement: Factoring out $9/16$ but failing to keep the plus sign, or "losing" one of the $9/16$ terms without placing the remaining terms in parentheses.
4. Denominator Addition: Adding across the denominators $(12+9)$ and numerators $(4-3)$.
5. Distributive Identity Error: Assuming distributive property means $a(b) + c$ instead of $a(b+c)$.
