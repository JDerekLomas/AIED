Step 1: Procedural Steps
1. Recall and translate the semantic phrase "Perimeter of a rectangle" into the algebraic formula $2(l + w) = P$.
2. Substitute the given values into the formula: $2(10 + w) = 32$.
3. Distribute the constant or divide by 2: $20 + 2w = 32$ or $10 + w = 16$.
4. Isolate the variable $w$ using inverse operations (subtraction): $w = 16 - 10$ or $2w = 12$.
5. Recall the formula for area: $Area = l \times w$.
6. Execute multiplication using the given length and the calculated width: $10 \times 6$.
7. Provide the final answers with correct units (cm and sq cm/$\text{cm}^2$).

Step 2: Buggy Rules / Systematic Errors
- **Formula Confusion:** Swapping perimeter and area formulas ($l \times w = 32$ or $2l + 2w = Area$).
- **The "Add and Done" Bug:** Simply adding 32 and 10 or subtracting 10 from 32 without accounting for the $2 \times$ multiplier.
- **Operator Misapplication:** When solving $20 + 2w = 32$, a student might add 20 to 32 instead of subtracting ($2w = 52$).
- **Partial Execution:** Finding the width ($w=6$) but stopping there, failing to perform the second half of the multi-step prompt (Area).
- **Distributive Failure:** In $2(10 + w) = 32$, solving as $20 + w = 32$, resulting in $w = 12$.
