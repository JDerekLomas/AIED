Step 1: Specific procedural steps
1. Semantic Retrieval: Distinguish between 'Environment', 'Natural Environment', and 'Human Environment' (a common point of confusion in NCERT Geography).
2. Definitional Mapping: Formulate a statement explaining that human environment encompasses human interactions, creations, and activities.
3. Identifying Causality: Connect human need/intent to a specific physical alteration of the natural world (modification).
4. Example Generation: Recall or construct a concrete instance (e.g., building roads, cutting forests, industrialization).
5. Language Encoding: Translate these abstract spatial concepts into a written sentence in English/Hindi that aligns with the rubric's keywords.

Step 2: Buggy Rules / Systematic Errors
1. "The Keyword Collapse": Using "human environment" and "human-made environment" (infrastructure) as synonyms, failing to include the "social/interactive" aspect of the definition.
2. "The Definitional Loop": Defining human environment simply as "where humans live" (generalizing from the root word) rather than the "creation/interaction" model.
3. "Example-Definition Mismatch": Providing an example of a human-made object (bridge) instead of the process of *modifying* (e.g., clearing land to build the bridge).
4. "The Environment Generalization": Defining environment in general (biotic/abiotic) instead of the specific sub-type requested.
