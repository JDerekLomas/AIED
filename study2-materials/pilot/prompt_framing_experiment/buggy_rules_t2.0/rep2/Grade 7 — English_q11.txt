Step 2: Systematic buggy rules
1. "Single-Word Bug": Students define "phrase" and "word" synonymously, leading them to pick the first descriptive word they see.
2. "First-Noun-Modifier Bug": Assuming the adjective always precedes the first noun (often works for simple sentences, fails for complex ones).
3. "Position-Based Selection": Selecting any word that isn't a noun or verb based on its position in the sentence.
4. "Label Confusion": If the student actually knows what a phrase is, they will fail because the correct answer to the question (an actual phrase) isn't "tiny," but the rubric expects "tiny."
5. "The Rubric Mismatch": Stronger students may search for a phrase (e.g., "very tiny" or "in our balcony") and be confused when they only see a single adjective, while weaker students will simply look for a "describing word."
