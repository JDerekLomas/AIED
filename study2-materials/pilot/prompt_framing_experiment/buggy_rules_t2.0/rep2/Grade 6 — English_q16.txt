Step 1: Specific procedural steps
1.  **Semantic decoding:** Comprehend the English prompt and identify the core task (identifying and naming a season).
2.  **Domain knowledge retrieval:** Recall the English names for seasons (Winter, Summer, Monsoon/Rainy, Spring) from the mental lexicon.
3.  **Orthographic encoding:** Retrieve the correct spelling for the chosen season name.
4.  **Selection/Categorization:** Isolate one specific season from the set to satisfy the "naming" requirement of the rubric.
5.  **Output production:** Write the name within the context of a paragraph (as per the instructions).

Step 2: Common "Buggy Rules" / Systematic Errors
1.  **Phonetic Spelling Bug (L1-L2 Transfer):** Students may apply Hindi phonetics to English spelling (e.g., "Samer" for Summer, "Munsun" for Monsoon).
2.  **L1 Substitution Bug:** Using the Hindi word (e.g., "Sardi", "Garmi", "Baarish") because the English vocabulary hasn't been consolidated in long-term memory.
3.  **Task Misalignment Bug:** Providing the description/activity (e.g., "I like rain") but failing to explicitly name the season (Monsoon), which the specific rubric requires for the mark.
4.  **Literal Translation Bug:** Translating the season name from a local dialect that may not have a 1:1 mapping with the four English options provided in the rubric.

Step 3: Population Considerations
The open-ended format is a "Recall" task, which is significantly harder than "Recognition" (Multiple Choice). For Grade 6 students from EWS backgrounds in India, English is often a "Tertiary" environmentâ€”limited exposure outside the classroom. While "Winter" and "Summer" are high-frequency words, "Monsoon" and "Spring" are less frequent in active usage. However, because the rubric awards 1 mark simply for *naming* the season, the barrier to success is lower than the barrier to writing the full 6-8 line paragraph.

Step 4: Holistic Analysis
While many students will struggle with the full paragraph (grammar, syntax), a significant majority will be able to produce at least the word "Summer" or "Winter" or "Rainy" (accepted as Monsoon) due to early primary exposure. The "bugs" primarily involve spelling or failing to name the season explicitly.
