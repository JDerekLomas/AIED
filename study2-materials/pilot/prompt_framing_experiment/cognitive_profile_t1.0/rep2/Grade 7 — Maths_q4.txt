**Cognitive Difficulty Profile Analysis**

*   **Concept Familiarity:** **Familiar.** Division involving decimals is introduced in Grade 6 and reinforced in Grade 7 (NCERT Chapter 2: Fractions and Decimals). However, dividing a whole number by a decimal (as opposed to a decimal by a whole number) is less intuitive for students at this level.
*   **Computational Complexity:** **2-3 steps.** Students must first eliminate the decimal (usually by multiplying both terms by 10 to get 80 รท 72), simplify the resulting fraction or perform long division, and manage the recurring decimal output.
*   **Common Error Traps:** **Major.** Students from these backgrounds often struggle with the "reciprocal" nature of decimal division. Common errors include:
    *   Misplacing the decimal point (e.g., getting 0.11 or 11.1).
    *   Inverting the operation (calculating 7.2 รท 8 = 0.9).
    *   Stopping the division prematurely because 72 does not divide 80 evenly.
*   **Format Demand:** **Open-ended.** Since there are no options to choose from, students cannot use estimation or process of elimination. They must correctly recall the procedure for handling a decimal divisor.
*   **Number Friendliness:** **Hard.** The result is $1.11...$ (a recurring decimal). For students accustomed to "clean" answers in school textbooks, a non-terminating result often causes anxiety and leads them to believe they have made a mistake.

**Holistic Estimate:**
Given the language barrier and the shift from "rote" whole-number division to decimal division, many students will attempt to simplify $8/7.2$ to $80/72$, but the subsequent long division of $80 \div 72$ or simplification to $10/9$ will result in high attrition.
