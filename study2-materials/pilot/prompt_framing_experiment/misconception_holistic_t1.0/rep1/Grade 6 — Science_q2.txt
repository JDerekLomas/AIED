*   **Mnemonic association:** The "Kitchen of the Plant" is a very common analogy used in Hindi-medium and English-medium textbooks alike (often translated as "पौधों का रसोईघर").

Step 4: Factors making it harder.
*   **Language Barrier:** The word "Photosynthesis" is phonetically difficult for students with limited English proficiency. Spelling errors are common.
*   **Open-ended format:** Without options (MCQ), students must recall the specific term from memory rather than recognizing it.
*   **Dual-task:** The question requires two distinct pieces of information for only 0.5 marks, increasing the chance of a partial (and thus "incorrect" per the rubric) response.

Step 5: Estimate.
Despite the language challenges, this is one of the most rehearsed facts in the Grade 6 Indian Science curriculum. Even students from economically weaker sections are typically drilled on this specific terminology in both Hindi and English. However, the requirement to produce the spelling "Photosynthesis" in an open-ended format will filter out a significant portion of students who understand the concept but struggle with the English terminology.
