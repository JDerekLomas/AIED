Step 2: List common misconceptions.
*   **Addition vs. Multiplication Rules:** Students often confuse the rule for adding integers (where -3 + -4 = -7) with the rule for multiplication. This would lead to an incorrect answer of -7 or -12.
*   **Sign Dominance:** A common misconception is that if there are negative signs in the problem, the answer must remain negative. This would lead to the incorrect answer of -12.
*   **Absolute Value Focus:** Some students ignore signs entirely, focusing only on the numbers. While this leads to the correct magnitude (12), it is often a result of luck rather than understanding if the student doesn't consciously apply the sign rule.
