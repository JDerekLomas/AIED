### Step 2: Common Misconceptions
*   **Whole Number Multiplication:** Multiplying the whole numbers and the fractions separately ($1 \times 3$ and $\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{4}$) to get $3 \frac{1}{8}$. **Impact:** Incorrect answer.
*   **Additive Conversion Error:** Mistakenly adding the denominator to the whole number instead of multiplying ($1 \frac{1}{2} \to \frac{1+2}{2} = \frac{3}{2}$ works by fluke, but $3 \frac{1}{4} \to \frac{3+4}{4} = \frac{7}{4}$ fails). **Impact:** Incorrect answer.
*   **Common Denominator Confusion:** Attempting to find a common denominator (as in addition) before multiplying. **Impact:** Increases complexity/error rate, though mathematically possible.
*   **Cross-Multiplication:** Confusing multiplication with fraction comparison or division and cross-multiplying. **Impact:** Incorrect answer.
