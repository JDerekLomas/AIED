- Language barrier: "Wasp sting" and "Vinegar" may be unfamiliar terms to Hindi-medium students if the test is in English.
- Spelling/Transcription: Students might struggle to recall the specific word "Neutralization."
- Open-ended format: No options to trigger recognition memory.
- Scientific confusion: Correctly identifying that a wasp sting is basic and a bee sting is acidic requires specific memorization.

Step 5: While the concept is a core part of the syllabus, the combination of the open-ended format and the language barrier for students from economically weaker backgrounds (limited exposure to English terminology like "vinegar" or "neutralization") will pose a significant challenge.
