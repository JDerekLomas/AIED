Students must recognize that wasp stings (alkaline) and bee stings (acidic) require the application of their chemical opposites to reach a neutral state.

Step 2: Common misconceptions include:
- Thinking all insect stings are the same (acidic), leading to using the same treatment for both. (Leads to incorrect answer)
- Believing that a stronger substance of the same type "washes away" the first (e.g., using acid to clean acid). (Leads to incorrect answer)
- Confusing "neutralization" with "dilution." (Might lead to partial credit or incorrect explanation)

- **Open-ended:** Without options (MCQ), students must recall the specific term from memory rather than recognizing it.
- **Complexity:** Distinguishing which is acidic (bee) and which is basic (wasp) requires specific memorization before applying the principle.
