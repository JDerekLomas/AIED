Step 1: Identify the mathematical concepts and skills required.
While this is a Social Science (Geography) item, the cognitive skills involved include categorization and identifying causal variables. In a mathematical sense, this involves understanding independent variables (factors) that determine a dependent variable (soil formation).

Step 2: List the most common misconceptions students at this grade level hold about these concepts.
- Confusion between "soil" and "land": Many students view soil as static dirt rather than a biological/geological process. (Could lead to incorrect answers like "construction" or "roads").
- Confusion between factors (causes) and degradation (results): Students often list "deforestation" or "pollution" (which destroy soil) instead of factors that *form* it. (Leads to an incorrect answer).
- Translation error: Students from Hindi backgrounds might know the concept ("Chattan" for rock, "Samay" for time) but struggle to recall the specific English term "Parent Rock" or "Topography."

Step 3: Consider factors that make the item easier:
- Familiarity: Soil formation is a core topic in Grade 8 NCERT geography.
- Low Threshold: The student only needs to recall *one* factor out of five possibilities.
- Simple Vocabulary: "Time" (Samay) is a one-word, universally understood factor that satisfies the rubric.

Step 4: Consider factors that make it harder:
- Open-ended format: No options (like a multiple-choice) to trigger memory.
- Language Barrier: Words like "Relief," "Topography," or "Organisms" are high-level academic English and may be difficult for ESL learners to spell or recall.
- Abstract vs. Concrete: Understanding "Time" as a geological factor is more abstract than "Rain" or "Rocks."

Step 5: Weighing ALL factors holistically:
The availability of "Time" and "Parent Rock" (common terms) as correct answers makes this accessible. In Indian government schools, rote memorization of list-based Geography facts is common.
