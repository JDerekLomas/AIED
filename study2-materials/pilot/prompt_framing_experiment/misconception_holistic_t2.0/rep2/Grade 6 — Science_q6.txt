- Low threshold: The rubric only requires *one* correct adaptation, not an exhaustive list.

- Language barrier: "Adaptation" is a sophisticated term. Students may know the concept in Hindi (अनुकूलन - Anukulan) but struggle to explain "specially built or behaves" in English.
- Open-ended format: No cues or options to trigger memory; requires active recall and sentence construction.
- Conceptual nuance: Distinguishing between "saving" vs "getting" water requires categorical thinking.

Step 5: Given the English language barrier in many government schools, the jump from "understanding the concept" to "explaining it in an English-medium assessment" is significant. However, because only one simple feature (like "long roots") is needed for a full mark, a reasonable portion of students will succeed.
