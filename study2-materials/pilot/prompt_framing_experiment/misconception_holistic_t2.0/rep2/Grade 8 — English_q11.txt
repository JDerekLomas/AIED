Step 2: List common misconceptions.
*   **Organizational Error:** Students may struggle to differentiate between descriptive attributes (appearance) and functional/symbolic attributes (importance). (Relevant to item structure).
*   **Constraint Neglection:** Students often ignore the "5-6 sentence" limit, writing too little or a rambling page. (Relevant to the rubric).
*   **Translation Mapping:** For Hindi-medium students, literal translation from a native dialect to English often results in syntactical errors that obscure the intended meaning. (Highly relevant).

*   **Flexibility:** The "history (if known)" clause lowers the bar for factual accuracy, allowing for imaginative or personal accounts.

Step 4: Factors making it harder:
*   **Language Barrier:** Writing a coherent descriptive paragraph in a second language is significantly harder than selecting a multiple-choice option.
*   **English Proficiency:** Students with limited English struggle with the vocabulary needed to describe "appearance" (e.g., gnarly, expansive, trunk, ancient) beyond very basic terms.
*   **Scoring Rigidity:** If the maximum score is 1, any significant failure to cover one of the three required pillars (appearance, history, or community) or poor grammar could lead to a zero.
*   **Abstract Prompting:** While the tree is concrete, describing "what it means to the community" requires abstract expressive language.
