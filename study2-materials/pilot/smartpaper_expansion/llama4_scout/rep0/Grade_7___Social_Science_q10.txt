## Step 1: Understanding the Question and Rubric
The question asks students to explain the significance of Ashoka's reign in Indian history, mentioning at least two key aspects of his rule. The rubric focuses on the accurate explanation of the overall significance/impact of Ashoka's reign, such as transformation of kingship, ethical governance, and religious impact.

## 2: Identifying Prerequisite Knowledge
Students need to have basic knowledge about Ashoka, his reign, and its impacts. This includes understanding Ashoka's conversion to Buddhism, his efforts in propagating Buddhism, the changes in his administrative policies post-Kalinga war, and his establishment of Dhamma (Ashoka's moral code).

## 3: Assessing Question Clarity and Student Preparedness
The question is clear about what is expected - the significance of Ashoka's reign and at least two key aspects of his rule. However, the clarity of the question and the emphasis on "significance" in Indian history might lead to varied interpretations. Students at Grade 7 level in India are expected to have a foundational understanding of Indian history, including major rulers and their contributions.
