## Step 1: Understanding the Question
The question asks students to identify the adjective phrase in the sentence "A tiny bird built its nest in our balcony." The rubric specifies that the student correctly identifies "tiny" as an adjective for full marks.

Students might lose marks if they:
- Misidentify the part of speech of "tiny" (e.g., thinking it's an adverb or a noun).
- Fail to recognize "tiny" as an adjective modifying the noun "bird".
- Confuse the term "adjective phrase" with a single adjective; in this case, "tiny" is a single adjective, not an adjective phrase. An adjective phrase would be a group of words functioning as an adjective.

## 3: Clarity of the Question and Expectations
The question seems straightforward but might cause confusion because it specifically asks for an "adjective phrase," which could lead students to look for a phrase rather than a single adjective.
