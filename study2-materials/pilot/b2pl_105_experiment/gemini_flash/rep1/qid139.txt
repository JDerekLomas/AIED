In my experience teaching expansion of brackets, this specific question is a "classic" trap because it targets the intersection of three potential errors: the operation (multiplying vs. adding), the sign rule (negative times negative), and the basic multiplication fact.

The fact that students are specifically asked for the "multiplied" result of -4 and -3 often triggers an additive response in lower-ability students, as they see the two negative signs and think "minus 4 minus 3". "Advanced" students almost never miss the double negative in this isolated context.

The most common wrong answer across the board is B (-12), because many students correctly identify 12 but feel that "negative things should result in a negative." A and D are chosen by those who add rather than multiply.
