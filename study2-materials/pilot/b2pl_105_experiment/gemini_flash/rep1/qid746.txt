This question tests the Order of Operations (BIDMAS/BODMAS). While the structure appears simple, it contains a classic "left-to-right" trap that many students fall into when they ignore the hierarchy of operations.

**Analysis of the Distractors:**
*   **B (7) is correct:** $4 + 12 \div 4 \rightarrow 4 + 3 = 7$.
*   **A (10):** Students calculate the numerator/top line as a sequence without hierarchy but handle the brackets: $(4 + 12) \div (2 + 2) = 16 \div 4 = 4$ (No, wait, A is $10$â€”this is likely $(4 + 12 \div 2) + 2 \rightarrow (4 + 6) + 2 = 12$ or someone doing $4 + 6$ then stopping/guessing). Actually, **A** is the most common error for students who ignore the bracket and go left to right: $4 + 12 = 16$, then $16 \div 2 = 8$, then $8 + 2 = 10$.
*   **C (12):** A common calculation error or an attempt to do $4 + (12 \div 2) + 2$, ignoring the grouping.
*   **D (4):** Adding the brackets first ($4$), then adding the $4+12$ to get $16$, then $16 \div 4 = 4$. This is a very common mistake where students treat the plus sign at the start and the bracket at the end as equal priority or simply do "addition, then addition, then division".

**Student Behavior:**
