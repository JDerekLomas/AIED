**Analysis of Likely Errors:**

1.  **"Describes" Misinterpretation:** This is a major hurdle. Students might:
    *   **Misunderstand "describes" as "to write something."** They see a verb and think they just need to write *anything* about the poet or the poem. This leads to answers like "I am reading" (as seen in the example, indicating they are just processing or reacting to the text, not analyzing style) or even just copying lines from the poem.
    *   **Not understand "describe" in the context of *style*.** They might understand "describe" as giving physical characteristics of the poet or the poem's subject, rather than the manner of writing.

2.  **"Poet's Writing Style" Abstract Concept:**
    *   **Difficulty with "style":** "Style" is an abstract concept for Class 7 students, especially those learning English as a second or third language. They might struggle to grasp that it refers to *how* something is written (word choice, sentence structure, tone) rather than *what* is written (the plot/theme).
    *   **Lack of vocabulary for literary analysis:** Even if they understand "style" somewhat, they likely lack the English vocabulary to describe it effectively (e.g., descriptive, informal, simple, evocative, vivid, metaphorical, direct, repetitive, rhythmic). Their productive vocabulary is limited.
    *   **Focus on content over form:** Most primary/junior secondary education focuses on understanding the *story* or *message* of a text, not its structural or stylistic elements. This question requires a higher-order thinking skill they may not have been explicitly taught in English.

3.  **Syntactic/Grammatical Issues (if they attempt a relevant answer):**
    *   Even if a student grasps the concept, they might struggle to formulate a grammatically correct sentence or even a single descriptive word. "Simple" might become "The simple writing" without a verb.

4.  **No Attempt / Empty Response:** Given the difficulty and abstract nature of the question, a significant portion of students will likely not even attempt an answer. They may feel overwhelmed or simply not understand what is being asked, leading to "Empty Response."

*   **"Empty Response":** Likely the most frequent.
*   **Irrelevant statement about the poem/poet:**
    *   "The poem is good."
    *   "The poet wrote the poem."
    *   "I like this poet."
    *   "It is about..." (describing the theme/content, not style)
    *   "The poet used simple words." (This *could* be correct if framed properly, but it would often be vague or disconnected from true analysis.)
*   **Literal interpretation/reaction:**
    *   "I am reading." (As seen in the example, interpreting "describes" as an action *they* are doing or *are expected to do* in relation to the poet/text.)
    *   "He is a poet."
*   **Single, uninformative word:**
    *   "Good"
    *   "Beautiful"
    *   "Simple" (This has potential, but often might be just a guess without understanding style)
