Let's break down the potential errors for this specific rearrangement question, considering the typical challenges faced by these students.

**Likely Errors and Misconceptions:**

*   **Understanding "Rearrange" itself:**
    *   Some students might not fully grasp the instruction "Rearrange the words to make a meaningful sentence." They might simply copy the words as they appear, or list them out without changing the order significantly. This would be a failure to attempt a rearrangement, not just an incorrect one.
    *   *Common wrong answer type:* Listing the words given: "book reading a is he" or "he is reading a book." (if they get it by chance without understanding).

*   **Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) Structure Confusion:**
    *   While simple SVO is fundamental, it might not be intuitive for students with limited exposure to formal English sentence structure, especially if their native languages have different typical word orders.
    *   They might place the verb before the subject or object.
    *   They might struggle to identify "he" as the subject.
    *   *Common wrong answer type:* "Reading he is a book." or "Is he reading a book." (trying to make a question, confusing statements with questions). "Book he is reading a." (object placed early).

*   **Inability to identify the "doer" (subject):**
    *   "He" is a pronoun. For students just learning, identifying "he" as the subject and understanding its role might be a hurdle. They might see "book" as the primary noun and try to build around that.

*   **Confusing word types (article vs. verb vs. noun):**
    *   While "is," "a," "book," "reading," and "he" are common words, in the context of rearranging, if a student is not clear on what role each word plays (e.g., "is" is a verb, "a" is an article, "book" is a noun), they'll struggle to place them correctly.

*   **Influence of local language grammar:**
    *   Indian languages (like Hindi, Marathi, etc.) often follow Subject-Object-Verb (SOV) order. While English SVO is simple, the mental "rewiring" might still be a factor, leading to attempts like "He book a is reading."

*   **Not attempting at all / "Empty Response":**
    *   Given the "Empty Response" common error on other questions, a significant number of students might look at this and, feeling overwhelmed or unsure where to start, simply leave it blank. This is a very likely scenario given their struggles with even descriptive questions.

**What the Most Common Wrong Answers Would Look Like:**

1.  **Empty Response/No Attempt:** The most prevalent, as seen in other questions with low pass rates.
2.  **Simply copying the words (or slight variations):** "book/reading/a/is/he" or "He a book is reading." (without any understanding of sentence flow).
3.  **Subject/Verb/Object mixed up (often influenced by SOV structure):** "He book reading a is."
4.  **Making it a question:** "Is he reading a book?" (indicates partial understanding but misunderstanding of sentence type).
5.  **Putting the article/noun out of place:** "A book he is reading."
