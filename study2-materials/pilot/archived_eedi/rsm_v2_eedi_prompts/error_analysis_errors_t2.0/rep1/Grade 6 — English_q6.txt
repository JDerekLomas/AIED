Let's break down the potential errors for the question "Which line from the poem do you like the most and why?" given the context of Class 6 Indian government school students from economically weaker sections with limited English.

**Analysis of Potential Errors:**

1.  **Difficulty understanding "Which line...do you like the most?"**:
    *   **Misconception:** Students might struggle with the comparative "most" and the abstract concept of "liking" a line in a poem. They are likely more accustomed to concrete factual recall.
    *   **Language Misunderstanding:** The phrasing "do you like" requires comprehension of personal opinion, which might be new or difficult for some. They might misinterpret "like" as "identify" or "remember."
    *   **Attempt/Failure/No Attempt:** Some might attempt to simply copy a line without expressing preference. Others might pick a line and just give a one-word answer for "why," or not attempt the "why" part at all.

2.  **Difficulty understanding "and why?"**:
    *   **Misconception:** This requires analytical or interpretive thinking, connecting the chosen line to a personal reason or feeling. This is a higher-order skill that these students generally struggle with, as evidenced by the very low pass rates on questions requiring explanation or description.
    *   **Language Misunderstanding:** "Why" necessitates forming a cohesive sentence or two explaining a reason, which is challenging with limited vocabulary and grammatical structures.
    *   **Attempt/Failure/No Attempt:** Many will likely choose a line but omit the "why" entirely. Some might give very short, tangential, or illogical "why" answers.

3.  **Remembering and correctly reproducing a "line from the poem"**:
    *   **Misconception:** Even if they understood the question, accurately recalling a specific line from a poem they've read (which itself might have been a challenge to understand) could be an issue. They might misquote or just summarize.
    *   **Language Misunderstanding:** English spelling and transcription will be a hurdle.
    *   **Attempt/Failure/No Attempt:** Many will try, but accuracy in transcription might be poor. Some might just recall a word or phrase, not a full "line."

4.  **Connecting the "line" with the "why" in a coherent manner**:
    *   **Misconception:** The rubric specifically requires *denoting a line* and *mentioning why*. This implies a connection. Students often provide fragmented responses or disconnect the two parts.
    *   **Language Misunderstanding:** Structuring a sentence like "I like [line] because [reason]" is a complex grammatical task.
    *   **Attempt/Failure/No Attempt:** This is where most attempts will fail, even if they choose a line and attempt a reason.

**Common Wrong Answers (Anticipated):**

*   **Simply stating a line from the poem without any "why."** (This would get 0 marks)
    *   Example: "The Last Bargain they about a child and labour." (copying from previous incorrect answers suggests this type of disconnected phrasing)
    *   Example: "I am working for you." (a generic line, potentially misremembered)
*   **Stating a line and a very weak, irrelevant, or one-word "why."** (e.g., "good," "nice")
    *   Example: "I like this line: 'I am a worker.' Why: good."
    *   Example: "My favorite line is... because it is true."
*   **Listing lines from the poem, perhaps indicating preference for one without elaboration.**
    *   Example: "Line 1, Line 5, Line 8." (perhaps intending to choose Line 8 as favorite)
*   **Completely off-topic or misunderstanding "line."**
    *   Example: "I like this poem." (without a specific line)
    *   Example: "Poet is good."
*   **Empty Response:** This is a significant factor in other questions, and will likely be common here too.
*   **Trying to rephrase the question instead of answering it:**
    *   Example: "Which line from poem you like?"
