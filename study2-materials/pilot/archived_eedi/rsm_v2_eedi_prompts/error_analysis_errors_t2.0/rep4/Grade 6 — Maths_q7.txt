Let's break down the likely errors for Class 6 students in Indian government schools tackling this problem.

**Analysis of Likely Errors:**

1.  **Calculating Time Elapsed (Crucial for the given rubric):**

    *   **Misconceptions about Mathematics (Time):**
        *   **Counting on Fingers/Mental Arithmetic Errors:** The jump from 10 am to 4 pm, especially crossing the "noon" barrier, can be tricky.
            *   Students might count 10, 11, 12, 1, 2, 3, 4 and get 7 hours.
            *   They might do simple subtraction (4 - 10) or 10 - 4 which yields negative numbers or incorrect positive numbers without understanding the 12-hour clock cycle.
            *   They might count in groups of two: (10-12 = 2 hours, 12-2 = 2 hours, 2-4 = 2 hours), getting it correct but it's a multi-step mental process.
        *   **Lack of Number Line/Visual Aid Concept:** Without a clear mental "number line" for hours, some struggle to visualize the duration.
        *   **Confusion with 24-hour clock vs. 12-hour clock (less likely at Class 6, but possible influence):** Some might have some rudimentary exposure and mix it up.
    *   **Language Misunderstandings (related to time):**
        *   "10 am in the morning" and "4:00 in the evening" are generally understood, but for some, the specific phrasing might not immediately translate to "how many hours later?". The core understanding of AM/PM for relative time should be okay.
    *   **Attempt/Fail or Not Attempt:** Most students would *attempt* to calculate the time, as it's the first step. Failing at this is highly probable for a significant portion. Some might get stuck, make a guess, or just count numbers between 10 and 4.

2.  **Applying the "increases by 2°C per hour" rule:**

    *   **Misconceptions about Mathematics (Multiplication/Repeated Addition):**
        *   If they get the hours wrong (e.g., 7 hours), they'll then multiply or add incorrectly (e.g., 7 * 2 = 14 or 30 + 14).
        *   Some might only add '2' once, without multiplying by the number of hours (e.g., 30 + 2 = 32), showing a basic lack of understanding of "per hour."
    *   **Language Misunderstandings:**
        *   "Per hour" (प्रति घंटा) should be generally understood if taught.
    *   **Attempt/Fail:** This part will almost always be attempted if they successfully determine a number of hours.

3.  **Calculating Final Temperature:**

    *   **Misconceptions about Mathematics (Addition):** Simple addition (Initial Temp + Increase). If previous steps are correct, this is less likely to be an error point.
    *   **Language Misunderstandings:** None really, it's a straightforward numerical result.
    *   **Attempt/Fail:** Most would add, but could make simple arithmetic errors.

**Most Common Wrong Answer Patterns & Errors (specifically for the *rubric*, which focuses on time elapsed):**
