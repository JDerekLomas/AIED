    *   **"Table":** A very common, visually similar word. Students might misread it as "table" and try to define "table" (which would be marked wrong, of course). This is a plausible phonetic and visual misreading error given basic literacy levels.
    *   **"Able":** Again, similar sound. Could be confused.
    *   **"Story" without specifics:** If they do recognize it as a type of story, they might just say "a story" which isn't specific enough for a full mark according to the rubric (needs "animals" and "moral lesson").

5.  **Direct Translation issues:** If they try to translate a concept from their local language, they might use words that don't precisely convey the meaning of "fable."
6.  **No attempt:** This is very likely due to not knowing the word at all or lacking confidence in their English writing ability.
7.  **Generic/incorrect definitions:** Students might try to define "story" or "poem" or just provide a very vague, unhelpful definition like "something you read."
