ERRORS Analysis:

*   **Social Science Misconceptions:**
    *   **Specificity vs. General Knowledge:** Students might name occupations common in any rural area (e.g., shopkeeping, labor) without focusing on Rajasthan's specific context. While these *exist* in rural Rajasthan, they might not be considered "main occupations" in the same vein as agriculture or animal husbandry, which are historically dominant and tied to the region's geography.
    *   **Lack of regional knowledge:** Students from other parts of India, even within government schools, might not have specific knowledge of Rajasthan's primary occupations. Their curriculum might be more general or focused on their own state.
    *   **Focus on isolated jobs:** Instead of "main occupations" (which imply widespread and primary economic activities), students might list very niche or individual jobs.
    *   **Confusing urban with rural:** Though less likely given the question's phrasing, some might bring in urban occupations.

*   **Language Misunderstandings:**
    *   **"Main occupations":** Some students might struggle to interpret "main" as "primary" or "most common." They might just list *any* occupation they can think of.
    *   **"Rural areas":** While this is a relatively common term, students from highly urbanized areas or with very limited exposure to English might have a fuzzy understanding.
    *   **"Describe any one in brief":** This part requires a short descriptive sentence or two. Some might only list occupations without attempting a description, or provide a description that is too generic or incorrect. The prompt implies naming two AND describing one. Given the max score of 1, naming at least two is the primary threshold for a pass, and description likely secondary for a "good" pass but not strictly required for any pass score if the name itself isn't wrong.
    *   **English Proficiency:** The core problem is that many students struggle with basic sentence construction and vocabulary, making it hard to express even known information. They might know the Hindi terms but struggle to translate them accurately into English.

*   **Attempt vs. Failure:**
    *   **High Attempt Rate:** This is a factual, recall-based question. Most students would attempt it because it doesn't require complex analysis. They will likely list *something*.
    *   **Failure Modes:**
        *   **Incorrect Listing:** Naming occupations not prevalent or incorrect for Rajasthan's rural areas (e.g., IT services, heavy industry).
        *   **Insufficient Listing:** Naming only one occupation.
        *   **Too General:** Naming something like "labour" or "work" without specific categories.
        *   **Language Barrier in Description:** Even if they name correctly, they might fail to "describe" due to English limitations. However, based on the rubric ("Names at least two main occupations correctly"), a brief description might not be absolutely necessary for a score of 1 if two are named correctly.

*   **Common Wrong Answers:**
    *   "They do labor."
    *   "Farming." (Only one occupation named)
    *   "Shopkeeper, selling things." (Potentially true, but maybe not considered *main* by the rubric)
    *   "Construction work, daily wage."
    *   Answers using the Hindi term or very broken English (e.g., "Kheti, pashu palan"). This might get a score if the intention is clear, similar to the partial English answers in other examples.

    *   **"Theme of 'The Last Bargain'" (5% pass):** High-level interpretation, difficult English expression.
