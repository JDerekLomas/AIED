**Analysis of "Define Fable":**

1.  **Complexity:** This question asks for a definition. Unlike simply recalling facts (like fundamental rights) or identifying specifics (like the king's questions), it requires a conceptual understanding and the ability to articulate it in a concise, accurate sentence.
2.  **Vocabulary:** "Fable" itself is a specific literary term. Students might know popular fables but might not have the explicit academic definition.
3.  **Key Elements of Definition:**
    *   Short story (easy enough)
    *   Uses animals as characters (this is a crucial and often defining characteristic)
    *   Imparts a moral lesson (another crucial aspect)

**Likely Errors and Misconceptions:**

*   **Vocabulary Deficit:** Students might not know the word "fable" at all, leading to no attempt or completely irrelevant answers.
*   **Partial Understanding:** They might know *a part* of the definition (e.g., "story with animals") but miss the "moral lesson" component, or vice versa. The rubric requires both key aspects for a full score.
*   **Confusion with Similar Terms:** Could confuse it with "story," "poem," "legend," or "myth" if their English vocabulary for literary genres is limited.
*   **Inability to Articulate:** Even if they *understand* what a fable is, expressing it in clear, grammatical English in a definition format can be challenging. Many might attempt a long-winded description or provide an example instead of a definition.
*   **Common Wrong Answers:**
    *   "A story about animals." (Missing moral lesson)
    *   "It is a story that teaches a lesson." (Missing animal characters)
    *   "Panchatantra is a fable." (Giving an example instead of a definition)
    *   Empty response due to not knowing the word.
    *   Random, irrelevant words.
