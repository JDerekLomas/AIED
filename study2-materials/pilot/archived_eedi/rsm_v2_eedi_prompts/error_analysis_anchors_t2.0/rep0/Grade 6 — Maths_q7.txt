Errors students might make:

*   **Misinterpreting "4:00 in the evening"**: This is the most significant potential language hurdle. While 4 PM is common in daily speech, students might confuse AM/PM, or calculate for 4 AM.
*   **Counting hours incorrectly**: Students might:
    *   Subtract 10 from 4 directly, leading to a negative or incorrect number.
    *   Count on their fingers and make an arithmetic error.
    *   Stop at 12 PM or continue counting past midnight if confused by PM.
    *   Start counting *from* 10 AM (i.e., 10 AM to 11 AM is 1 hour, etc.) and miscount the number of *intervals*. (e.g., 10, 11, 12, 1, 2, 3, 4 -> 7 numbers, so they might think 7 hours instead of 6).
    *   Exclude the starting or ending hour.
*   **Mathematical Operations**:
    *   They might correctly find the time elapsed (6 hours) but then incorrectly multiply by 2 (e.g., add 2, subtract 2, or miscalculate 6x2).
    *   They might incorrectly add the increase to the initial temperature. (e.g., 30 + 2 = 32 and forget the hours entirely).
*   **Not attempting or incomplete**: Due to the multi-step nature of the problem, some students might attempt the first step (finding the hours) but not proceed, or simply not understand how to begin.
*   **Misreading 2^0 C as 20 C**: Unlikely for a clear superscript but possible for a small minority if font is unclear.

**Common Wrong Answers:**

*   **Confusion with AM/PM leading to a very different time:** e.g., calculating for 4 AM next morning, or just 4 hours after 10 AM (10, 11, 12, 1, 2 -> 14:00, or 2 PM. Then adding 4 hours for some reason leading to a higher number.)
*   **Incorrect hour count:**
    *   Thinking 5 hours (10 to 3 pm or similar miscount)
    *   Thinking 7 hours (10,11,12,1,2,3,4 - simple count of numbers instead of intervals)
*   **Only calculating temperature change or not adding to initial:**
    *   Reporting `12^0 C` (6 hours * 2 degrees/hour) instead of `42^0 C`.
    *   Reporting `32^0 C` (just 30 + 2, ignoring hours).

The rubric for this specific question is just to *calculate the time elapsed from 10:00 am to 4:00 pm as 6 hours*. This means that if they make the arithmetic error with 2^C or the final addition, but correctly find "6 hours," they *still* get the point. This significantly simplifies the problem and makes the target narrower.
