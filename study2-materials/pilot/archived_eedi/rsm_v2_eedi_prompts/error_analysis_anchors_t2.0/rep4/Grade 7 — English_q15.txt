Let's break down the likely student responses and errors for the 'My Favourite Hobby' paragraph.

**Analysis of Likely Errors and Student Behavior:**

1.  **Limited English Vocabulary and Sentence Structure:**
    *   **Common Struggle:** Students will likely have a small active vocabulary. Constructing coherent sentences beyond simple subject-verb-object might be challenging.
    *   **Impact:** They might resort to very short, choppy sentences or struggle to connect ideas smoothly, even if they have the right ideas in mind.
    *   **Example Failure:** "My hobby is play. I play everyday. It good." (Doesn't meet paragraph length or depth.)

2.  **Difficulty with "Paragraph" and Sentence Count:**
    *   **Common Struggle:** The concept of a "paragraph" (connected sentences discussing one idea) might not be fully grasped. The explicit "4-5 sentences" guideline is crucial but still prone to misinterpretation. Some might just list things.
    *   **Impact:** Some might write only 1-2 sentences, or a list of single words/phrases instead of sentences. Others might write long, run-on sentences without proper punctuation, or disconnected thoughts.
    *   **Example Failure:** "Playing. Evening. Fun." (Not a paragraph, not sentences).

3.  **Understanding "Favourite" and "Hobby":**
    *   **Misconception:** While "favourite" is common, "hobby" might be a slightly less frequent word for some. They might equate it with "play" or "what I do in free time." This is unlikely to cause a complete misunderstanding, but might limit the range of chosen activities to very common ones like playing, reading (if available), or watching TV.
    *   **Impact:** Unlikely to cause failure *on its own*, but could lead to simpler content.

4.  **Addressing All Prompts ("When," "Why Enjoy"):**
    *   **Common Struggle:** Remembering to include all three elements (what, when, why) can be difficult, especially under time pressure or with limited writing ability.
    *   **Impact:** Students might mention the hobby, but forget "when" or simply say "always" or "sometimes" without much elaboration. "Why I enjoy it" might be reduced to single words like "good" or "fun," not elaborated into a reason.
    *   **Example Failure:** "My hobby is cricket. I play everyday. It good to play." (Missing elaboration on "why good," potentially short on sentences).

5.  **Lack of Creativity/Confidence in Expressing Personal Feelings:**
    *   **Common Struggle:** Some students might struggle to articulate *why* they enjoy something beyond a simple positive descriptor. They might feel more comfortable stating facts than personal opinions.
    *   **Impact:** Leads to superficial answers for the "why enjoy it" part, often the weakest part of their response.

6.  **"Not Attempting At All" vs. "Attempt but Fail":**
    *   Compared to the poem analysis or historical differentiation, this question is highly relatable and direct. Most students will *attempt* it.
    *   **Risk:** The failure will primarily come from the quality of the attempt: insufficient length, incomplete addressing of prompts, or severe grammatical/structural issues that render the paragraph incomprehensible.

**Most Common Wrong Answer/Partial Attempt:**

*   **Format:** A few disconnected sentences or a very short, unelaborated statement.
*   **Content:** "My hobby is play. I play in the evening. It is fun." (Too short, doesn't meet 4-5 sentences, very minimal elaboration).
*   **Alternative:** "Playing games. I play everyday. Good." (Not full sentences, no paragraph structure).
