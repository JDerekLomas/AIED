Let's break down the question "Find the area of a triangle whose base is `8` cm and height is `6` cm."

**Prior Knowledge Assumed:**
*   **Basic Arithmetic:** Multiplication and division (especially by 2).
*   **Geometric Concepts:** Understanding of what "base" and "height" refer to in the context of a triangle.
*   **Formula Recall:** Knowing the formula for the area of a triangle ($A = \frac{1}{2} \times b \times h$). This is typically introduced in Class 6 or 7.

**Language Demands:**
*   **Key terms:** "Find the area," "triangle," "base," "height." These are fairly common geometric terms and are likely part of their mathematics vocabulary, even if their general English is limited. The numbers `8` and `6` are universally understood.
*   **Sentence structure:** A straightforward declarative sentence instructing a calculation. Not complex.

**Recall, Application, or Reasoning?**
This is primarily an **application** question, heavily relying on **recall** of the formula. Students need to recall the formula and then apply it to the given numbers. There's minimal higher-order reasoning involved beyond recognizing the shape and its dimensions.

**Difficulty Compared to Typical Textbook Exercises:**
This is a very standard, introductory type of problem for finding the area of a triangle. It's usually one of the first exercises given after the formula is taught. It doesn't involve:
*   Unit conversions.
*   Working backward (e.g., finding base or height given area).
*   Compound shapes.
*   Fractions/decimals for base/height (simple integers).
*   Complex diagrams.
