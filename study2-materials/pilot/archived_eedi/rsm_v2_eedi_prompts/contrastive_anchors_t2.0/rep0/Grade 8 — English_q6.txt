Here's a breakdown of the analysis for the question "Define fable":

**Contrastive Analysis:**

1.  **Prior Knowledge Assumed:**
    *   **Direct:** The student needs to have encountered the concept of a fable before. This is generally taught in primary school English/language arts curricula in India (even in Hindi medium, students often learn about "कहानी" or moral stories which align with fables). They might know Aesop's fables through local adaptations or translated versions.
    *   **Indirect:** Understanding of basic story elements (characters, moral).

2.  **Language Demands (for students with limited English):**
    *   **Keyword "Define":** This is a standard academic instruction. While a challenge for very limited English speakers, by Class 8, most would understand "define" to mean "explain what it is."
    *   **Required Vocabulary:**
        *   "Short story": Fairly common, may be understood even if not perfectly articulated.
        *   "Animals as characters": "Animals" is common, "characters" might be slightly harder but still likely familiar in the context of stories.
        *   "Imparts a moral lesson": "Moral lesson" is the hardest part. "Moral" can be confused with morale, or simply not known in English. "Lesson" is more common.
    *   **Sentence Structure:** Students need to form a coherent English sentence or two to express the definition. This is a challenge, as evidenced by some of the calibration anchors where responses are fragmented or ungrammatical.

3.  **Recall, Application, or Reasoning?**
    *   This is primarily **recall**. Students need to retrieve a memorized or understood definition. There's no application of the definition to a new text, nor any complex reasoning required.

4.  **Difficulty Compared to Typical Textbook Exercises:**
    *   Defining terms is a very common textbook exercise. However, the *specific term* "fable" might not be given the same prominence as, say, "noun" or "verb."
    *   Textbooks might explain fables by giving examples, or ask students to identify a fable, rather than asking for a direct definition using specific English terms.
    *   The demand for "animals as characters" and "imparts a moral lesson" in English makes it harder than simply identifying an example of a fable in a multiple-choice format or recalling it in Hindi.
