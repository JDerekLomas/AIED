## Contrastive Analysis of "Main occupations of people in rural Rajasthan"

Here's an analysis of the question's difficulty compared to the calibration anchors, focusing on factors relevant to Class 7 students in Indian government schools from economically weaker sections with limited English proficiency:

**Prior Knowledge Assumed:**

*   **Regional Geography/Social Studies:** Students need to have a basic understanding of Rajasthan's general socio-economic context, particularly its rural characteristics. This knowledge would typically be covered in Social Science textbooks for Class 6 or 7.
*   **Vocabulary:**
    *   "Occupations" is a common word, but understanding it in the context of "main occupations" requires linking it to common livelihoods.
    *   "Rural areas" should be a familiar concept.
    *   "Rajasthan" is a specific state in India, so geographic knowledge is necessary.

**Language Demands (for limited English proficiency):**

*   **Keywords are relatively straightforward:** "Main occupations," "rural areas," "Rajasthan," "describe any one in brief." These are not abstract or highly academic terms.
*   **Simple sentence structure required for answer:** Students are asked to *name* occupations and then *describe one in brief*. This doesn't demand complex sentence construction.
*   **Lack of advanced vocabulary:** No specific challenging terms are present in the question itself.

**Recall, Application, or Reasoning?**

*   **Mostly Recall:** This is primarily a recall question. Students need to remember what they've learned about the economy and livelihoods in specific Indian regions, particularly Rajasthan.
*   **Some mild Application/Brief Description:** The "describe any one in brief" part involves a very basic level of application, where they take one of the recalled occupations and briefly state what it entails (e.g., "farming means growing crops," "animal husbandry means raising animals for milk/wool"). This isn't deep reasoning.

**Comparison to Typical Textbook Exercises:**

*   This question is a **very typical Social Science textbook question**. It's direct, factual, and common for topics like "Geography of India" or "Livelihoods" in Class 6/7.
*   It's comparable to questions like "Name the rivers of Punjab" or "What is a desert?" where basic facts about a region are tested.
