**Contrastive Analysis for 'My Favourite Hobby' paragraph:**

**1. Prior Knowledge Assumed:**

*   **Conceptual understanding of "hobby":** This concept is generally well-understood even in vernacular languages (e.g., *shauk*, *pasandida kaam*). Most students, irrespective of background, have activities they enjoy in their free time.
*   **Basic paragraph structure:** Students should have been taught that a paragraph has a few connected sentences about a single topic. They might not consistently apply it perfectly, but the concept is introduced by Class 7.
*   **Simple past/present tense usage:** The prompt naturally leads to using present tense (my hobby *is*, I *do* it, I *enjoy* it). These are basic tenses usually covered by Class 7.

**2. Language Demands:**

*   **Understanding the prompt:**
    *   "Write a short paragraph (4-5 sentences)": "Paragraph" might be a challenge if not directly reinforced in English class. "4-5 sentences" provides a clear, manageable length.
    *   "'My Favourite Hobby'": "Favourite" is a common word. "Hobby" is key, as discussed.
    *   "Mention what it is, when you do it, and why you enjoy it": This is the most crucial part for clarity. "Mention" is quite accessible. "What it is" is simple. "When you do it" is straightforward. "Why you enjoy it" also uses common vocabulary.
*   **Production in English:**
    *   **Vocabulary:** Students need basic vocabulary to describe their chosen hobby (e.g., "cricket," "reading books," "drawing pictures," "music"). They also need simple adjectives/adverbs to express enjoyment ("relaxing," "fun," "learn new things").
    *   **Sentence construction:** Requires the ability to form simple subject-verb-object sentences and connect them coherently. E.g., "My favourite hobby is playing cricket." "I play it everyday in the evening." "I enjoy it because it is fun." This is within the grasp of Class 7, even with limited English, as it uses very basic sentence structures.
    *   **Grammar:** Basic agreement (I play, not I plays), simple prepositions (in the evening, with my friends). Expect errors but should be largely comprehensible.
    *   **Spelling:** Likely to have phonetic spellings or common errors, but the rubric focuses on content, not perfect spelling/grammar.

**3. Is this recall, application, or reasoning?**

*   **Application/Personal Response:** This is primarily an application question. It asks students to apply their basic English writing skills to a personal topic. It draws on their personal experience rather than recalled facts from a textbook.
*   **Some Reasoning (implicit):** Choosing a "why" requires a simple form of reasoning or justification, but this is usually quite rudimentary at this level ("it is fun," "I learn things").

    *   **STRAIGHTFORWARD INSTRUCTIONS:** The instructions "mention what it is, when you do it, and why you enjoy it" explicitly guide the student, making it easier to meet the rubric requirements even if their overall English is weak.
