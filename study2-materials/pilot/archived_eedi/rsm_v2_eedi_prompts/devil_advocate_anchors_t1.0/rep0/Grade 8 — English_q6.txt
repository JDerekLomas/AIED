**Analysis of the Question: "Define fable."**

*   **Complexity of Concept:** "Fable" is a literary term. While common in English language arts, its precise definition might not be readily known or articulated by students with limited English exposure, especially those who struggle with abstract concepts or specific vocabulary.
*   **English Proficiency:** The question requires defining a term. Students need to understand the word "define" and then accurately express the core components of a fable (short story, animals, moral lesson) in English. This is more demanding than simply listing facts or identifying elements.
*   **Recall vs. Application:** This is a pure recall question. Students either know the definition or they don't. There's little room for inference or partial understanding to earn points, unlike questions where a partially correct answer might get some credit.
*   **Likely Errors:**
    *   Blank responses due to not knowing the term or being unable to articulate it in English.
    *   Partial definitions (e.g., "story with animals" but missing the moral).
    *   Incorrect definitions (confusing it with myth, legend, or just "a story").
    *   Poorly worded or grammatically incorrect responses that might miss key elements or be unintelligible.
