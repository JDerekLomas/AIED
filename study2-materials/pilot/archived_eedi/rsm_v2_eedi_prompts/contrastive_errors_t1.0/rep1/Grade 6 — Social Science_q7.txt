Let's break down the question and its potential impact on Class 6 students in Indian government schools.

**Contrastive Analysis:**

1.  **Prior Knowledge Assumed:**
    *   **Geographical Awareness:** Students need to know that Rajasthan is a state in India and have some basic understanding of its geography and resources. This is generally covered in Class 6 Social Science textbooks, but the depth of recall can vary.
    *   **Definition of "Natural Resources":** They need to understand what constitutes a natural resource. This term is fundamental to geography/environmental studies at this level.
    *   **Causal Relationship:** They need to be able to connect a resource to its influence on people's lives (e.g., water scarcity leading to specific agricultural practices or lifestyle adjustments).

2.  **Language Demands (Limited English Proficiency):**
    *   **"Mention any two natural resources":** This is straightforward. "Mention" is a common verb, "natural resources" is a key term.
    *   **"found in Rajasthan":** Again, clear.
    *   **"explain how one of them influences the lives of the people there":** This is where the language complexity increases.
        *   "influences": This verb might be slightly more challenging than "affect" but is generally understandable in context.
        *   "lives of the people there": Clear.
        *   The need to *explain* a causal link, rather than just list, requires more nuanced English expression.
    *   **Common Errors related to English:** The previous examples show "Empty Response" which could indicate a lack of understanding of the question or an inability to formulate an answer in English. "Ashok seamb" and "Asher' stand" indicate phonetic attempts or misinterpretations, which are less likely here as the question asks for factual recall and simple explanation. However, struggling to articulate the *explanation* part in English is a significant hurdle.

3.  **Recall, Application, or Reasoning?**
    *   **Recall:** Listing two natural resources in Rajasthan is purely recall. This is likely the easiest part.
    *   **Reasoning/Application:** Explaining the *influence* of one resource requires basic reasoning or application of known facts. For example, if they mention "water," they might reason that its scarcity means people conserve it, or that rain-fed agriculture is common. If they mention "sand," they might link it to tourism (desert safaris) or specific housing styles.

4.  **Difficulty Compared to Typical Textbook Exercises:**
    *   **Typical exercises:** Often ask to list resources of a region or state, or to define terms.
    *   **This question:** Adds the "explain how one of them influences..." which elevates it slightly from pure recall to a basic analytical task. This part is harder than a simple listing question but less complex than explaining a historical event's significance (like Ashoka's reign).
    *   **Rubric:** The rubric is quite generous for "natural resources" (e.g., sand, sunlight, wind are all acceptable). This increases the chance of students getting the first part correct. The "explain" part is where the difficulty lies, but even a simple, direct link would likely earn the point for the overall question (as max score is 1).
