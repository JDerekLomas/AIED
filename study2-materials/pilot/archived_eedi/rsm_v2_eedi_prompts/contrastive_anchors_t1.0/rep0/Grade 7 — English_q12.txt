## Contrastive Analysis: "Change the following sentence from Active Voice to Passive Voice: The wind blew the leaves away."

**What prior knowledge is assumed?**

*   **Understanding of basic sentence structure:** Students need to identify the subject, verb, and object in the active voice sentence.
*   **Concept of active and passive voice:** This is a specific grammatical concept that needs to have been explicitly taught. It's not intuitive for all students, especially those learning English as a second language.
*   **Knowledge of past tense verbs and past participles:** "blew" needs to be transformed into "were blown." This requires knowing the past participle form of "blow" (blown) and the correct auxiliary verb for the past tense passive voice ("were").
*   **Prepositional phrase "by the wind":** Students need to know how to reintroduce the original agent in the passive voice.

**What language demands does it place on students with limited English?**

*   **Understanding of instructions:** "Change the following sentence from Active Voice to Passive Voice" is clear English, but "active voice" and "passive voice" are technical grammatical terms that might be new or challenging.
*   **Vocabulary:** The words in the sentence itself ("wind," "blew," "leaves," "away") are fairly common and should be accessible to Class 7 students.
*   **Grammatical transformation:** This is the core challenge. It requires manipulating sentence structure, verb forms, and adding prepositions, which is a high linguistic demand for students with limited English proficiency. They are not just recalling a word, but applying a rule to transform a sentence.

**Is this recall, application, or reasoning?**

*   **Application:** This is a clear application task. Students aren't just recalling definitions of active/passive voice; they have to *apply* the rules of transformation to a specific sentence. This is generally harder than pure recall.

**How does it compare in difficulty to typical textbook exercises?**

*   **Medium-High difficulty for its type:** Changing voice is a standard grammar exercise found in textbooks. However, for students with limited English proficiency and potentially less exposure to complex grammatical structures, it can be quite challenging.
    *   **Easier than:** Open-ended writing tasks (like "My favorite season" or "Theme of the poem") that require original thought, extensive vocabulary, and complex sentence construction.
    *   **Similar to/Slightly harder than:** Differentiating between historical sources (requires specific knowledge and ability to articulate differences).
    *   **Harder than:** Simple recall questions (like "Name two fundamental rights" or "What were the king's three questions?") where the answer is more direct and less transformational.
