## Contrastive Analysis: Active to Passive Voice Transformation

Let's break down the difficulty of "Change the following sentence from Active Voice to Passive Voice:\nThe wind blew the leaves away." for Class 7 students in Indian government schools, considering their specific context.

**Prior Knowledge Assumed:**

*   **Understanding of basic sentence structure (Subject-Verb-Object):** Students need to implicitly identify "the wind" as the subject, "blew" as the verb, and "the leaves" as the object.
*   **Concept of active and passive voice:** This is a grammatical concept. While it might be introduced in Class 7 English textbooks, active mastery, especially for students with limited English exposure, is not guaranteed. They need to understand *what* active and passive voice are and *why* they are used.
*   **Knowledge of verb tenses and their passive forms:** The original sentence is in simple past tense ("blew"). To transform it, students need to know that the passive form for simple past uses "was/were" + past participle. This requires knowing the past participle of "blow" (blown). This is a significant hurdle.
*   **Prepositional phrase "by the wind":** They need to know to reintroduce the original subject using "by."

**Language Demands on Students with Limited English:**

*   **Grammatical Terminology:** "Active Voice," "Passive Voice," "Sentence" â€“ these are technical terms. While they might be exposed to them, their comprehension and ability to apply them are often weak.
*   **Sentence Comprehension:** The sentence "The wind blew the leaves away" is relatively straightforward English, but understanding "blew...away" as a phrasal verb might be a minor point of confusion, though "blew" itself should be comprehensible.
*   **Producing Grammatically Correct English:** The core task is to *produce* a correct sentence in English. This demands not just conceptual understanding but also accurate recall of grammar rules (auxiliary verb, past participle, "by" phrase) and correct spelling.

**Recall, Application, or Reasoning?**

*   This is primarily an **application** task, but it heavily relies on **recall** of specific grammatical rules and verb forms.
    *   Recall: What is passive voice? How do you form it for simple past tense? What is the past participle of "blow"?
    *   Application: Applying these recalled rules to the given sentence.
*   There's minimal higher-order reasoning involved beyond the mechanical transformation.

**Difficulty Compared to Typical Textbook Exercises:**

*   **Typical textbook exercises:** Often present examples, then provide sentences for practice. The difficulty here lies in whether they have sufficiently practiced this *specific* transformation (simple past tense, irregular verb) to an extent that it's internalized.
*   **The specific sentence:** "The wind blew the leaves away" is a very common and standard example for active-to-passive transformation, making it somewhat "textbook-like." However, the irregular verb "blow-blew-blown" adds a layer of complexity compared to a regular verb like "The boy kicked the ball."
