    *   **Concept of Ascending Order:** Understanding what "ascending" means (smallest to largest).

    *   **"in ascending order":** This is the biggest language barrier. "Ascending" (आरोही क्रम - aarohi kram) and "descending" (अवरोही क्रम - avarohi kram) are often taught as pair concepts. If the student has only learned these terms in Hindi, they might not understand "ascending order." Even if they have seen it, it might not be internalized.
    *   **Numbers themselves:** The numbers are standard Hindu-Arabic numerals, so recognizing them isn't an issue.

    *   It's primarily **Application** of previously learned number ordering rules and place value concepts.
    *   It involves a bit of **Reasoning** to compare numbers, especially when they have different numbers of digits (e.g., 736 vs. 3367).
*   **Difficulty compared to typical textbook exercises:**

This simplifies the initial comparison greatly, as students who understand place value will immediately identify 736 as the smallest because it has fewer digits. This is a common "trick" or differentiator in ordering problems.
