**1. Analyze the Question and Rubric:**

*   **Question:** "What are the main occupations of people in the rural areas of Rajasthan? Describe any one in brief."
*   **Key elements:**
    *   Identify *main occupations*.
    *   Focus on *rural areas*.
    *   Specific region: *Rajasthan*.
    *   *Describe any one in brief*.
*   **Rubric:** "Names at least two main occupations correctly (e.g., Agriculture/farming, Animal Husbandry/pastoralism, handicrafts). Max score: 1"
    *   This is a generous rubric. Students only need to *name* two, and the "describe" part seems to be an implicit way to ensure they understand what they named, rather than a separate scoring point. If they name two correctly, they likely get the point.
    *   The examples (Agriculture, Animal Husbandry, Handicrafts) are very common and easily recognizable.

*   **Class 7, Indian government schools.**
*   **Economically weaker sections.**
*   **Limited English.**

*   **Subject:** Social Science. This is a core subject, and topics like occupations and geography are fundamental.

**3. Brainstorm Likely Errors and Challenges:**

*   **Content Knowledge (Social Science):**
    *   **Misconception:** Do students know what "occupation" means? Yes, this is a basic vocabulary word in Social Science.
    *   **Specificity:** Do they know *Rajasthan* specifically? This is the trickier part. While agriculture and animal husbandry are universal in rural India, linking them specifically to Rajasthan (known for its desert, animal herding) requires some geographical and social studies knowledge.
    *   **Lack of recall:** Students might know some occupations but fail to link them to *rural Rajasthan*.
    *   **Generic answer:** They might just list "farmer, worker" which might be too general if not implicitly linked to the rural context. However, "Agriculture/Farming" is explicitly accepted.
    *   **"Describe" part:** While the rubric is lenient, some might stumble on writing a brief description in English. But given the low bar (just naming two), this is less of a concern for *passing*.

*   **Language Misunderstandings (English):**
    *   "Occupations": Unlikely to be a major hurdle.
    *   "Rural areas": Fairly common term.
    *   "Rajasthan": Proper noun, should be recognized.
    *   "Describe in brief": Again, the rubric minimizes the impact of this if they can just *name* two.
    *   **Spelling:** Students might misspell words like "agriculture," "husbandry," "handicrafts." The examiners are usually lenient with spelling as long as the intent is clear (as seen in the calibration examples).

*   **Attempt vs. Failure to Attempt:**
    *   This is a straightforward, factual recall question. Students who know it will attempt it. Those who don't might write something generic or leave it blank. Unlike the poetry analysis, there's less room for complex interpretation that leads to non-attempts.

*   **Common Wrong Answers:**
    *   Listing occupations not specific to rural Rajasthan (e.g., "teacher, doctor" if they miss "rural").
    *   Listing only one occupation.
    *   Generic terms like "labourer" without further context.
    *   Attempting to describe but failing to name two distinct occupations first.
    *   Blank.
