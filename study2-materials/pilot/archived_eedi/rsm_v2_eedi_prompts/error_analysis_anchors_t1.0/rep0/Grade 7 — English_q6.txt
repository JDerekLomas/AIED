**3. Brainstorm Likely Errors and Misconceptions:**

*   **Vocabulary Deficiency:**
    *   Students know the poem but don't have the English word for "simple," "descriptive," etc.
    *   They might use a vague or incorrect adjective (e.g., "good," "nice," "emotional," "sad," "happy," "beautiful"). These describe the *effect* or *content*, not the *style*.
    *   They might use an adjective that's too general or not specific to writing style (e.g., "long," "short").
*   **Misinterpretation of "Writing Style":**
    *   Confusion with "theme," "message," "what the poet writes about." (Similar to the 5% item: "Write a theme...")
    *   Confusion with "poet's feelings" or "mood of the poem."
    *   They might describe the *language* as "English" or "Hindi" (if they translate the concept).
*   **Lack of Content Knowledge (of the poem itself):** If they haven't read/understood the poem, they can't describe its style. However, this question is usually linked to a specific poem in the exam, so we assume they've been exposed to it. The challenge is describing it.
*   **Attempt vs. Non-Attempt:** Many will likely attempt, but fail due to the above reasons. Some might leave it blank if they truly have no idea.
*   **Common Wrong Answers:**
    *   "The poet writes about nature." (Theme/Content)
    *   "The poem is beautiful." (Effect/Opinion)
    *   "The poet feels sad." (Poet's emotion)
    *   "He writes in English." (Obvious, not style)
    *   "Easy language." (This *could* be correct if they use "simple," but often expressed poorly).
    *   Vague adjectives like "good," "nice."
