Let's break down the potential errors for "Rearrange the words to make a meaningful sentence: book/reading/a/is/he".

**Analysis of Likely Errors:**

Given the context of Class 6 students from economically weaker sections with limited English and the common errors observed in other questions:

*   **Misconceptions about English Grammar/Sentence Structure:**
    *   **Lack of Subject-Verb-Object Understanding:** This is fundamental. Many might not grasp that a sentence needs a clear "doer" (subject), an "action" (verb), and sometimes "what is being acted upon" (object). They might string words together based on familiarity or the order they've seen them in simple phrases.
    *   **Confusion with Auxiliary Verbs:** "is" might be difficult to place correctly. They might know "he is" but struggle when other words are interspersed.
    *   **Articles ("a"):** While "a book" might be a common phrase, placing "a" correctly in the rearranged sentence could be an issue if their understanding of articles is weak.
    *   **Verb Forms (Gerund "reading"):** While "reading" is given, understanding its role in the "is reading" continuous present tense structure might be challenging.

*   **Language Misunderstandings:**
    *   **Vocabulary Recognition (Individual Words):** The words "book," "reading," "a," "is," "he" are all very basic and likely recognizable. This is less of a barrier than in the "poet's writing style" question.
    *   **Concept of "Meaningful Sentence":** They might understand "sentence" but "meaningful" could be subjective if their grammatical understanding is poor. A grammatically incorrect sentence might still hold some "meaning" to them if they recognize the words.

*   **Attempt vs. No Attempt:**
    *   **High Attempt Rate:** This question is relatively straightforward in terms of instructions and vocabulary. It doesn't require complex thinking, writing a paragraph, or interpreting abstract concepts. Most students would likely attempt it.
    *   **Failed Attempts:** Many attempts would likely be incorrect due to the grammatical issues mentioned above.

*   **Most Common Wrong Answers (based on observed errors in other questions):**

    *   **Random Ordering/Lack of Coherence:** Similar to the "empty response" or "random words" seen in other questions, students might just write the words in a different, illogical order.
        *   "Book reading is he a."
        *   "Is he reading a book." (Missing capital, but grammatically almost there)
        *   "Reading he is a book."
        *   "A book is reading he."
    *   **Subject-Verb Inversion (Common in questions, but confusing for statements):**
        *   "Is he reading a book?" (If they confuse it with a question structure)
    *   **Dropping words:** Less likely here as there are so few words and all are clearly given.
    *   **Partial Correctness:** They might get "he is" or "a book" right, but struggle with the overall arrangement.
        *   "He is a book reading."
        *   "Reading a book he is."

**Why this question is "easier" than the examples provided:**

*   **Concrete Vocabulary:** All words are highly common and concrete.
*   **Simple Task:** Rearranging words is a more direct task than describing style, identifying a theme, or writing a multi-sentence paragraph.
*   **No Abstract Concepts:** No need to infer, interpret, or summarize.

**Why it's still challenging for a low-pass-rate group:**

*   **Fundamental Grammar:** While basic, Subject-Verb-Object structure is *fundamental*. If students lack this, even simple rearrangements will be hard.
*   **Lack of Exposure/Practice:** Students with limited English exposure might not have internalized the "feel" of correct English sentence structure.
*   **Stress/Exam Conditions:** Even simple tasks can be botched under pressure.
