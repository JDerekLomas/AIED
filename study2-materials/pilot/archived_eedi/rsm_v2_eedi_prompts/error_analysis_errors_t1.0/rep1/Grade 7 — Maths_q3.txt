**Analysis of Likely Errors:**

1.  **Misconceptions about Mathematics:**
    *   **Formula Recall/Confusion:** This is the *most significant* mathematical hurdle.
        *   **Multiplication of base and height only (Area = b * h):** This is a very common error, confusing it with the area of a rectangle/parallelogram. Students might remember "multiply the two numbers" but forget the "half." This is often reinforced by simpler area problems in earlier grades.
        *   **Adding base and height (Area = b + h):** Less common for area, but possible if they are just looking for *any* operation between the two numbers.
        *   **Confusion with perimeter:** Though unlikely for this specific question (as it asks for "area"), if students are weak, they might associate "finding something about the shape" with adding sides, even if not explicitly presented as sides.
        *   **Units Confusion:** While not affecting the score for *finding* the area, some might forget or incorrectly state units (e.g., cm instead of cm²). The rubric focuses on the formula and calculation, so this might not penalize *correctness* as much as neatness.
    *   **Fractional Operations (1/2):** While Class 7 students should be familiar with fractions, the "1/2" might be an extra step where errors occur if they try to do it in multiple steps (e.g., 8 * 6 = 48, then 48/2 = ?). Simple division by 2 should be manageable.

2.  **Language Misunderstandings:**
    *   **"Area":** This term should generally be understood by Class 7, as area is a fundamental concept taught from earlier grades. However, for a student with very weak foundational English, it *could* be a barrier, leading them to guess an operation or simply not attempt.
    *   **"Base" and "Height":** These are specific geometrical terms. If the teaching has been purely rote or they missed the specific lesson, they might not associate "base" and "height" with the sides to be multiplied. However, given these are the *only* two numbers provided, most students would infer they are the relevant ones.
    *   **Overall sentence structure:** The sentence is straightforward ("Find the area of a triangle whose base is 8 cm and height is 6 cm."). It's unlikely to pose a significant grammatical barrier beyond the key terms.

3.  **Attempt vs. Not Attempt:**
    *   **Attempt:** Most students *will* attempt this question. It's a direct problem with numbers, not abstract reasoning. Even if they don't know the correct formula, they will likely try to perform *some* operation with 8 and 6.
    *   **Not Attempt:** Only students with extreme gaps in understanding or very low confidence in English/Math would leave it blank. A student who genuinely doesn't know what "area" means or what to do with the numbers would not attempt.

4.  **Most Common Wrong Answer:**
    *   **48 (cm²):** This is overwhelmingly the most common wrong answer. It comes from applying `Area = base × height`, omitting the `1/2`.
    *   Less common but possible:
        *   14 (cm): From `base + height`.
        *   Some might write the correct formula but make a calculation error, though for 48/2=24, it's a simple division.
