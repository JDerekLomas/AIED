    *   *Example Error:* "My hobby is playing cricket. I play cricket in the evening. Cricket is good." (3 short, repetitive sentences, not a cohesive paragraph).

*   **Repetition:** To fill the sentence count, they might repeat information or phrases.
    *   *Example Error:* "My hobby is playing football. I like football. I play football in the ground. Football is a good game."

**2. Language Misunderstandings:**

*   **"Mention what it is, when you do it, and why you enjoy it":** This instruction might be misinterpreted or partially followed.
    *   **"What it is":** Most will get this part (e.g., "My hobby is reading").
    *   **"When you do it":** Might be vague (e.g., "sometimes," "in free time") or a simple, single sentence.
    *   **"Why you enjoy it":** This is the most challenging part for students with limited vocabulary and abstract thinking in English. They might struggle to articulate *reasons* for enjoyment beyond "it is good" or "I like it." This is where many will fail to meet the "clearly mentions why enjoyable" part of the rubric.
        *   *Example Error:* "I like drawing because it is good." (Doesn't elaborate on *why* it's good or what specific enjoyment it brings).
*   **Vocabulary Limitations:** Difficulty finding appropriate words to describe their hobby or the enjoyment derived from it.
*   **Grammar and Spelling Errors:** While the rubric focuses on content, egregious errors can sometimes obscure meaning, though typically, if the content is there, minor errors are overlooked. However, severe grammatical issues can prevent a sentence from being "clear."

*   **Attempt but Fail:** Given that "My Favourite Hobby" is a relatively common and accessible topic, most students will attempt it. This is not like "Describes the poet's writing style" which is highly abstract.
*   **Partial Attempt:** Many will write 1-3 sentences but fail to meet the 4-5 sentence count or address all three prompts clearly.
