3.  **Lack of Understanding of Tense Conversion:**
    *   The original sentence is in the Simple Past ("blew"). To form the passive voice, they need "was/were + past participle." This involves understanding subject-verb agreement ("leaves *were* blown," not "leaves *was* blown") and the correct past participle.

4.  **Identifying the Subject/Object:**
    *   Correctly identifying "the wind" as the *doer* (agent) and "the leaves" as the *receiver* (object) is crucial for the transformation. They might flip them incorrectly or struggle to identify them.

5.  **Placement of "by the agent":**

6.  **"Be" verb confusion:** Many will forget or misuse the "be" verb (was/were) which is essential for passive voice construction.

7.  **Simple Word Swap Attempt:** Given limited understanding, many might attempt a simple word reordering without understanding the grammatical rules.
