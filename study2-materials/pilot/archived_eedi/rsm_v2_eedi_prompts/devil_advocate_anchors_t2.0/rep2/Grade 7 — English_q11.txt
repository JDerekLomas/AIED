The question asks students to identify an adjective *phrase*. The rubric, however, states that a score of 1 is given if "the student correctly identifies 'tiny' as an adjective." This creates a significant discrepancy.

Let's first consider the question as posed: "Identify the adjective phrase in the sentence: A tiny bird built its nest in our balcony."
An adjective phrase consists of an adjective and any words that modify it, acting like a single adjective. In this sentence, there isn't a complex adjective phrase; "tiny" is simply an adjective modifying "bird." If students are truly looking for an *adjective phrase*, they might struggle because the most obvious adjective ("tiny") isn't a phrase on its own. They might even try to identify "in our balcony" as a phrase modifying "nest" (an adverbial phrase, or a prepositional phrase acting adverbially), or misunderstand what an adjective phrase is.

Now let's consider the rubric's interpretation: "The student correctly identifies 'tiny' as an adjective."
This dramatically changes the difficulty. Identifying a single adjective is much easier than identifying an adjective phrase, especially in a sentence where no clear adjective phrase (beyond a single adjective) exists.

Given the rubric's interpretation, where simply identifying "tiny" scores 1 point, this question becomes much more straightforward.

*   **"Identify the adjective 'tiny'" (hypothetically, under the rubric):** This requires understanding what an adjective is and applying that knowledge to a simple, common word. Many students, even those with limited English, would be able to point to a word describing something (size, color, etc.) as an "adjective" if they have been taught the concept. The word "tiny" is quite simple. The sentence structure is also simple.

The challenge is the discrepancy. If the teachers actually score based on "tiny" as an adjective, then the question is quite easy. If the question implicitly asks for something more complex (like a more involved adjective phrase, or students try to analyze "A tiny bird" as a noun phrase with an adjective, etc.), then it's harder.

I would place this closer to the 50-66% range if it were simply "Identify the adjective."
