Students at this level often struggle with operations involving negative integers, especially when the result is negative. They are more comfortable with situations where a smaller number is subtracted from a larger one (e.g., `9 - 4`), rather than `4 - 9`.
    *   **Conceptual misunderstanding of 'balancing' an equation:** While they might have been taught to "do the same thing to both sides," the *why* behind it might be weak. They might see `y + 9 = 4` and instinctively try to make `y` a positive number.
    *   **Order of operations confusion (less likely here, but possible):** In simpler equations like this, it's less of a problem, but if they try to do something with 9 and 4 before isolating y, it could lead to errors.
    *   **"Bigger minus Smaller" heuristic:** They might default to subtracting the smaller number from the larger number, regardless of the order in the equation. So, `9 - 4` is a very strong pull.

    *   **Most students would attempt this.** It's a fundamental algebra problem with simple numbers. It doesn't look overly complex at first glance.
    *   **Failure would be common.** The attempt would likely involve performing an incorrect operation.
