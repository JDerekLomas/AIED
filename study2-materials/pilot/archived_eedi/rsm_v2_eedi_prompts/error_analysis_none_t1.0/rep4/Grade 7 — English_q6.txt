**Likely Errors and Misconceptions:**

1.  **"Describes" Misunderstanding:**
    *   **Misconception:** Students might interpret "describes" as "to write about" or "to tell a story about." They might think they need to provide an example of *what* the poet wrote about, rather than *how* they wrote it.
    *   **Language Misunderstanding:** The nuance of "describes" in this context (characterizing a style) might be lost. They might associate "describe" with "give details about" a subject, not an attribute.
    *   **Attempt/Fail:** Most would attempt but fail to hit the mark. They'd likely provide content from the poem.

2.  **"Poet's Writing Style" Misunderstanding:**
    *   **Misconception:** This is likely the biggest hurdle. Many students, especially at this level, might not have a strong grasp of what "writing style" actually means in a literary context. They might confuse it with:
        *   **Theme/Message:** What the poet is *trying to say*.
        *   **Mood/Tone:** The feeling of the poem.
        *   **Subject Matter:** What the poem is *about*.
        *   **Format:** Is it a poem, a story, etc.? (Less likely, but possible).
        *   **Poet's Life/Personality:** Is the poet happy, sad, old?
    *   **Language Misunderstanding:** "Style" is an abstract concept. They might know "style" in terms of fashion ("good style of clothes") but not "literary style." "Writing style" might just sound like "how the poet writes," which they might interpret as "what the poet writes about."
    *   **Attempt/Fail:** Most would attempt, but their answers would be off-topic, focusing on content or theme rather than style.

3.  **Vocabulary Limitations:**
    *   Even if a student understands "writing style," they might lack the specific vocabulary to articulate it (e.g., "concise," "evocative," "metaphorical," "colloquial"). They might struggle to find a single, suitable word even if they conceptually grasp it. They might go for simpler, less precise words that don't quite fit the rubric.
    *   **Attempt/Fail:** They might try to use a simple adjective (e.g., "good," "nice," "emotional") that doesn't effectively describe style.

4.  **Not Attempting at All:**
    *   Some students might be completely overwhelmed by the abstract nature of the question and the perceived difficulty of the vocabulary required.
    *   **Attempt/Fail:** Likely a smaller percentage, but it will happen.

**Most Common Wrong Answers Would Look Like:**

Based on these errors, the most common wrong answers would likely fall into these categories:

*   **Focus on Theme/Content:** "The poet describes nature." "The poet talks about sadness." "The poet writes about life." "He writes about his feelings."
*   **Focus on Mood/Effect:** "The poem is emotional." "It makes me feel happy." "The poet's writing is touching."
*   **Generic Positive/Negative:** "The poet writes well." "It's a beautiful style." "Good writing."
*   **Restating the Question (partially):** "The poet's writing style is writing." (Less common but possible from very weak students).
*   **Confusion with Poet's Traits:** "The poet is wise." "The poet is observant."
