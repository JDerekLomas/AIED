Let's break down the potential errors for Class 7 Indian government school students from economically weaker sections with limited English attempting the question: "Describes the poet's writing style."

**Likely Errors and Misconceptions:**

1.  **"Describes" Misunderstanding:**
    *   Students might interpret "describes" as "tells what" or "talks about" in a very general sense, rather than "gives the characteristics of."
    *   They might think they need to *summarize* the poem's content rather than characterize the *way* it's written.
    *   *Example:* "The poem describes a village." (focus on content)

2.  **"Poet's" Misunderstanding:**
    *   This is less likely to be a major hurdle, but some might confuse "poet's" with "poem's" and thus focus on the poem itself rather than the creator's *method*.

3.  **"Writing Style" Misunderstanding (The Biggest Hurdle):**
    *   **Lack of Metalanguage:** This is the most significant issue. "Writing style" is an abstract concept that requires a specific vocabulary (metalanguage) to address. Most Class 7 students, especially with limited English exposure, won't have been explicitly taught terms like "descriptive," "informal," "direct," "figurative," "simple," "complex," "lyrical," "narrative," "autobiographical," etc., in an analytical context.
    *   **Concrete vs. Abstract:** Their English comprehension is likely more geared towards concrete nouns, verbs, and simple sentence structures. "Writing style" is an abstract concept.
    *   **Synonym Confusion:** Even if they grasp "style," they might struggle to think of suitable synonyms. They might associate "style" with fashion or appearance rather than literary technique.
    *   *Example:* "The poet's writing style is nice." (Too vague, doesn't describe the *characteristics* of the style). "The poet writes well." (Again, too evaluative, not descriptive).

4.  **Vocabulary Deficit:**
    *   Even if they understand the question's intent, they might not possess the specific English vocabulary to answer it correctly. The rubric words ("simple," "descriptive," "informal," "direct") are relatively common, but applying them precisely to "writing style" is a different skill.
    *   They might know "simple" but use it to mean "easy to understand" (which is close but not quite "writing style"). They might not know "descriptive" or "informal" at all in this context.

5.  **Grammatical Errors/Sentence Structure:**
    *   Even if they get the word, they might struggle to formulate a grammatically correct sentence or phrase. "Describes the poet's writing style... simple." might be a common attempt.

**Would students attempt but fail, or not attempt at all?**

*   **Attempt and Fail (Most Common):** Given the average pass rate of 29%, most students will likely attempt the question. They've been taught to try answering.
*   **Not Attempt (Less Common, but significant):** A smaller but notable percentage will leave it blank if they feel utterly lost or cannot extract a relevant word from their limited vocabulary. This would be true for those who genuinely don't understand "writing style" at all.

**What would the most common wrong answer look like?**

Based on the above, here are likely common wrong answers, often reflecting attempts to answer with the closest concept they understand:

*   **Focus on Content/Theme:**
    *   "The poem is about nature."
    *   "It describes the beauty of the village."
    *   "The poet describes his feelings."
*   **Vague Evaluation (lacking specific style description):**
    *   "The writing style is good."
    *   "The poet writes well."
    *   "The writing style is nice."
    *   "It is understandable." / "It is easy to understand." (This is getting closer to "simple" but often isn't framed directly as a style characteristic.)
*   **Misinterpretation of "style" (fashion/appearance):**
    *   (Less likely, but possible in extreme cases) "The poet has a good style of dress." (Highly unlikely, but indicates complete misunderstanding of "writing style").
*   **Identifying the Poem's Type (not the *writing* style):**
    *   "It is a poem."
    *   "It is a story."
*   **One-word answer, not suitable:**
    *   "Happy."
