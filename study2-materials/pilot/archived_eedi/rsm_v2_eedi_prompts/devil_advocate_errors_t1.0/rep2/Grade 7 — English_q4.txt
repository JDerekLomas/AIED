Let's break down the question and the context.

**The Question:** "Do you think the king's questions are important in real life? Give one reason."

**Key Challenges for the Students:**

1.  **"Do you think... important in real life?"**: This requires an abstract thought process, connecting a fictional scenario to their own reality. This is a higher-order thinking skill than simple recall.
2.  **"Give one reason"**: This demands not just an opinion, but justification. Even if they grasp the concept, articulating a *reason* in English can be difficult.
3.  **Understanding the King's Questions (from the passage)**: The rubric mentions the king wanted to know "the right time to do everything, the right thing to do, and the right people to work with." Students would need to have understood this from the preceding passage, which itself could have been challenging.
4.  **Limited English Proficiency**:
    *   "Important" is a common word, but "real life" might be slightly more abstract for some.
    *   Formulating a coherent sentence for the *reason* will be a major hurdle. The common errors show fragmented sentences, grammar issues, and simply listing things rather than explaining.
5.  **Lack of Practice with this Question Type**: The previous examples show students struggling with "describe," "write a theme," "write a paragraph." This question is even more open-ended and analytical.
6.  **Exam Anxiety/Blank Responses**: Given the 29% average pass rate and the prevalence of "Empty Response," many will simply give up.
7.  **Basic Concepts Not Basic**: While the king's questions (right time, right thing, right people) seem universally important to adults, for a student from an economically weaker background, especially if they struggle with the language, connecting these abstract ideas to their "real life" and articulating it might not be intuitive or simple.
8.  **Vague Rubric**: "Any valid reason" is good, but students still need to articulate *a* reason.

Highly unlikely. They might just write "Yes" or struggle with the "because" part.
