**Challenging the Instinct (Applying the Given Constraints):**

1.  **Limited English:**
    *   "At 10 am in the morning..." - "am" might be understood, but "in the morning" is redundant and adds words.
    *   "...temperature of Jodhpur was 30^0 C." - "temperature" is a key word, but some might struggle. "Jodhpur" is irrelevant noise.
    *   "...temperature increases by 2^0 C per hour..." - "increases by" is crucial. "Per hour" is also crucial for multiplication.
    *   "...what will be the temperature of Jodhpur at 4:00 in the evening?" - "what will be" implies future, requiring calculation. "in the evening" helps with 4:00 pm, but again, extra words.
    *   **Impact:** Students might miss "increases," confuse "per hour" with just adding 2 once, or struggle to grasp the core of the problem due to vocabulary load.

2.  **"Basic" Concepts Aren't Basic for Struggling Learners:**
    *   **Time Calculation (10 am to 4 pm):** This is the *most crucial* step and where many will falter.
        *   They need to understand "am" vs. "pm."
        *   They need to count hours: 10 to 11 (1), 11 to 12 (2), 12 to 1 (3), 1 to 2 (4), 2 to 3 (5), 3 to 4 (6).
        *   *Common error:* Some might just do 4 - 10 (negative) or 10 - 4 = 6 (by coincidence, but flawed reasoning). Or simply 4 hours. Or assume 12-hour clock vs. 24-hour clock issues.
        *   *Challenge:* This isn't just subtraction. It's understanding time intervals across noon.
    *   **Multiplication:** 6 hours * 2 degrees/hour = 12 degrees. This is simple multiplication, but if they get the hours wrong, the multiplication is wrong.
    *   **Addition:** 30 degrees + 12 degrees = 42 degrees. This is simple addition.
    *   **Impact:** The time calculation is a significant barrier. If they can't get `6` hours, the rest of the problem is doomed.

    *   Many students might see a multi-step word problem and simply shut down.
    *   They might not have practiced problems that combine time calculation with rate changes.
    *   They might not know how to break down the problem.
