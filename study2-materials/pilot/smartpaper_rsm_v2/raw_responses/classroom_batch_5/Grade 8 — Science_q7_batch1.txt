**Student 4 (Competent)**

*   **Answer:** Yes, the gardener will definitely notice a significant change after some days. The plant, vegetable, and fruit waste will begin to decompose. Initially, it might shrink in volume, become darker in color, and develop a distinct smell. The texture will change from solid waste to a more crumbly, soil-like substance.

    The reason for this change is the action of microorganisms such as bacteria and fungi, along with insects like earthworms. These organisms break down the complex organic matter into simpler substances through a process called decomposition.

    This process is highly beneficial for soil health. The decomposed material is rich in nutrients and forms compost or manure. Compost enriches the soil by:
    1.  **Adding Nutrients:** It releases essential plant nutrients like nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium.
    2.  **Improving Soil Structure:** It makes the soil more porous, allowing better air circulation and water retention.
    3.  **Increasing Water Holding Capacity:** Compost acts like a sponge, helping the soil retain moisture for longer.
    4.  **Enhancing Microbial Activity:** It promotes the growth of beneficial soil microbes, which further improve soil fertility.
    5.  **Reducing Chemical Fertilizer Need:** Regular use of compost reduces the reliance on artificial chemical fertilizers.

    Therefore, collecting and composting organic waste is an excellent practice for maintaining and improving long-term soil health and fertility.
