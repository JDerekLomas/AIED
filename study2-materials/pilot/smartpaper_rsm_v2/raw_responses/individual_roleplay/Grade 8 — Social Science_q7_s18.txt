**The Battle of Plassey in 1757 was a very important event that greatly helped the British to expand their power in India.** Here's how:

1.  **Gaining Political Control in Bengal:** Before the battle, the British East India Company was mainly a trading company in Bengal, operating under the Nawab's authority. After their victory over Siraj-ud-daulah, they installed Mir Jafar as the new Nawab. Mir Jafar was a puppet ruler, meaning he did whatever the British wanted. This gave the British direct political control over Bengal, which was one of the richest provinces in India.

2.  **Access to Bengal's Wealth:** With Mir Jafar as Nawab, the British East India Company gained huge amounts of wealth. They received large sums of money as "war reparations" and gifts from Mir Jafar. More importantly, they got the undisputed right to collect revenue (taxes) from a vast area of Bengal. This immense wealth was crucial. It funded their armies, helped them pay for their administration, and financed their further military campaigns across India.

3.  **Weakening of Indian Rulers:** The Battle of Plassey showed how an Indian ruler (Siraj-ud-daulah) could be betrayed by his own people (like Mir Jafar and Jagat Seth) and defeated by a comparatively smaller British force.
