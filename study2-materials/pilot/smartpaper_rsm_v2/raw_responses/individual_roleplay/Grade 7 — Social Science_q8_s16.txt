**Main occupations in rural areas of Rajasthan are:**

1.  **Agriculture:** This is the most important one. People grow crops like bajra, wheat, jowar, pulses, and some spices.
2.  **Animal Husbandry:** Many families keep animals like cows, buffaloes, goats, camels, and sheep. They get milk, wool, and use animals for farming work.
3.  **Handicrafts:** Some people make things by hand like pottery, carpets, jewellery, block printing clothes, and leather goods. These are often sold to tourists or in markets.
4.  **Labour work:** Many people work as daily wage labourers in fields, construction, or other small jobs.
5.  **Small businesses/Shops:** Some people have small shops selling daily use items or run small businesses like tea stalls.

**Brief description of Agriculture:**

In Rajasthan's villages, farming is the main work for most people. Because there is less rain in many parts, farmers often grow crops that don't need too much water, like bajra (pearl millet) and jowar (sorghum). They also grow wheat, especially in areas with irrigation from canals or wells. During the rainy season (kharif), they plant bajra and pulses. In winter (rabi), they grow wheat and mustard.
