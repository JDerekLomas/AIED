This is a great question that really makes me think about my students in Class 6. Let's break it down.

First, let's assume the poem has been taught and discussed in class. If it hasn't, then the scores would be significantly lower across the board. Assuming it has, here's my breakdown:

**Struggling (35% of students):**
* **Language barriers:** Many struggle to even read the poem independently, let alone understand nuances. They might recognize a few words but the overall meaning is lost.
* **Recall vs. Reasoning:** This question requires both. They need to recall lines from the poem AND reason why they like it. The "why" is a major hurdle.
* **Specificity:** "Denotes a line from the poem and mentions why." This is quite specific. Just picking a line isn't enough.
* **Common mistakes:** They might just point to a random line, or repeat a line without any explanation. If they try to explain, it will likely be in Hindi or very broken English, making it hard to understand their reasoning. They might say "I like this line because it is good," which isn't sufficient for the "why."

**Struggling: 0% full marks**
*Realistically, none of my struggling students would manage to articulate a specific line and a coherent reason in English that meets the rubric's requirements. They might point, or say a word, but not a full line with a 'why'.*

**Basic (35% of students):**
* **Language barriers:** They can read simpler lines, but complex vocabulary or sentence structures still pose a challenge. They might understand the general gist of the poem.
* **Recall vs. Reasoning:** They can recall some lines, especially if they were highlighted or repeated in class. The "why" is still a big hurdle. They might be able to offer a very simple reason.
* **Specificity:** They might pick a line, but their "why" will likely be very brief and perhaps not fully elaborated. For example, "I like this line because it is about birds," which might be too generic.
* **Common mistakes:** Providing a reason that is too simple or just a restatement of the line's content rather than a personal connection or deeper understanding. They might struggle with sentence construction for their explanation.

**Basic: 5% full marks**
*A very small percentage, perhaps 1 or 2 students in this group, if they've grasped a particular line and have a very simple, direct reason (e.g., "I like 'The sun shines bright' because I like sun") and can articulate it in basic English, might scrape by. They would need significant support or prior scaffolding to get here.*

**Competent (20% of students):**
* **Language barriers:** They can read and understand most of the poem with some dictionary help or teacher explanation.
* **Recall vs. Reasoning:** They can recall several lines. The "why" is where they shine. They can connect the line to their own experience or to a theme in the poem.
* **Specificity:** They can generally select a line and provide a clear, albeit perhaps not deeply profound, reason in relatively correct English.
* **Common mistakes:** Their "why" might still be a bit superficial, or their English might have minor grammatical errors, but it would be understandable and meet the criteria.

**Competent: 60% full marks**
*Most of these students would be able to pick a line and give a reasonable explanation. They might not write perfect English, but their understanding and ability to articulate it would be evident.*

**Advanced (10% of students):**
* **Language barriers:** They read and understand the poem well, often picking up on subtleties.
* **Recall vs. Reasoning:** They can easily recall lines and provide well-articulated, insightful reasons for their choice, often connecting it to themes, imagery, or personal reflection.
* **Specificity:** They would effortlessly meet both parts of the rubric, often exceeding expectations with their depth of explanation.
* **Common mistakes:** Minimal to none for this specific task.

**Advanced: 90% full marks**
*These students are likely to score full marks. The 10% not scoring might be due to a momentary lapse, misinterpreting the "why" slightly, or just not fully engaging with the question on that particular day. But generally, they would nail it.*

---

**Summary of full marks:**

struggling: 0% full marks
basic: 5% full marks
competent: 60% full marks
advanced: 90% full marks
