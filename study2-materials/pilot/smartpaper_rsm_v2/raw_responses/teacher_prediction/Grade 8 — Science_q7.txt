**Analysis of the Question:**

*   **Language Barrier:** The language is relatively simple English, but "evaluate if this is beneficial for soil health" might be slightly tricky for the struggling group. "Collected waste," "pit," "notice any change," "reasons," "beneficial for soil health" are all common words they'd encounter.
*   **Recall vs. Reasoning:** This question primarily requires recall of the concept of decomposition/composting, which is usually covered in Class 7 or 8. The "reasons" part requires recalling the role of microorganisms. "Beneficial for soil health" also requires recall of the benefits of manure/compost.
*   **Specificity:** The required answer is quite specific:
    *   Yes, change will be noticed (decay/rotting/disappearance).
    *   Reason: Microorganisms (bacteria, fungi) decompose the waste.
    *   Benefit: Forms manure/compost, which improves soil fertility, water retention, provides nutrients.
*   **Common Mistakes:**
    *   Not connecting the "change" to decomposition.
    *   Not mentioning microorganisms as the *reason*. They might just say "it rots."
    *   Struggling to articulate the *benefits* for soil health beyond "it's good."
    *   Confusing decomposition with burning or just "disappearing."
    *   Language issues in expressing the scientific terms.
