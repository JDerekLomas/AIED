Let's break down the question "Write a short paragraph (4-5 sentences) about 'My Favourite Hobby'. Mention what it is, when you do it, and why you enjoy it." for Class 7 students in Indian government schools.

**Contrastive Analysis:**

1.  **Prior Knowledge Assumed:**
    *   **Vocabulary:** "Hobby," "enjoy," "favourite," "paragraph," "mention." These are fairly common words, likely encountered in earlier grades or daily life, even if English proficiency is limited. "Paragraph" might be a challenge if formal writing instruction has been minimal.
    *   **Grammar/Syntax:** Basic sentence structure (subject-verb-object), present tense for habits/preferences, conjunctions (and, because).
    *   **Concept:** Understanding what a "hobby" is (leisure activity). This is a very common and relatable concept across cultures.
    *   **Self-reflection:** Ability to articulate personal preferences and reasons. This is usually easier than abstract concepts.

2.  **Language Demands on Students with Limited English:**
    *   **Simplicity of Prompt:** The instructions are very direct and use relatively simple vocabulary. "Write a short paragraph" is clear. "Mention what it is, when you do it, and why you enjoy it" breaks down the task into explicit components, which is helpful.
    *   **Relatability:** The topic "My Favourite Hobby" is extremely personal and familiar. Students don't need to understand complex literary devices or historical facts. They can draw directly from their own experiences. This significantly reduces the cognitive load compared to understanding a poem or abstract concept.
    *   **Sentence Structure:** Students can likely use simple, declarative sentences. "My favourite hobby is playing cricket. I play it in the evening. I enjoy it because it is fun." This level of English is accessible.

3.  **Recall, Application, or Reasoning?**
    *   This question primarily requires **recall** of personal information (what their hobby is, when they do it) and **application** of basic English sentence construction.
    *   It requires very minimal **reasoning** beyond articulating "why I enjoy it," which is usually a simple statement for this age group (e.g., "it is fun," "I learn new things," "I get strong").
