**Contrastive Analysis:**

1.  **Prior Knowledge Assumed:**

        *   "Style": This is the trickiest word. While it has a general meaning in Hindi (e.g., "shaili" or "andaz"), applying it specifically to "writing" in an analytical sense might be challenging for students with limited English exposure to academic/literary terms. 3.  **Cognitive Demand (Recall, Application, Reasoning):**
    *   This is primarily **Application/Reasoning**, but with a strong prerequisite of **Prior Knowledge/Conceptual Understanding**.
    *   It's not recall, as they haven't memorized a poet's style.
    *   It requires them to analyze (or pretend to analyze, if decontextualized) a piece of writing (even if imaginary or generic) and abstractly characterize its manner of expression. This is a higher-order thinking skill than simple comprehension or factual recall.
    *   Without a specific poem, it becomes an exercise in general literary understanding rather than specific textual analysis. They are being asked to apply a concept (writing style) that they may not have fully grasped or been taught to apply.
