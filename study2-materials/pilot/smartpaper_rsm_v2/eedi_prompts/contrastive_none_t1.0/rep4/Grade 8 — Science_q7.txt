**Contrastive Analysis of the Question:**

"A gardener has collected waste of plants, vegetables and fruits in a pit. Does gardener notice any change after some days? Give reasons and evaluate if this is beneficial for soil health"

    *   **Decomposition:** The fundamental concept that organic matter breaks down. This is typically covered in Class 6-8 science, often in chapters about waste management, natural cycles (e.g., carbon cycle, nutrient cycle), or even basic biology (microorganisms).
    *   **Role of Microorganisms:** While not explicitly asked, understanding *why* decomposition happens (bacteria, fungi) would strengthen the reasoning. This might be a slightly more advanced concept, but the *effect* of their action (decomposition) is generally understood.
    *   **Compost/Manure:** The idea that decomposed organic matter is good for plants/soil. This is very commonly taught in agriculture-related topics, waste management, and environmental science sections.
    *   **Observational Skills:** The ability to infer "changes" (smell, appearance, reduction in volume).

2.  **Language Demands (Limited English Proficiency):**

    *   **"After some days":** Clear time frame.
    *   **"Give reasons":** Requires causal explanation. This is where language becomes slightly more complex, as students need to formulate sentences explaining *why* the change occurs.
    *   **"Evaluate if this is beneficial for soil health":** "Evaluate" is a slightly higher-order thinking verb. "Beneficial" and "soil health" are key scientific terms that should be known. The structure "if X is Y" is simple enough.
    *   **Overall:** The vocabulary is mostly accessible. The main challenge would be *articulating* the reasons and evaluation clearly in English, rather than understanding the individual words. Hindi-medium students might struggle to translate their scientific understanding into fluent English explanations.

        *   Inferring the *cause* of the change (microorganisms, natural processes).
        *   Evaluating the *effect* of this process on soil health.
