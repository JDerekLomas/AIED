"Evaluate" and "beneficial" might be particularly tricky.
    *   **Self-correction:** Many of these concepts (kabad, gaddha, badlav, fayda, mitti) would be familiar in Hindi. The challenge is connecting them to the English terms. Visuals or simple examples often work best here.

    *   **Recall:** Students need to recall the concept of decomposition/composting. They would have likely learned this when discussing microorganisms, waste management, or the life cycle of plants (nutrient cycling).

    *   **Incomplete explanation of "beneficial":** They might just say "it's good for soil" without specifying *why* (nutrients, fertility).
    *   **Vocabulary issues:** Using terms like "rot" or "decay" might be there, but more specific scientific terms like "decomposition," "composting," "humus," "manure," "microorganisms" might be missing or misused.
    *   **Focusing only on the first part:** Answering "yes, it will change" and stopping there, without addressing reasons or benefits.
    *   **Conceptual misunderstanding:** A few might confuse it with simple drying out or just piling up without understanding the biological process.
