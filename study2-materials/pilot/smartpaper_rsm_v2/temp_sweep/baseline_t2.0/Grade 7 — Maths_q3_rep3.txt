*   **Language Barrier:** The problem is short and uses common mathematical terms ("area," "triangle," "base," "height," "cm"). Even for Hindi-medium students, these terms are often introduced in English from Class 6 onwards in math class, alongside their Hindi equivalents (क्षेत्रफल, त्रिभुज, आधार, ऊंचाई). The main hurdle won't be understanding *what* "base" and "height" are, but perhaps the sentence structure for some. However, the numbers are clear.
*   **Recall vs. Reasoning:** This question *purely* tests recall of a formula. There's no complex reasoning, multi-step problem, or non-standard shape involved. It's a direct application.
*   **Specificity:** The required answer is very specific – using the formula `Area = 1/2 × base × height`. Even if they forget `1/2` but multiply `base × height`, it's not full marks. The unit `cm^2` is also important, though the rubric focuses on the formula itself.
*   **Common Mistakes:**
    *   **Forgetting `1/2`:** This is *very* common, especially for struggling students who might confuse it with the area of a rectangle.
    *   **Incorrect multiplication:** Simple arithmetic errors can happen.
    *   **Incorrect units:** They might write `cm` instead of `cm^2` (though the rubric doesn't penalize this directly for the formula).
    *   **Blank:** Some struggling students might just leave it blank if they don't recall the formula at all.
    *   **Writing the formula but not applying:** Some might just write "Area = 1/2 * b * h" and stop, forgetting to substitute. The rubric says "States *or uses*". So stating it *and then substituting* correctly would fulfill "uses." Stating and stopping without substitution for the *given* problem might not be considered "using." Let's assume "uses" implies successful substitution and calculation.
