**Analysis of the Question and Challenges:**

*   **"Describes"**: This is an instruction, but understanding "describe" in an academic context can be tricky. They might think of "describe the story" not "describe the *style*".
*   **"poet's"**: They might struggle to link "poet" to the specific text if it's not explicitly stated. Sometimes they forget who wrote the poem.
*   **"writing style"**: This is the trickiest part. It's an abstract concept. For many Hindi-medium students, translating "writing style" into something meaningful in English is a major hurdle. They might confuse it with the *topic* of the poem or the *mood*.
    *   *Language Barrier*: Many will not know what "style" means in this context. They might have a Hindi word for it, but connecting it to the expected English vocabulary (simple, descriptive, vivid, lyrical) is tough.
    *   *Vocabulary:* Even if they understand "style," knowing the right English adjectives to describe it is a barrier. "Simple" might be the most accessible, but even that requires analysis.
    *   *Reasoning:* This requires them to analyze the text's *form and language use*, not just its content. This is a higher-order thinking skill than just recalling facts from the poem.
    *   *Specificity*: "A suitable and relevant word" means they need to pick an appropriate adjective, not a phrase.

**Common Mistakes My Students Make:**

1.  **Describing the content/theme:** "He writes about trees," or "The poet writes about nature."
2.  **Describing their feelings:** "The poem makes me happy."
3.  **Using simple evaluation:** "The writing is good," or "It is a nice poem."
4.  **Leaving it blank:** If they don't understand "writing style."
5.  **Giving very general answers not related to style:** "He uses words." (Which is true, but not helpful!)
6.  **Writing in Hindi:** If they are truly lost.
