This is a classic question from the Social Science curriculum. Given the student demographics and learning levels, here's my assessment:

**struggling: 0% full marks**
Even if they vaguely remember agriculture or farming, they'll struggle with the second part (describing one in brief) due to language barriers and inability to formulate sentences in English. They might just write one word if they manage to decipher the question.

**basic: 20% full marks**
Many will likely identify 'agriculture' or 'kheti' (Hindi for farming). A smaller subset might also mention 'pashupalan' (Hindi for animal husbandry). The challenge will be "describing any one in brief". Some might manage a very basic sentence like "farmers grow crops" but it won't be elaborated much. Only a few in this group would accurately articulate two occupations and provide a minimal description due to language limitations and lack of practice in structured writing.

**competent: 70% full marks**
Most of these students would recall both Agriculture and Animal Husbandry, as these are foundational topics. They'd also be able to frame at least a couple of simple sentences to describe one of them, like "In agriculture, people grow wheat, bajra, and other crops to earn money." They might even add a detail about irrigation. Their descriptions might still be brief, but would meet the "in brief" criterion.

**advanced: 90% full marks**
These students would confidently list agriculture and animal husbandry. They might even include handicrafts or tourism. They would have no difficulty describing one clearly and concisely, potentially adding specific examples relevant to Rajasthan like bajra or camels. Their language proficiency allows them to formulate complete and coherent answers.

---

**Reasoning behind the percentages:**

*   **Language Barrier:** This is the biggest hurdle for struggling and basic students. The question itself, "What are the main occupations... Describe any one in brief," contains several key English words that can trip them up (main, occupations, describe, brief). If they can't fully understand the question, they can't answer it.
*   **Recall vs. Reasoning:** This is primarily a recall question. They need to remember facts taught in class. This is usually easier than reasoning for all groups, but the **articulation** of the recalled fact is where the gap widens with language proficiency.
*   **Specificity of the Required Answer:** Naming *two* and *describing one in brief* increases the difficulty from just listing one word. The "describing in brief" part is a significant jump in demand, requiring sentence formation and some factual elaboration.
*   **Common Mistakes:**
    *   **Only listing one occupation:** Students, especially those struggling with reading the full question, might just give the first occupation they remember (usually agriculture).
    *   **Inability to describe:** Even if they know the occupation, framing sentences in English to describe it is a major challenge for many. They might write isolated words or very broken sentences.
    *   **Generalizing too much:** Some might give occupations without specifically linking them to Rajasthan or rural areas, although the context helps.
    *   **Vocabulary issues:** They might know "kheti" but not "agriculture," or "pashu palna" but not "animal husbandry." If the question requires English terms, it's an added barrier.

My teaching strategy for such questions usually involves repeated revisions, using visuals, and practicing sentence formation in simple English/Hindi mixed. We'd often practice answering such questions orally first. Even with that, the written articulation in English remains a struggle for the lower two groups.
