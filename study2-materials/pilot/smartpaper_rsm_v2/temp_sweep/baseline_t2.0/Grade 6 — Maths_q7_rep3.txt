**Basic: 10% full marks**

*   **Language Barrier:** A few students at this level might be able to piece together enough of the English (especially if "temperature" and "increase" are known from prior lessons) to *sense* it's an addition problem involving 2. However, understanding "per hour" and calculating the time duration will be very challenging.
*   **Reasoning:** The concept of calculating elapsed time (4:00 pm - 10:00 am) is still tricky. Some might correctly calculate it if they mentally tick through the hours (11, 12, 1, 2, 3, 4), but it's not a given. They're more likely to stumble here.
*   **Common Mistakes:**
    *   Getting the time difference wrong (e.g., 4 hours, or just focusing on the number '4').
    *   Multiplying 30 by 2.
    *   Simply adding 30+2, ignoring the time aspect.
    *   Even if they somehow get 6 hours, they might just say 30+6, not realizing to multiply by 2.

**Competent: 60% full marks**

*   **Language Barrier:** These students have a decent grasp of basic English. They will understand 'temperature', 'increases by', 'per hour'. "10 am" and "4 pm" are common time expressions they'd know.
*   **Reasoning:** They are generally capable of calculating elapsed time from 10 am to 4 pm correctly, often by mentally counting or using simple subtraction. They would then recognize the need to multiply the increase per hour by the number of hours.
*   **Common Mistakes:**
    *   The primary mistake here would be a mental miscount of the hours, perhaps arriving at 5 or 7 hours instead of 6.
    *   A very small number might forget to multiply the 6 hours by the 2-degree increase and just add 6 to 30.

**Advanced: 95% full marks**

*   **Language Barrier:** No issue at all. They'll understand the entire question perfectly.
*   **Reasoning:** This is a fairly straightforward multi-step problem for them. They will easily calculate the 6 hours and then multiply it by 2 to find the total increase, finally adding it to the initial temperature.
*   **Common Mistakes:** A very rare careless error in calculation (e.g., 6 x 2 = 10, or 30 + 12 = 40) is the only reason one might lose a mark, but it's unlikely to happen with the specific instruction of just calculating "time elapsed." Given the rubric *specifically* asks only for "Calculates the time elapsed... as 6 hours," they will almost certainly get that one part correct. The subsequent calculations for the final answer are not part of *this specific rubric*.
