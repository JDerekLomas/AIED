**Common Mistakes & Challenges Across Groups:**

1.  **Language Barrier (Major Issue for Struggling & Basic):**
    *   The term "ascending order" is a direct translation issue. While we use it in class and illustrate it, in the heat of a test, students might forget its meaning, especially if they are primarily thinking in Hindi. Many will rely on the Hindi word *badhte kram* or *chote se bada*. If they don't grasp "ascending order" quickly, they might arrange randomly or descending.
    *   Reading four-digit numbers accurately in English can be a small hurdle for some. While they know numbers, recognizing them written out can be slow.

2.  **Place Value Confusion (Struggling & Basic):**
    *   Many will primarily look at the first digit. For example, 736 vs 3367 â€“ they will intuitively know 736 is smaller because it has fewer digits, but when comparing 3367 and 4799, they'll just look at '3' and '4'. This is fine until numbers have the same number of digits.
    *   The outlier "736" is key. Students might quickly identify it as the smallest because it's the only three-digit number. This makes the *first part* of the ascending order relatively easy.

3.  **Accuracy and Sequence (All Groups, but more for Struggling/Basic):**
    *   Even if they understand ascending order, a slip in writing or picking the *next* number can lead to errors.
    *   Distraction: They might correctly pick 736, but then get confused between 3367 and 4799, putting 4799 next, then correcting themselves, or making a different error.

4.  **Recall vs. Reasoning:**
    *   This problem primarily requires reasoning based on their understanding of number value and place value. "Ascending order" is a term they need to recall, but the core task is number comparison. This is less about memorizing a fact and more about applying a concept.
