Okay, this is a very practical question for my Class 7 English students. Let's break it down.

**Analysis of the Question and Rubric:**

*   **Question:** "Do you think the king's questions are important in real life? Give one reason."
*   **King's Questions (from the passage, implicit knowledge needed):**
    1.  What is the right time to begin everything?
    2.  Who are the right people to listen to?
    3.  What is the most important thing to do?
*   **Scoring Rubric:** "The king wanted to answer (based on the reading passage) the right time to do everything, the right thing to do, and the right people to work with. Any valid reason. Max score: 1"

**Thinking Process for Each Group:**

*   **Language Barriers:** This is huge. "Important in real life" is a phrase they might *understand* conceptually if explained, but reading it themselves and then formulating an answer in English will be a struggle. Many will just pick up keywords.
*   **Recall vs. Reasoning:**
    *   **Recall:** They need to remember *what* the king's questions were about (right time, right people, right thing). This is a foundational recall task from the reading.
    *   **Reasoning:** They then need to apply a *personal opinion* ("Do *you* think...") and provide a *valid reason*. This is a step beyond simple recall and requires critical thinking, even if basic.
*   **Specificity of Answer:** "Any valid reason" makes it quite open-ended, which is good. It doesn't demand a specific academic point, just common sense. However, they need to *express* that reason in English.
*   **Common Mistakes:**
    *   Misunderstanding "important in real life" or not connecting it to their own lives.
    *   Struggling to articulate *any* reason in simple English.
    *   Just saying "Yes" without a reason.
    *   Writing down unrelated sentences due to confusion.
    *   Copying a sentence from the passage that doesn't answer *this* specific question.

---

**Percentage of Students Scoring Full Marks:**

*   **struggling: 5% full marks**
    *   *Reasoning:* These students often cannot read the question fully or understand complex vocabulary. They might pick up "king" and "questions" and write something entirely irrelevant, or just "Yes." Even if they grasp the gist, articulating *why* in English is a massive hurdle. Only the very few who either perfectly grasp the spoken question/explanation *and* can form a simple, short sentence like "Yes, because it helps us" might get it.

*   **basic: 20% full marks**
    *   *Reasoning:* They can generally read the question but might misinterpret parts. They likely remember the king's questions from the passage. The challenge is connecting "important in real life" to the king's specific inquiries and then formulating a simple reason in English. Some might say "Yes, it is important," but fail to give a valid reason. Those who manage a simple, direct reason like "Yes, because we need to know what to do" or "Yes, for good decisions" would score.

*   **competent: 70% full marks**
    *   *Reasoning:* These students can read and comprehend the question fairly well. They will recall the king's questions easily. They understand the concept of "important in real life" and can connect it to the benefits of knowing the right time, people, and actions. They can usually articulate a simple, valid reason, even if it's short, like "Yes, because it helps us make correct choices" or "Yes, so we don't waste time."

*   **advanced: 95% full marks**
    *   *Reasoning:* They will understand the question thoroughly and recall the king's core dilemmas. They'll have no trouble formulating a coherent and valid reason in English. They might even offer slightly more sophisticated reasons, such as "Yes, because knowing the right time, people, and task is fundamental for success in any endeavor," or "It helps us avoid mistakes and lead a purposeful life." The 5% loss might be due to careless error or misinterpretation of "real life" if they overthink it.
