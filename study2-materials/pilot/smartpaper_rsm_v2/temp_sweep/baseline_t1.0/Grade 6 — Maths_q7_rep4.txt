**Language barriers:** The biggest hurdle here will be the English language. "Temperature," "increases," "per hour," "elapsed," "what will be" – these terms can be tricky. I'd definitely be translating or explaining these words in Hindi. The numerical part (10 am, 4 pm, 30°C, 2°C) is universally understood, which helps.

**Recall vs. Reasoning:** This question primarily requires reasoning, not just recall of a formula. Students need to:
1.  Understand the concept of time passing and calculating the duration.
2.  Understand the concept of repeated addition or multiplication based on a rate.
3.  Add the increase to the initial temperature.
The calculation for time elapsed (10 am to 4 pm) is a common one, but not everyone can do it quickly or accurately.

**Specificity of the required answer:** The "6 hours" is a very specific intermediate step. While some might get the final answer without explicitly writing "6 hours," the rubric focuses on that specific step. This means if they jump straight to multiplying by some other number, they won't get full marks for *this specific step*.

**Common mistakes:**
*   **Time calculation:** Many students struggle with time calculations, especially crossing noon (e.g., 10 am to 12 pm is 2 hours, then 12 pm to 4 pm is 4 hours, total 6 hours). Some might just subtract 10 from 4 directly, leading to a negative or incorrect answer if they don't adjust for am/pm. Some might count on their fingers but miscount.
*   **Misinterpreting "per hour":** Some might just add 2°C once, or multiply 30 by 2.
*   **Ignoring initial temperature:** Some might just calculate the increase but forget to add it to the starting temperature.
*   **Language confusion:** As mentioned, not understanding key terms will lead to a complete misunderstanding of the problem.
*   **Calculation errors:** Simple addition or multiplication mistakes.
