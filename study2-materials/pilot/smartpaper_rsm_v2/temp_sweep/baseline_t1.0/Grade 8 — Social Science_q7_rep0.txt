**Analysis of the Question and Rubric:**

*   **Question:** "How did the Battle of Plassey in 1757 contribute to the expansion of British power in India?"
*   **Rubric:** "For explaining that the victory at Plassey led to the establishment of British political dominance/control over Bengal (e.g., placing Mir Jafar as a puppet nawab)."
*   **Key Information Required:**
    *   Recognition of "Battle of Plassey" and "1757".
    *   Understanding the *consequence* (contribution to British power).
    *   Specific outcome: British political dominance/control in Bengal, ideally mentioning Mir Jafar as a puppet.
*   **Language Barrier:** The question is in English. While "Battle of Plassey" and "1757" are likely recognized, "contribute to the expansion of British power" and "political dominance/control" are higher-level English terms. Students might grasp "British power badha" (British power increased) but struggle with the precise nuance of "political dominance."
*   **Recall vs. Reasoning:** This is primarily recall. Students need to remember the direct consequence of Plassey as taught in class. There's an element of connecting cause (victory) to effect (dominance), but the specific effect is memorized.
*   **Specificity:** The required answer is quite specific â€“ not just "British won" but *how* it contributed: political dominance *in Bengal* and the puppet nawab. This is a crucial distinction.
*   **Common Mistakes:**
    *   **Focusing on the *why* instead of the *how*:** Some might explain *why* the British fought (trade, resources).
    *   **Generalizing:** "British became powerful" without specifying *how* or *where*.
    *   **Not mentioning Bengal:** Just saying "in India" generally.
    *   **Not mentioning Mir Jafar/puppet ruler:** This is a key detail that shows understanding of *political* dominance.
    *   **Language issues:** Understanding the question fully, and then formulating an answer in English even if they know the facts in Hindi. Many will attempt a Hindi translation in their head and then try to write it in broken English.
