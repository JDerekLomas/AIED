**Thought Process:**

*   **Language Barrier:** The biggest hurdle for a significant portion of my students will be understanding the English text. "Area," "triangle," "base," "height," "cm" might be recognized, but putting it all together to understand the *question* is tough. Many will latch onto the numbers 8 and 6.
*   **Recall vs. Reasoning:** This question is purely recall-based. They either remember the formula or they don't. There's no complex reasoning involved beyond substituting values.
*   **Specific Answer:** The formula is very specific. No room for approximation or alternative methods.
*   **Common Mistakes:**
    *   **Multiplication only:** Many will just multiply 8 x 6 = 48. They'll forget the 1/2.
    *   **Addition:** A few might add 8 + 6 = 14.
    *   **Incorrect Formula:** Some might use the rectangle/square area formula (base x height) without the 1/2.
    *   **Units:** Even if they get the number right, they might forget "cmÂ²" or just write "cm". (Though the rubric only cares about the formula, so this is secondary for *this* specific scoring point).

    *   **English Comprehension:** This is paramount. If they don't understand "Find the area of a triangle," they're lost from the start.
