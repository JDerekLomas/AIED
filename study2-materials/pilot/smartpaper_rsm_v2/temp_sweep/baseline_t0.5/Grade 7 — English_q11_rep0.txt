**Scenario 1: Students are asked to identify an "adjective phrase" and understand what that means.**

*   An adjective phrase modifies a noun or pronoun and consists of an adjective and any words that modify it. In "A tiny bird built its nest in our balcony," "tiny" is an adjective, but it's not an adjective *phrase* on its own. A simple adjective like "tiny" is sometimes considered an adjective phrase of one word, but typically, an adjective phrase has more than one word (e.g., "very tiny," "tiny and brown"). Given the context of Class 7 and the usual curriculum, students are more likely to be taught adjective phrases as multi-word units.

*   If students are strictly looking for a multi-word adjective phrase, they might struggle to find one in this sentence, or they might incorrectly identify "in our balcony" (prepositional phrase acting adjectivally) or "A tiny bird" (noun phrase).
