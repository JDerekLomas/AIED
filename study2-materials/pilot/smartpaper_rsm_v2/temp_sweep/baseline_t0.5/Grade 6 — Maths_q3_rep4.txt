* **Language barrier:** This is the biggest hurdle. Many struggling students will not be able to read the numbers correctly in English, let alone the instruction "Arrange in ascending order." They might recognize the digits but not the place value if presented as words.
* **Recall vs. Reasoning:** While arranging numbers is a basic reasoning skill, the language barrier will prevent them from even attempting it. If they somehow understand the task (perhaps through a teacher's translation), they might still struggle with comparing numbers beyond three digits.
* **Common mistakes:** Misreading numbers, confusing ascending with descending, or simply not understanding the task.

**basic: 40% full marks**
* **Language barrier:** A significant portion of these students might be able to decipher "ascending order" with some effort or prior exposure to the term. They might recognize the numbers.
* **Recall vs. Reasoning:** This is a core skill taught at this level. They have been exposed to comparing and ordering numbers up to thousands.
* **Common mistakes:** Some might still confuse ascending/descending. A few might make a small error in placing one of the middle numbers, but most would get the smallest number correct.

**competent: 85% full marks**
* **Language barrier:** Most competent students would be able to read and understand the question without much difficulty.
* **Recall vs. Reasoning:** This is a straightforward application of number comparison skills they have mastered.
* **Common mistakes:** Very few, perhaps a momentary lapse in concentration leading to a minor error, but generally, they would get this correct.

**advanced: 95% full marks**
* **Language barrier:** No issue here.
* **Recall vs. Reasoning:** This would be very easy for them. They would likely solve it quickly and accurately.
* **Common mistakes:** Extremely rare, perhaps a silly mistake due to overconfidence or rushing, but highly unlikely for this simple task.
