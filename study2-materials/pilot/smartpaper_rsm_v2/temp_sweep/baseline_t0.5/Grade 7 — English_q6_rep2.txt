This is a challenging question for most of my students, especially given the context of a Class 7 English student from an economically weaker section and a Hindi-medium background.

Here's my breakdown:

**Struggling (35% of students):**
*   **Language barriers:** Many will not understand "describes," "poet's," "writing style," or "suitable and relevant word." They might pick out random words from the poem or simply stare blankly.
*   **Recall vs. reasoning:** This requires reasoning and understanding of literary terms, which is far beyond their current grasp. They haven't been explicitly taught to identify "writing style" in this nuanced way.
*   **Specificity:** The answer needs to be a specific *type* of word (adjective describing style), which is an abstract concept for them.
*   **Common mistakes:** Guessing, copying lines from the poem, writing "good" or "nice," or leaving it blank.

**struggling: 0% full marks** (It's highly improbable any of them would get this right without significant scaffolding and prior teaching on this specific concept.)

**Basic (35% of students):**
*   **Language barriers:** They might understand "poet" and "writing," but "style" and "describes" could still be problematic. They might interpret "writing style" as "what the poet writes about."
*   **Recall vs. reasoning:** They might be able to identify *some* features of the poem (e.g., it tells a story, it uses simple words), but translating that into a single, appropriate adjective for "writing style" is difficult. They haven't been formally taught literary analysis terms.
*   **Specificity:** They might offer a descriptive phrase rather than a single word, or a word that describes the *content* rather than the *style*.
*   **Common mistakes:** "The poet writes about nature," "The poet uses easy words," "The poet tells a story," "Good writing." They might get *close* to "simple" but struggle to articulate it as a *style*.

**basic: 5% full marks** (A very small number might stumble upon "simple" if the poem is indeed very straightforward, or if they've heard the term "simple language" before in a different context and apply it here. It would be more luck than deep understanding.)

**Competent (20% of students):**
*   **Language barriers:** They would likely understand the question.
*   **Recall vs. reasoning:** This group has a better chance of reasoning. They might be able to pick up on the simplicity of language, the use of imagery, or the directness of the message. They might have a basic vocabulary of descriptive adjectives.
*   **Specificity:** They are more likely to attempt a single adjective.
*   **Common mistakes:** They might choose a word that describes the *tone* or *theme* rather than the *style*. For example, if the poem is sad, they might say "sad writing" instead of "descriptive" or "evocative." They might also use a general positive adjective like "beautiful." However, some might correctly identify "simple," "descriptive," or "clear."

**competent: 40% full marks** (This group has the potential. If the poem is genuinely simple and direct, many would be able to identify "simple" or "direct." If it's very visual, some might get "descriptive." They have enough English vocabulary and comprehension to attempt this with some success.)

**Advanced (10% of students):**
*   **Language barriers:** No issue with understanding the question.
*   **Recall vs. reasoning:** They would be able to analyze the poem's structure, vocabulary, and overall presentation to deduce a suitable adjective. They might have a broader vocabulary of literary terms or descriptive adjectives.
*   **Specificity:** They are most likely to provide a precise and appropriate single word.
*   **Common mistakes:** Even here, they might occasionally lean towards tone or theme if not careful, but they would quickly self-correct or offer a more nuanced answer. They would likely pick up on "simple," "descriptive," "evocative," "direct," "narrative," etc., depending on the poem.

**advanced: 80% full marks** (This group is most equipped for this. They have the language skills, the reasoning ability, and a wider vocabulary to choose from. The remaining 20% might make a minor error in choosing the *most* appropriate word or misinterpret a subtle aspect of the style.)

**Summary:**

struggling: 0% full marks
basic: 5% full marks
competent: 40% full marks
advanced: 80
