**struggling: 0% full marks**
* **Language barrier:** The English is too complex. "Collected waste," "pit," "notice any change," "beneficial for soil health" â€“ these phrases would be difficult to fully grasp. They might pick up on "waste" and "pit" but miss the nuance of "change" and "beneficial."
* **Recall vs. Reasoning:** This requires both recall (knowledge of decomposition) and reasoning (applying it to soil health). Struggling students often lack both.
* **Common mistakes:** They might simply say "yes" or "no" without any reason, or focus on the smell without connecting it to decomposition or soil health. They wouldn't use terms like "decomposition" or "manure."

**basic: 10% full marks**
* **Language barrier:** They might understand the core idea of "waste" and "pit" and "change," but "beneficial for soil health" might still be a stretch. They'd need some prompting or simpler language.
* **Recall vs. Reasoning:** They might recall that waste rots or smells bad, but connecting it to "manure" or "fertilizer" and then to "soil health" is a multi-step reasoning process that many would struggle to complete fully.
* **Common mistakes:** They might say "yes, it will smell bad" or "yes, it will rot." They might mention "khad" (manure) but struggle to explain *why* it's beneficial for soil health in a complete sentence. They might miss the "give reasons" part or provide very vague reasons.

**competent: 50% full marks**
* **Language barrier:** They would likely understand the question, possibly with a little re-reading or translating key terms in their head.
* **Recall vs. Reasoning:** They would recall the concept of decomposition and its products (manure/compost). They would be able to reason that this is good for soil.
* **Common mistakes:** They might give a good answer for the "change" and "reasons" but might not explicitly state *how* it's beneficial for soil health beyond "it makes the soil better." They might not use scientific terms like "nutrients" or "fertility." They might miss the full depth required for "evaluate if this is beneficial."

**advanced: 90% full marks**
* **Language barrier:** No significant barrier. They would understand the question fully.
* **Recall vs. Reasoning:** They would easily recall decomposition, microorganisms, and the formation of manure/compost. They would articulate clearly how this enriches the soil with nutrients, improves its structure, and enhances water retention, directly addressing the "beneficial for soil health" part.
* **Common mistakes:** A very small percentage might miss a specific detail in the reasoning or evaluation, or their English phrasing might not be perfectly articulate, but they would grasp the core concepts and provide a comprehensive answer. They would likely use terms like "microorganisms," "nutrients," "humus," and "soil fertility."
