**Thinking Process:**

"Collected waste," "pit," "notice any change," "reasons," "evaluate," "beneficial for soil health" are all terms that might be challenging for the struggling and even some basic students. I'd likely have to read it aloud and explain key terms in Hindi.

    *   "Does gardener notice any change?" - Requires recall of decomposition or observation. Most students would have seen this happen at home or in their surroundings (e.g., fruit rotting).
    *   "Give reasons" - Requires recall of decomposition by microorganisms. This is a core concept taught in Class 8 (Microorganisms: Friend and Foe).
    *   "Evaluate if this is beneficial for soil health" - Requires reasoning based on the output of decomposition (manure/compost) and its role in enriching soil. This is also covered.

3.  **Specificity of Answer:**
    *   "Significant changes" - Yes, they need to state decomposition.
    *   Reasons: Microorganisms, decomposition.
    *   Beneficial: Yes, manure, nutrients, soil health.
    The answer needs to connect these three points clearly.

        *   Confusing it with burning or just throwing away.

        *   Grammar and sentence structure issues in English.
    *   **Competent:**
        *   Generally good at identifying all parts. Might miss a specific detail or struggle with precise scientific terminology in English.
        *   Could explain decomposition and its benefits well.
    *   **Advanced:**
        *   Should be able to articulate the entire process clearly, using appropriate scientific terms.
