Okay, let's break this down based on my experience with Class 6 students in a government school, keeping all the factors in mind.

**Analysis of the Question:**

*   **Language Barrier:** This is a significant hurdle. "Temperature," "increases by," "per hour," "elapsed" (even though not explicitly asked for, it's the underlying concept) are all English terms. Many students, especially those from Hindi-medium backgrounds, will struggle to fully comprehend the question, even if they know the individual words. The concept of "degrees Celsius" might be familiar, but the phrasing "increases by 2°C per hour" can be tricky.
*   **Recall vs. Reasoning:** This question primarily requires reasoning.
    *   **Step 1 (Reasoning):** Calculate the time difference between 10:00 am and 4:00 pm. This involves understanding time on a 12-hour clock and potentially converting pm to "afternoon" in their minds.
    *   **Step 2 (Reasoning/Application):** Multiply the time difference by the rate of increase (2°C/hour).
    *   **Step 3 (Reasoning/Application):** Add this total increase to the initial temperature.
*   **Specificity of Answer:** The required answer is a single numerical value (the final temperature). The rubric, however, specifically focuses *only* on calculating the time elapsed. This is a crucial distinction. If the rubric was about the *final answer*, the percentages would be even lower.
*   **Common Mistakes:**
    *   **Time Calculation:**
        *   Counting on fingers incorrectly (e.g., 10 to 11 is 1 hour, but some might count 10 as 1st hour).
        *   Confusion with am/pm, especially when crossing noon. Some might just subtract 10 from 4, getting -6 or 6 without understanding the time context.
        *   Thinking 4 pm is 16:00 and then calculating 16-10 = 6 hours. This is a valid method if they know 24-hour time, but many won't.
        *   Simply subtracting 10 from 4 (4-10 = -6 or 6, ignoring am/pm).
    *   **Multiplication/Addition:** Even if they get the time correct, some might forget to multiply by 2, or forget to add to the initial temperature.
    *   **Understanding "per hour":** Some might just add 2°C once, or add 2°C for *each* hour (e.g., 30+2+2+2+2+2+2).
    *   **Language Misinterpretation:** "Increases by 2°C per hour" could be misunderstood as "temperature becomes 2°C every hour" or some other incorrect interpretation.

**Scoring Rubric Focus: Calculates the time elapsed from 10:00 am to 4:00 pm as 6 hours.**

This specific rubric simplifies the task significantly, as it only focuses on the first major step of the problem. It removes the hurdles of multiplication and addition, and the final answer. However, the language barrier for *understanding* that they need to find the time elapsed is still present.
