**Thinking about the Scoring Rubric:** "Calculates the time elapsed from 10:00 am to 4:00 pm as 6 hours." This is *very* specific. If they get any other number for the time elapsed, even if they do everything else correctly, they wouldn't get full marks on *this specific rubric point*.

Here's my percentage breakdown:

**struggling:** 5% full marks
*   Even with my assistance, the multi-step nature (time calculation, then multiplication, then addition) is too much.
*   The time calculation from am to pm is a significant cognitive load for them. They often struggle with elapsed time problems.
*   Language barrier will be almost insurmountable for many in this group even with some translation.

**basic:** 25% full marks
*   Many in this group will struggle with the *am/pm* time calculation. They might get 4 hours or 7 hours (10 to 12 = 2, then 1,2,3,4 = 4, so 2+4=6, but sometimes they get confused at the 12 o'clock transition).
*   If I translate and guide them through the time calculation, some might get it.
*   They understand "increases by" and "per hour" if explained, but putting it all together independently is hard.

**competent:** 60% full marks
*   This group has a better grasp of time and basic arithmetic.
*   The language might still be a slight hurdle, but they can usually infer or understand with minimal translation.
*   They are more likely to correctly calculate the elapsed time (10, 11, 12, 1, 2, 3, 4 - count 6 fingers).
*   The multi-step problem solving is within their capabilities. Some might still make a small calculation error, but many will get the elapsed time correct.

**advanced:** 85% full marks
*   These students are generally strong in problem-solving and can handle multi-step problems.
*   While English might not be their first language, they can typically comprehend this level of problem statement.
*   They are very likely to correctly calculate the elapsed time (6 hours) either by mental math or a quick count.
*   Their main risk would be a silly arithmetic error or misreading a number, but the logic of the time calculation is usually sound for them.
