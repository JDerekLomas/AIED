The question is: "Choose the correct word. There are (some/any) apples in the basket."

**Analysis for each group:**

*   **Struggling (35%):**
    *   **Language barriers:** Many would struggle to read the sentence fluently, let alone understand the nuance of 'some' vs. 'any'. The word "choose" might even be a stumbling block. They might only pick up on "apples" and "basket".
    *   **Recall vs. Reasoning:** This requires a specific grammatical rule recall (some for positive statements, any for negative/questions) and applying it. They likely haven't firmly grasped this rule.
    *   **Common mistakes:** They might pick one randomly, or choose 'any' because it's a shorter word, or because they've heard it in other contexts (e.g., "Is there any...?"). They might just copy what their neighbour wrote if they get a chance.

*   **Basic (35%):**
    *   **Language barriers:** They can likely read the sentence, though perhaps slowly. They might understand "apples" and "basket" but the core grammatical difference between 'some' and 'any' is still shaky.
    *   **Recall vs. Reasoning:** They might have been introduced to the rule but haven't practiced it enough for it to be automatic. They might try to reason it out, but could easily get confused, especially if they try to translate directly from Hindi which doesn't have such a clear distinction for this context.
    *   **Common mistakes:** They might guess based on what sounds "more natural" to them, which isn't always correct. They might remember that 'any' is used in questions or negatives and incorrectly apply that to this positive statement, or just make an educated guess.

*   **Competent (20%):**
    *   **Language barriers:** No significant barrier here. They can read and understand the sentence.
    *   **Recall vs. Reasoning:** They have likely been taught this rule and have practiced it. They can recall the rule: 'some' for positive statements, 'any' for questions and negative statements.
    *   **Common mistakes:** Very few. The most common might be a momentary lapse or overthinking if they haven't seen the specific "there are" structure recently, but generally, they should get this right.

*   **Advanced (10%):**
    *   **Language barriers:** No barrier.
    *   **Recall vs. Reasoning:** This is a straightforward application of a basic grammatical rule for them. They will recall it instantly.
    *   **Common mistakes:** Practically none. This is a very simple concept for their level.
