## Step 1: Understanding the Question
The question asks students to estimate the gradient of a curve at a specific point \( t = 6 \) seconds, given a velocity-time graph of a parachutist. The graph provides a tangent line at \( t = 6 \) that passes through the points \( (2, 31) \), \( (6, 47) \), and \( (8, 55) \). The gradient of the curve at \( t = 6 \) is essentially the gradient of this tangent line.

## 2: Identifying the Gradient Calculation
To find the gradient of the tangent line (and thus the curve at \( t = 6 \)), we can use any two points on the line. The formula for gradient is \( \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} \). Using points \( (2, 31) \) and \( (6, 47) \):
\[ \text{Gradient} = \frac{47 - 31}{6 - 2} = \frac{16}{4} = 4 \]
Alternatively, using points \( (6, 47) \) and \( (8, 55) \):
\[ \text{Gradient} = \frac{55 - 47}{8 - 6} = \frac{8}{2} = 4 \]
Both calculations yield the same result, confirming the gradient is \( 4 \).
