## 2: Analyze the column with the star
Looking at the rightmost column, we have \(2 - \bigstar = 3\). This implies that \(\bigstar = 2 - 3 = -1\). However, since \(\bigstar\) represents a digit in a number, it cannot be negative. This indicates a potential issue because, in standard subtraction, if the top digit is smaller than the bottom digit in a column, borrowing is required.

## 3: Consider borrowing in the column with the star
Given \(2 - \bigstar = 3\), to avoid a negative \(\bigstar\), we must consider borrowing. If we borrow 1 from the next column (which is 5), the 2 becomes 12. So, \(12 - \bigstar = 3\), which means \(\bigstar = 12 - 3 = 9\).
