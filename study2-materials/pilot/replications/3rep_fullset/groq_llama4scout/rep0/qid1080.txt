To approach this question, let's analyze the potential pitfalls and the requirements for rounding to 1 significant figure.

Rounding to 1 significant figure involves looking at the first digit of the number and then checking the second digit. If the second digit is 5 or greater, we round up the first digit; if it's less than 5, we leave the first digit unchanged. The number of significant figures is then reduced to 1, and all other digits are replaced with zeros, or if the number is a decimal, they are dropped.

## Step 1: Understanding the Task
The task is to round \(73975\) to \(1\) significant figure. According to the rules, we look at the first digit (7) and then the second digit (3). Since 3 is less than 5, the first digit remains unchanged.

## 2: Applying the Rounding Rule
The number \(73975\) starts with 7, and since the second digit is 3, which is less than 5, we do not change the 7. Thus, \(73975\) rounded to \(1\) significant figure is \(70000\).
