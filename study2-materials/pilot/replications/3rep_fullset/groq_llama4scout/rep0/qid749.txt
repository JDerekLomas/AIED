## Step 1: Understanding Rounding Rules
Rounding to the nearest ten involves looking at the units digit. If the units digit is 5 or greater, you round up. If it's less than 5, you round down. For \( 989.989 \), the whole number part is 989, and the decimal part is .989. When rounding 989 to the nearest ten, we look at the 9 in the units place.

## 2: Applying Rounding Rules to the Given Number
Since 9 is greater than 5, we round 989 up to 990. The decimal part (.989) does not affect the rounding to the nearest ten of the whole number part. Therefore, \( 989.989 \) rounded to the nearest ten is \( 990 \).
