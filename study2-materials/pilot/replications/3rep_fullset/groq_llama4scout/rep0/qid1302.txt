## Step 1: Understanding the Question
The question presents a table related to a diver's trip, categorized by types of sea creatures encountered: Manta Ray, Hammerhead, and Conger Eel, with their respective frequencies and the degrees they represent in a pie chart. The task is to find a correct method for calculating the degrees for Conger Eel, denoted as \(F\).

## 2: Analyzing the Table
- Manta Ray: 16 encounters, 180 degrees
- Hammerhead: 4 encounters, 45 degrees
- Conger Eel: 12 encounters, \(F\) degrees to be found

## 3: Total Encounters and Total Degrees in a Pie Chart
The total number of encounters is \(16 + 4 + 12 = 32\).
A pie chart represents data using 360 degrees.

## 4: Evaluating Option A
Option A: \(360 - 180 + 45\). This calculation does not take into account the frequency of Conger Eel encounters and seems to subtract and add given degrees rather than calculate \(F\) based on Conger Eel's frequency.

## 5: Evaluating Option B
Option B: \(\frac{12}{20} \times 360\). This option incorrectly uses 20 as the total frequency instead of 32. It should be \(\frac{12}{32} \times 360\) to be correct.

## 6: Evaluating Option C
Option C: \(360 - 180 - 45\). This calculates the remaining degrees after subtracting the degrees for Manta Ray and Hammerhead, which is a valid approach since \(360 - (180 + 45)\) gives the degrees left for Conger Eel.

## 7: Evaluating Option D
Option D: \(\frac{360}{32}\). This option simply divides the total degrees in a pie chart by the total number of encounters but does not account for the specific frequency of Conger Eel encounters.

## 8: Correct Approach
The correct approach to find \(F\) is to understand that the fraction of the total encounters for Conger Eel should equal the fraction of 360 degrees it represents. So, \(F = \frac{12}{32} \times 360\), which simplifies to \(F = 360 - (180 + 45)\) because \(180 + 45 = 225\) degrees are already accounted for, leaving \(360 - 225 = 135\) degrees for Conger Eel.
