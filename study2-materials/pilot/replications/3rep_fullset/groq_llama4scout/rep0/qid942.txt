The question requires students to understand how transformations affect the equation of a quadratic curve. The general form of a quadratic equation is \(y = ax^2 + bx + c\), and when considering transformations, it can be expressed in vertex form as \(y = a(x-h)^2 + k\), where \((h, k)\) is the vertex of the parabola. For the curve \(y = x^2\), translating it \(h\) units to the left and \(k\) units down results in the equation \(y = (x + h)^2 + k\) if \(h\) is positive and \(k\) is negative.

Given the graph is \(y = x^2\) translated 2 squares to the left and 5 squares down, the correct equation should reflect these translations. Moving 2 squares to the left implies \(x\) becomes \(x + 2\), and moving 5 squares down implies subtracting 5 from the result, leading to the equation \(y = (x + 2)^2 - 5\).
