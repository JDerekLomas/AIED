## Step 1: Understand the concept of similar shapes
Similar shapes have the same shape but not necessarily the same size. This means that corresponding angles are equal and the corresponding sides are in proportion.

## 2: Identify the given information
We have two similar trapeziums. The smaller trapezium has a base of 60cm and a top side labeled as \( y \) cm. The larger trapezium has a base of 150cm and a top side of 70cm.

## 3: Apply the concept of proportionality
Since the shapes are similar, the ratios of the lengths of the corresponding sides are equal. We can set up a proportion using the bases and the tops of the trapeziums: \( \frac{60}{150} = \frac{y}{70} \).

## 4: Solve for \( y \)
To find \( y \), we can cross-multiply: \( 60 \times 70 = 150 \times y \), which gives \( 4200 = 150y \). Then, divide both sides by 150 to solve for \( y \): \( y = \frac{4200}{150} = 28 \).
