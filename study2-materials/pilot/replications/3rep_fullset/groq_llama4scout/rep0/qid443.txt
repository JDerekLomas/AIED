## Step 1: Understanding the Question
The question presents a Venn diagram with two sets: one labeled "Square number" and the other labeled "Odd number." The regions are labeled A (Square number not odd), B (intersection of Square and Odd numbers), C (Odd number not square), and D (neither Square nor Odd). The task is to determine in which region the number 36 would belong.

## 2: Identifying Key Concepts
- A square number is a number that can be expressed as the square of an integer. For example, 1, 4, 9, 16, 25, 36 are square numbers because they are the squares of 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6 respectively.
- An odd number is any integer that cannot be exactly divided by 2. For example, 1, 3, 5, 7, 9 are odd numbers.

## 3: Analyzing the Number 36
36 is a square number because 6^2 = 36. It is also an even number because it can be divided by 2 without leaving a remainder.

## 4: Applying Concepts to the Venn Diagram
Given that 36 is a square number but not an odd number, it does not belong in the intersection of square and odd numbers (B) or in the odd number not square category (C). It also does not belong outside both circles (D) since it is a square number.
