To approach this question, let's consider the properties of a rhombus and the common misconceptions students might have.

A rhombus is a type of polygon that has four equal sides. It also has the properties that its diagonals bisect each other at right angles and that the opposite sides are parallel. However, not all rhombuses have right angles (if they do, they are also squares), which means not all adjacent sides are perpendicular.

Given these properties:

- Option A: \( AB \) and \( BC \) are adjacent sides of the rhombus. While in a square (a special type of rhombus) these sides would be perpendicular, in a general rhombus, they are not necessarily perpendicular. Students might mistakenly think they are, especially if they confuse a rhombus with a square.
- Option B: \( AB \) and \( DC \) are opposite sides of the rhombus, and in any parallelogram (which includes rhombuses), opposite sides are parallel, not perpendicular.
- Option C: \( AC \) and \( DB \) are the diagonals of the rhombus. A well-known property of a rhombus is that its diagonals intersect at right angles (are perpendicular).
- Option D: \( None \) of them. This would be correct if none of the pairs of lines mentioned are necessarily perpendicular.

- **basic**: Students here have a better grasp but might still fall into common traps. They are likely to know that opposite sides are parallel but might not recall that diagonals are perpendicular. \( A = 25\% \), \( B = 15\% \), \( C = 40\% \), \( D = 20\% \).
- **proficient**: These students understand the basic properties of a rhombus, including that diagonals are perpendicular. \( A = 10\% \), \( B = 5\% \), \( C = 80\% \), \( D = 5\% \).
- **advanced**: Students at this level not only know the properties but can also apply them accurately. They are less likely to be tricked by misconceptions. \( A = 0\% \), \( B = 0\% \), \( C = 95\% \), \( D = 5\% \).
