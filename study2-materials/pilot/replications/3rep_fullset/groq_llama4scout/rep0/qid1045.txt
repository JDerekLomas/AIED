## 2: Identify the relevant trigonometric ratios and their definitions
- Sin(\( \theta \)) = opposite side / hypotenuse
- Cos(\( \theta \)) = adjacent side / hypotenuse
- Tan(\( \theta \)) = opposite side / adjacent side

Given the sides provided, the 6cm side is the adjacent side to \( \theta \), and we can find the opposite side using the Pythagorean theorem: \( \sqrt{10^2 - 6^2} = \sqrt{100 - 36} = \sqrt{64} = 8 \) cm.

## 3: Determine the correct ratio for finding \( \theta \)
To find \( \theta \), we need a ratio that involves the known sides. Since we have the adjacent side (6cm) and the hypotenuse (10cm), the correct ratio to use is Cos(\( \theta \)) = adjacent side / hypotenuse = 6 / 10.
