## Step 1: Understanding Trigonometric Ratios
- Sine (\( \sin \)) of an angle in a right-angled triangle is defined as the ratio of the length of the side opposite the angle to the length of the hypotenuse.
- Cosine (\( \cos \)) of an angle is defined as the ratio of the length of the side adjacent to the angle to the length of the hypotenuse.
- Tangent (\( \tan \)) of an angle is defined as the ratio of the length of the side opposite the angle to the length of the side adjacent to the angle.

## 2: Identifying the Correct Ratio for \( \theta \)
Given \( \theta \) is the angle opposite the 10cm side, and the adjacent side is 7cm, but the hypotenuse is unknown:
- The sine of \( \theta \) would require the hypotenuse.
- The cosine of \( \theta \) would also require the hypotenuse.
- The tangent of \( \theta \) can be calculated as \( \tan(\theta) = \frac{opposite}{adjacent} = \frac{10}{7} \), which does not require the hypotenuse.
