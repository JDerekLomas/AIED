## Step 1: Understanding the Question
The question presents a simple geometric problem involving parallel lines and a transversal. The task is to calculate the value of \( t \), where \( t \) is the lower interior angle to the right of the transversal. The angle to the right of the transversal and lying above the upper parallel line is given as \( 78^{\circ} \).

## 2: Identifying Relevant Geometric Properties
For parallel lines cut by a transversal, corresponding angles are equal, and interior angles on the same side of the transversal are supplementary (add up to \( 180^{\circ} \)).

## 3: Applying Geometric Properties to Solve for \( t \)
Given that the angle above the upper parallel line and to the right of the transversal is \( 78^{\circ} \), this angle corresponds to \( t \) because they are alternate interior angles, which are equal. However, to find \( t \) using the information given directly: since \( t \) and \( 78^{\circ} \) are supplementary (they form a straight line), we have \( t + 78^{\circ} = 180^{\circ} \). Solving for \( t \) gives \( t = 180^{\circ} - 78^{\circ} = 102^{\circ} \).

## 4: Analyzing Potential Misconceptions and Student Errors
- A common misconception might be to confuse \( t \) with its corresponding angle or to mistakenly think \( t \) and \( 78^{\circ} \) are the same angle, leading to \( t = 78^{\circ} \).
- Another error could be misunderstanding the relationship between angles formed by a transversal and parallel lines.
