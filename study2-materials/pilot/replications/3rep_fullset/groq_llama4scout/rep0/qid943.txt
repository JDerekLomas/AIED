## Step 1: Understanding the concept of perpendicular lines
To determine which line is perpendicular to \(y = 2x + 1\), we need to recall that for two lines to be perpendicular, the product of their slopes must be -1. The slope of the given line \(y = 2x + 1\) is 2.

## 2: Finding the slope of a perpendicular line
The slope of a line perpendicular to \(y = 2x + 1\) must satisfy \(2 \times m = -1\), where \(m\) is the slope of the perpendicular line. Solving for \(m\), we get \(m = -\frac{1}{2}\).
