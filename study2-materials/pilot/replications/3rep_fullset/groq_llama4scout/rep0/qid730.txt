## Step 1: Understanding the Task
The task is to shade \(60\%\) of a shape that is divided into 20 equal parts. To find \(60\%\) of 20, students can calculate \(0.60 \times 20 = 12\). This means 12 parts out of 20 need to be shaded to represent \(60\%\).

- A common misconception might involve confusing the percentage with the number of parts directly or miscalculating the percentage as a fraction of the total parts.
- Another potential error could be not converting the percentage to a decimal correctly or misunderstanding how to apply it to the total number of parts.
