## Step 1: Understanding the Question
The question requires students to find the smallest number of packets of bread rolls and hot dogs that Sophie can buy such that she has exactly the same number of bread rolls as hot dogs. The packets contain 18 bread rolls and 15 hot dogs, respectively.

## 2: Identifying the Mathematical Concept
To solve this, students need to find the smallest number of packets where the total number of bread rolls equals the total number of hot dogs. This translates to finding the lowest common multiple (LCM) of 18 and 15, as they need to find the smallest quantity where 18x = 15y (x and y being the number of packets of bread rolls and hot dogs, respectively).

## 3: Analyzing the Options
- Option A: Product. The product of 18 and 15 would give a common multiple, but not necessarily the smallest.
- Option B: Sum. The sum of 18 and 15 is not relevant to finding equal quantities of bread rolls and hot dogs.
- Option C: Lowest Common Multiple (LCM). This is the correct mathematical concept to find the smallest quantity where the number of bread rolls equals the number of hot dogs.
- Option D: Highest Common Factor (HCF). The HCF would give the greatest common divisor, not the smallest set of packets to achieve equality.
