## Step 1: Understanding the Question
The question presents an irregular quadrilateral with angles labeled as \( s \), \( 2s \), \( 2s \), and \( 3s \). The task is to find the size of angle \( s \).

## 2: Identifying the Key Concept
The key concept here is that the sum of the interior angles of a quadrilateral is \( 360^\circ \). This can be used to solve for \( s \).

## 3: Setting Up the Equation
Given the angles are \( s \), \( 2s \), \( 2s \), and \( 3s \), we can write the equation: \( s + 2s + 2s + 3s = 360^\circ \).

## 4: Solving the Equation
Simplifying the equation gives \( 8s = 360^\circ \). Solving for \( s \) yields \( s = 360^\circ / 8 = 45^\circ \).
