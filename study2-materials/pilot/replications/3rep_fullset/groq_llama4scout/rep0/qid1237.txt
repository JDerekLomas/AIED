## Step 1: Understanding the Question
The question asks students to find the radius of a circle given its diameter is \( 5.4 \mathrm{~cm} \). The formula to find the radius from the diameter is \( \text{radius} = \frac{\text{diameter}}{2} \).

A common misconception might be that the radius is the same as the diameter or that it's double the diameter. However, the relationship between diameter and radius is well-defined and simple: the radius is half the diameter.
