## Step 1: Understanding the Question
The question asks students to identify the correct units for the area of a trapezium given that James has calculated the area to be \( 54 \). The area of a trapezium is calculated using the formula: \( \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times (a + b) \times h \), where \( a \) and \( b \) are the lengths of the parallel sides and \( h \) is the height (the perpendicular distance between the parallel sides). The units of area are typically square units (e.g., \( \mathrm{m}^2 \), \( \mathrm{cm}^2 \), \( \mathrm{mm}^2 \)).
