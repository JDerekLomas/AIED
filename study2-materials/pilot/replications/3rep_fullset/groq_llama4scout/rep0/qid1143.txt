The question presents a circle intersected by curves at 5 equally spaced intervals about its circumference. The task is to determine which regular polygon could be constructed by continuing to join up the intersections of the curves with straight lines.

## Step 1: Understanding the Misconception
The key here is understanding that joining the intersections of the curves (which are essentially the points where the curves meet the circle and each other) with straight lines will form a polygon. For a regular polygon, the number of sides must divide 360 degrees evenly because each interior angle of a regular n-gon is 180(n-2)/n degrees, and the sum of exterior angles is always 360 degrees.

## 2: Identifying Possible Polygons
Given that there are 5 equally spaced intervals, this implies a pentagon (5 sides) because 360 degrees / 5 = 72 degrees. This means each central angle corresponding to a side of the polygon is 72 degrees, which directly corresponds to a regular pentagon.

## 3: Analyzing Option C - Pentagon
Option C, a pentagon, directly matches the number of intervals (5) shown and thus is a strong candidate. A regular pentagon has 5 equal sides and angles, which aligns with the symmetry of the circle divided into 5 equal parts.
