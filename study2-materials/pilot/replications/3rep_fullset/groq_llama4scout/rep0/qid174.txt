## Step 1: Understand the concept of parallel lines in the context of linear equations.
Lines are parallel if they have the same slope but different intercepts. The given line is \(y = 3x + 5\), which means it has a slope of 3.

## 3: Analyze option B.

## 4: Analyze option C.
Option C is \(y = 4 - 3x\). Rewriting it gives \(y = -3x + 4\). This line has a slope of -3, which is different from the slope of the given line. Therefore, it is not parallel to \(y = 3x + 5\).

## 5: Analyze option D.
Option D is \(y = 3(1 + x)\). Expanding it gives \(y = 3 + 3x\) or \(y = 3x + 3\). This line has a slope of 3, making it parallel to \(y = 3x + 5\).
