## Step 1: Understand the question
The question asks for the equation of a line that passes through the coordinates: (-5,7), (0,7), (5,7), and (10,7). This line is clearly horizontal, as all the y-coordinates are the same.

## 2: Recall the general equation of a line
The general equation of a line can be given in several forms, but for a horizontal line, it is \(y = c\), where \(c\) is a constant. For a vertical line, the equation is \(x = c\).

## 3: Identify the correct equation
Given that the line passes through points with the same y-coordinate (7), the equation of this line is \(y = 7\).

## 4: Analyze the options
- A: \(y = 7x\) suggests a line through the origin with a slope of 7, which does not match.
- B: \(x = 7y\) implies a line with a slope of \(\frac{1}{7}\) and a y-intercept of 0, which does not match.
- C: \(y = 7\) matches the line described.
- D: \(x = 7\) suggests a vertical line at \(x = 7\), which does not match.

## 5: Consider common misconceptions and student errors
Students might confuse the equation of a horizontal line with that of a vertical line or mistakenly apply the slope-intercept form (\(y = mx + b\)) incorrectly. However, given the clear visual cue that the line is horizontal and passes through \(y = 7\), and assuming students can read coordinates or understand the concept of a horizontal line, option C should be readily identifiable as correct for students at or above the basic level.
