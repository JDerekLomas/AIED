## Step 1: Understand the question and the sequence given
The question provides two consecutive triangle numbers in a sequence: 120 and 136. The task is to find the next triangle number in the sequence. A triangle number or triangular number counts objects arranged in an equilateral triangle. The nth triangle number is the number of dots in the triangular arrangement with n dots on a side, and it is given by the formula: \( T_n = \frac{n(n + 1)}{2} \).

## 2: Identify the relationship between consecutive triangle numbers
To find the next triangle number after 136, we first need to understand the pattern or difference between consecutive triangle numbers. The difference between consecutive triangle numbers increases by 1 each time because \( T_{n+1} - T_n = \frac{(n+1)(n+2)}{2} - \frac{n(n+1)}{2} = \frac{n^2 + 3n + 2 - n^2 - n}{2} = \frac{2n + 2}{2} = n + 1 \).

## 3: Determine the specific triangle numbers given
Let's denote 120 as \( T_n \) and 136 as \( T_{n+1} \). The difference between \( T_{n+1} \) and \( T_n \) is 16. Using the formula for the difference between consecutive triangle numbers: \( T_{n+1} - T_n = (n + 1) = 16 \), which implies \( n = 15 \). So, 120 is \( T_{15} \) and 136 is \( T_{16} \).

## 4: Calculate the next triangle number
Given that 136 is \( T_{16} \), the next triangle number, \( T_{17} \), can be found using the formula \( T_n = \frac{n(n + 1)}{2} \) with \( n = 17 \): \( T_{17} = \frac{17(17 + 1)}{2} = \frac{17 \times 18}{2} = 153 \).
