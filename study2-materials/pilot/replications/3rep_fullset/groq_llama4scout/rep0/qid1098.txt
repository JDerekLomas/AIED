## Step 1: Understanding the Question
The question requires simplifying an expression involving division and multiplication of fractions, and then choosing the correct simplified form from the given options.

## 2: Identifying the Misconception
A common misconception in algebra is failing to cancel out common factors in the numerator and denominator of fractions. However, the expression given is designed to test if students can correctly apply the rules of dividing by a fraction (which is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal) and then simplifying.

## 3: Simplifying the Expression
The expression to simplify is:
\[
\frac{3x + 1}{3} \div \frac{9x^2 - 1}{12}
\]
This becomes:
\[
\frac{3x + 1}{3} \times \frac{12}{9x^2 - 1}
\]
Given that \(9x^2 - 1 = (3x - 1)(3x + 1)\), the expression simplifies to:
\[
\frac{3x + 1}{3} \times \frac{12}{(3x - 1)(3x + 1)}
\]
Cancelling out the \((3x + 1)\) from the numerator and denominator:
\[
\frac{1}{3} \times \frac{12}{3x - 1} = \frac{12}{3(3x - 1)} = \frac{4}{3x - 1}
\]

## 4: Analyzing the Options
- A: \( \frac{12}{9x - 1} \) - Incorrect because it doesn't account for the factor of 3 in the denominator.
- B: \( \frac{4(3x + 1)}{(3x - 1)(3x + 1)} \) - Incorrect because it doesn't cancel out the \(3x + 1\).
- C: \( \frac{5}{3x} \) - Incorrect as there's no \(5\) or \(3x\) in our simplified expression.
- D: \( \frac{4}{3x - 1} \) - This matches our simplification.
