To approach this question, let's first understand the key terms involved and then consider how students at different ability levels might respond.

- **Equation**: A statement that two mathematical expressions have the same value. Equations can be solved to find the value of a variable.
- **Formula**: A general expression or rule that can be used to describe a relationship between quantities. It often involves variables and is used to compute a value.
- **Identity**: A mathematical statement that is always true, regardless of the values of the variables involved. For example, \(2x + 2 = 2(x + 1)\) is an identity.
- **Expression**: A group of mathematical symbols that represent a value. Expressions do not have an equals sign.

The question provides \(x + 3 = 5\), which is clearly an equation because it states that two expressions have the same value and can be solved for \(x\).
