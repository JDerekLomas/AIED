The question asks to identify which number line represents the inequality \(-1 \leq x < 3\). This means \(x\) is greater than or equal to \(-1\) and less than \(3\).

## Understanding the Inequality
- The inequality \(-1 \leq x < 3\) indicates that \(-1\) is included (so it should be a closed or filled circle) and \(3\) is not included (so it should be an open or unfilled circle).
- The line should extend from \(-1\) to \(3\), but not include \(3\).

## Analyzing the Options
- A: Open circle at \(-1\), filled circle at \(3\). Incorrect because \(-1\) is not included and \(3\) is included.
- B: Filled circle at \(-1\), open circle at \(3\), with an arrow to the right from \(-1\) and to the right from the open circle at \(3\). Correct because \(-1\) is included and \(3\) is not, and it shows \(x\) can be any value in between and beyond \(-1\) up to but not including \(3\).
- C: Open circle at \(-1\), filled circle at \(3\), with a line connecting them. Incorrect because it suggests \(x\) can only be between \(-1\) and \(3\), not beyond \(-1\).
- D: Filled circle at \(-1\), open circle at \(3\), with a line connecting them. Incorrect for similar reasons as C; it does not indicate \(x\) can be less than \(-1\).
