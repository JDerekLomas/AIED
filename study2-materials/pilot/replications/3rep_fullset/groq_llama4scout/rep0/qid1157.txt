## Step 1: Understanding the Question
The question presents a rectangle with labeled sides: left side \(x\), right side \(8\), top side \(2x + 4\), and bottom side \(3x - 4\). The task is to identify the incorrect statement among the given options regarding the area, perimeter, and an equation derived from the sides.

## 2: Analyzing Option A - Area
The area of a rectangle is given by length times width. So, the area \(A = x \times (2x + 4)\) or \(A = 8 \times (3x - 4)\). Let's calculate both to verify option A: \(A = x(2x + 4) = 2x^2 + 4x\) and \(A = 8(3x - 4) = 24x - 32\). Option A states \(A = 3x^2 - 4\), which does not match either calculation, suggesting it could be incorrect based on standard area calculations.

## 3: Analyzing Option B - Perimeter
The perimeter \(P\) of a rectangle is given by \(P = 2 \times (\text{length} + \text{width})\). So, \(P = 2(x + 2x + 4)\) or \(P = 2(8 + 3x - 4)\). Simplifying, \(P = 2(3x + 4) = 6x + 8\). This matches option B, suggesting option B could be correct.

## 4: Analyzing Option C - Equation from Sides
For a rectangle, opposite sides are equal. Thus, \(x = 3x - 4\) or \(2x + 4 = 3x - 4\). Let's examine \(2x + 4 = 3x - 4\): \(2x + 4 = 3x - 4 \Rightarrow 4 + 4 = 3x - 2x \Rightarrow 8 = x\). This implies option C could be a correct equation but let's see if it directly relates to being incorrect as per the question.

## 5: Analyzing Option D - Value of \(x\)
Option D states \(x = 8\). Let's verify if this is consistent with the equations derived from the sides. If \(x = 8\), then the top and bottom sides should be equal when \(x = 8\): Top side \(= 2(8) + 4 = 16 + 4 = 20\), Bottom side \(= 3(8) - 4 = 24 - 4 = 20\). This matches, suggesting \(x = 8\) could indeed be a valid solution.
