## Step 1: Understand the question and the content it tests
The question tests the understanding of fractional indices, specifically \( (a^m)^n = a^{m \cdot n} \) and \( a^{\frac{m}{n}} = \sqrt[n]{a^m} \). For \( (16)^{\frac{5}{2}} \), students need to apply the rule \( a^{\frac{m}{n}} = (\sqrt[n]{a})^m \) or directly calculate \( (16)^{\frac{1}{2}} = 4 \) and then \( 4^5 \).

## 2: Identify potential misconceptions and difficulties
A common misconception could be applying the exponent directly without understanding fractional exponents, or getting confused between \( (16^{\frac{1}{2}})^5 \) and \( (16^5)^{\frac{1}{2}} \). However, the numbers given are powers of 2 and 4, which might help or hinder understanding depending on the student's familiarity with these numbers.
