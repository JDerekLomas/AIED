## Step 1: Understand the question and the equation given
The equation provided is \(y = x^2\). This means that for any value of \(x\), \(y\) will be the square of \(x\). The question asks to find the value of \(y\) when \(x = -9\).

## 2: Calculate the correct value of \(y\) for \(x = -9\)
Substitute \(x = -9\) into the equation \(y = x^2\). So, \(y = (-9)^2 = 81\).

## 3: Analyze the potential misconceptions and errors
A common misconception could be that \(y = x^2\) implies \(y = x \times 2\) or that the square of a negative number is negative, which is not the case. The correct operation is squaring, which for \((-9)^2\) yields a positive result because squaring a negative number always results in a positive number.
