The question asks students to identify the most appropriate word to describe \( A=\frac{b h}{2} \), which represents the area of a triangle with base \(b\) and height \(h\). The options provided are: Equation, Formula, Variable, and Expression.

- **Equation**: Typically refers to a statement that two mathematical expressions have the same value, often with an equal sign but not necessarily solved for a variable. 
- **Formula**: A general expression or rule that can be applied to a specific situation, often derived from more basic principles. 
- **Variable**: A symbol that represents a value that can change.
- **Expression**: A group of mathematical symbols, such as numbers, operations, and variables, that represent a value.

Given that \( A=\frac{b h}{2} \) describes a relationship between variables and is a general rule for calculating the area of a triangle (not a specific numerical equation, and more than just a single variable or a general algebraic expression), the most appropriate term here is **Formula**.
