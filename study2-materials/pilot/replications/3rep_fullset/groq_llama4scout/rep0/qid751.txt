The question requires students to understand that if a darts player hits his target \(40\%\) of the time, then he misses \(60\%\) of the time. This translates to a pie chart where \(40\%\) of the chart represents hits and \(60\%\) represents misses.

## Step 1: Understanding the Question
The question presents four pie charts (A, B, C, D) and asks which one correctly represents a darts player hitting his target \(40\%\) of the time.

## 2: Analyzing Option A
Option A shows hits with \(320\) degrees and misses with \(40\) degrees. Since a full circle is \(360\) degrees, \(320\) degrees is \(\frac{320}{360} \approx 0.889\) or \(88.9\%\), and \(40\) degrees is \(\frac{40}{360} \approx 0.111\) or \(11.1\%\). This does not match \(40\%\) hits and \(60\%\) misses.

## 3: Analyzing Option B
Option B shows hits with \(\frac{2}{3}\) of the chart and misses with \(\frac{1}{3}\). \(\frac{2}{3}\) is approximately \(66.7\%\), and \(\frac{1}{3}\) is approximately \(33.3\%\). This does not match \(40\%\) hits and \(60\%\) misses.

## 4: Analyzing Option C
Option C shows hits with \(320\) degrees and misses with \(40\) degrees, similar to Option A. This means hits are \(88.9\%\) and misses are \(11.1\%\), which does not match.

## 5: Analyzing Option D
