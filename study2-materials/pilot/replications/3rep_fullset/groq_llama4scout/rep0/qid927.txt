## Step 1: Understanding the Question
The question asks students to calculate the mean number of spots per ladybird given a table summarizing the number of spots counted on 20 ladybirds. The table provides the number of spots and their respective frequencies.

## 2: Identifying the Calculation Needed
To find the mean, students need to calculate the total number of spots and then divide by the total number of ladybirds. The total number of spots can be found by multiplying each number of spots by its frequency and then summing these products.

## 3: Calculating the Total Number of Spots
Total spots = (5 * 3) + (6 * 4) + (7 * 12) + (8 * 1) = 15 + 24 + 84 + 8 = 131.

## 4: Calculating the Mean
The total number of ladybirds = 3 + 4 + 12 + 1 = 20.
Mean = Total spots / Total ladybirds = 131 / 20 = 6.55.

## 5: Rounding the Mean
Rounded to 1 decimal place, the mean is 6.6.
