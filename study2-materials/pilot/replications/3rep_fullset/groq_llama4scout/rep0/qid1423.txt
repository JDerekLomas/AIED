## Step 1: Understanding Standard Form
Standard form, also known as scientific notation, is a way of writing very large or very small numbers as \(a \times 10^b\), where \(1 \leq a < 10\) and \(b\) is an integer.

## 2: Analyzing the Given Number
The given number is \(0.0003001\). To convert it into standard form, we need to move the decimal point to the right until we have a number between 1 and 10, then multiply by \(10\) raised to the power of the number of places we moved the decimal point. Since we're moving the decimal point 4 places to the right to get \(3.001\), and the original number is less than 1, the power will be negative.

## 3: Converting to Standard Form
Moving the decimal point 4 places to the right gives us \(3.001\), and since we started with a number less than 1, we use \(10^{-4}\). Therefore, \(0.0003001 = 3.001 \times 10^{-4}\).
