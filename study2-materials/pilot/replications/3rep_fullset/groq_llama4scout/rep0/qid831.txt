## Step 1: Understand the question
The question asks students to identify a factor of the expression \(6p^{2} + 11p\) from given options.

## 2: Analyze the expression
The expression \(6p^{2} + 11p\) can be factored by taking out a common factor. In this case, \(p\) is a common factor, so we can rewrite the expression as \(p(6p + 11)\).

## 3: Evaluate the options
- Option A: \(6\) is not a factor of the expression \(6p^{2} + 11p\) because there's no \(6\) that can be factored out from both terms without \(p\).
- Option B: \(11\) is not a factor in the same sense as \(p\) or \(6p + 11\), but we should consider if it directly relates to the factored form.
- Option C: \(p^{2}\) is not a factor because \(p\) is the highest power of \(p\) that can be factored out from both terms.
- Option D: \(p\) is a factor since \(p(6p + 11)\) shows \(p\) as a common factor.

## 4: Consider common misconceptions
A common misconception might be to think that coefficients or higher powers of variables must be factors if they appear in the expression. However, the actual process of factoring involves finding common factors.
