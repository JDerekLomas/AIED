To approach this question, let's first understand what each term means and then apply that understanding to the given context.

- **Equation**: A statement that two mathematical expressions have the same value, often with an equals sign (=).
- **Formula**: A general expression or rule made up of variables, constants, and mathematical operations that describes a relationship between quantities.
- **Variable**: A symbol that represents a value that can change.
- **Expression**: A group of numbers, symbols, and/or operations that represents a value.

Given \( V=\frac{1}{3} \pi r^{2} h \), this represents the volume \(V\) of a cone, with \(r\) being the radius of the base and \(h\) being the height. This statement equates the volume \(V\) to \(\frac{1}{3} \pi r^{2} h\), making it an **equation** because it has an equals sign and states that two expressions are equal. However, in common mathematical terminology, especially in the context of geometry and when solving problems, \( V=\frac{1}{3} \pi r^{2} h \) is referred to as a **formula** because it expresses a general rule for calculating the volume of a cone.
