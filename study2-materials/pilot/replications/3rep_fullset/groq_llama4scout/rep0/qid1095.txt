To approach this question, let's first understand what standard form (or scientific notation) means. A number in standard form is written as \(a \times 10^n\), where \(1 \leq a < 10\) and \(n\) is an integer. The number \(a\) is often a decimal, and \(n\) indicates the number of places the decimal point in \(a\) needs to be moved to get the original number.

The given number is \(300,200\). To convert this into standard form:

1. We move the decimal point to the left until we have a number between 1 and 10, which gives us \(3.002\).
2. We moved the decimal point 5 places to the left to get \(3.002\), so \(n = 5\).

Therefore, \(300,200\) in standard form is \(3.002 \times 10^5\).
