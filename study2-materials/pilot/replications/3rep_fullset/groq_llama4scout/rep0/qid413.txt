## Step 1: Understanding the Question
The question presents a right-angled triangle with given measurements: base length = 12 cm, slant length = 15 cm, and perpendicular height = 9 cm. The task is to calculate the area of the triangle.

## 2: Identifying the Formula for the Area of a Triangle
The formula for the area of a triangle when the base and height are known is \(\frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height}\).

## 3: Applying the Formula
Substituting the given values into the formula: \(\frac{1}{2} \times 12 \times 9 = \frac{1}{2} \times 108 = 54 \, \mathrm{cm}^2\).
