## Step 1: Understanding the Question
The question provides a conversion factor between miles and kilometers: \(3\) miles \(\approx 5\) km. It then asks to convert \(30\) km into miles.

## 2: Identifying the Misconception
A common misconception in conversion problems is not applying the conversion factor correctly, especially when the numbers are large or when the conversion factor is not 1:1.

## 3: Analyzing the Conversion
Given \(3\) miles \(\approx 5\) km, to find out how many miles \(30\) km is approximately equal to, we set up a proportion: \(\frac{3 \text{ miles}}{5 \text{ km}} = \frac{x \text{ miles}}{30 \text{ km}}\).

## 4: Solving the Proportion
Solving for \(x\), we get \(x = \frac{3 \text{ miles} \times 30 \text{ km}}{5 \text{ km}} = \frac{90}{5} = 18\).
