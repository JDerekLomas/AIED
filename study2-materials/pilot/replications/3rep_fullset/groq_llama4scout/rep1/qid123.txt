Parallel lines are lines that lie in the same plane and never intersect, no matter how far they are extended. A key characteristic of parallel lines is that they have the same slope.

## Step 1: Understanding the Lines
The first line in both scenarios goes two squares to the right and one square up. This means for every 2 units it travels to the right, it goes up 1 unit. The slope (or gradient) of this line can be calculated as rise over run, which is 1/2.

## 2: Analyzing Tom's Lines
Tom's lines go 2 squares right and 1 square up for one line, and 4 squares right and 2 squares up for the other. The slope of the second line is 2/4, which simplifies to 1/2. Since both lines have the same slope (1/2), they are parallel.

## 3: Analyzing Katie's Lines
Katie's lines go 2 squares right and 1 square up for one line, and 2 squares right and 1 square down for the other. The slope of the first line is 1/2 (as calculated before), and the slope of the second line is -1/2 (since it goes down 1 unit for every 2 units it goes to the right). These lines do not have the same slope, so they are not parallel.
