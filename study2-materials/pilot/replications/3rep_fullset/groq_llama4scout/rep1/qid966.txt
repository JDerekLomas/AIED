## Step 1: Understand the quadratic formula and its components.
The quadratic formula is given by \(h = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}\). For the equation \(3h^2 - 10h + 4 = 0\), \(a = 3\), \(b = -10\), and \(c = 4\).

## 2: Identify the components of the quadratic formula in the given equation.
To use the quadratic formula, we need to calculate \(b^2\) and \(4ac\). Here, \(b^2 = (-10)^2 = 100\) and \(4ac = 4 \times 3 \times 4 = 48\).

## 3: Determine the part of the formula that needs to be replaced.
The part of the formula that involves \(b^2 - 4ac\) under the square root needs to be identified. So, \(b^2 - 4ac = 100 - 48 = 52\).

## 4: Analyze the denominator of the quadratic formula.
The denominator is \(2a = 2 \times 3 = 6\).
