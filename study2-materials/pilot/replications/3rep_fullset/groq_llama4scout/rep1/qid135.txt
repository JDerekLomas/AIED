## Step 1: Understanding the Question
The question requires students to understand that the total quantity distributed must add up to the original amount, which in this case starts at 60%. One of the branches takes 2/5 of the 60%.

## 2: Converting 2/5 to a Percentage
To work with 2/5 easily, convert it to a percentage or a decimal. 2/5 as a decimal is 0.4, and as a percentage, it is 40%. This means one branch takes 40% of 60%, not 40% of the whole.

## 3: Calculating the Amount Taken by the First Branch
40% of 60% is 0.4 * 60% = 24%. So, 24% is taken by one branch.

## 4: Finding the Missing Value
The missing value must make the total add up to 60%. Since one branch takes 24%, the other branch must take 60% - 24% = 36%.
