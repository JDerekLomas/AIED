The question involves reflecting a point \( P(3, 3) \) in the line \( y = 1 \). The reflection of a point \( (x, y) \) in the line \( y = k \) results in the point \( (x, 2k - y) \). Applying this to the given problem with \( k = 1 \) and \( P(3, 3) \), we find the new coordinates of \( P \) to be \( (3, 2*1 - 3) = (3, -1) \).

## Step 1: Understanding the Reflection Concept
The concept here is to understand that reflecting a point across a horizontal line (in this case, \( y = 1 \)) changes the y-coordinate but keeps the x-coordinate the same. The formula for reflecting \( (x, y) \) over \( y = k \) is \( (x, 2k - y) \), which simplifies to \( (x, -y + 2k) \) or specifically for \( y = 1 \), \( (x, 2 - y) \).

## 2: Identifying Potential Misconceptions
A common misconception might be not understanding that the reflection across \( y = 1 \) means the distance of \( P \) from \( y = 1 \) will be mirrored on the other side. Since \( P \) is 2 units above \( y = 1 \), its reflection will be 2 units below \( y = 1 \), leading to \( y = -1 \).
