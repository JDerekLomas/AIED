The question asks students to identify the most appropriate word to describe \(3g\) in the expression \(6 - 3g\). The options provided are Variable, Expression, Equation, and Term.

- **Variable**: A variable is a symbol that represents a value that can change.
- **Expression**: An expression is a collection of numbers, symbols, and operators that represents a value.
- **Equation**: An equation is a statement that two mathematical expressions have the same value, often indicated by an equals sign.
- **Term**: A term is a single part of an expression that is separated by an operation (like +, -, etc.).

Given \(6 - 3g\), the entire expression could be considered an equation if there were an equals sign (which there isn't), but specifically, it is an expression. Within \(6 - 3g\), \(6\) and \(3g\) are terms. \(3g\) itself combines a coefficient and a variable, making it a term.
