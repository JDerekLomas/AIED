1. **Understanding the Terms**:
   - **Equation**: A statement that two mathematical expressions have the same value, often containing one or more variables.
   - **Formula**: A general rule expressed using symbols and/or numbers, often derived from an equation.
   - **Identity**: A mathematical statement that is always true, often involving variables and typically expressed as an equation.
   - **Expression**: A group of numbers, symbols, and mathematical operations combined without an equals sign.

2. **Analysis of the Given Statement**:
   - The statement \(x + 3 = 5\) is clearly an equation because it states that two expressions are equal. It's not an identity because it's not universally true for all values of \(x\) (only for \(x = 2\)). It's not a formula in the traditional sense used in mathematics (like \(C = 2\pi r\) for circumference), nor is it just an expression because it contains an equals sign.
