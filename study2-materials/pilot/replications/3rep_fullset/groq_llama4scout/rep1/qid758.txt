## Step 1: Understand the question
The question presents a geometric problem involving two parallel lines cut by a transversal. The task is to find the measure of angle \( t \), given that one of the angles formed is \( 78^{\circ} \). This specific angle is an exterior angle to one of the parallel lines and an interior angle on the same side of the transversal as \( t \).

## 2: Recall relevant geometric properties
When two parallel lines are cut by a transversal, corresponding angles are equal, and interior angles on the same side of the transversal are supplementary (add up to \( 180^{\circ} \)).

## 3: Apply geometric properties to the given problem
Given that the angle to the right of the transversal and above the upper parallel line is \( 78^{\circ} \), and knowing that \( t \) and this \( 78^{\circ} \) angle are supplementary because they are interior angles on the same side of the transversal, we can find \( t \) as \( 180^{\circ} - 78^{\circ} = 102^{\circ} \).
