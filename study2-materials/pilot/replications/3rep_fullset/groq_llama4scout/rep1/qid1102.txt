A common misconception might involve misunderstanding how negative numbers compare, potentially thinking that the larger the negative number, the "greater" or "shallower" it is.

## 2: Identify the correct order
To arrange these depths in ascending order (starting with the deepest), we compare the negative numbers:
- The more negative the number, the deeper it is.
So, \( -6 m \) (Ray) is deeper than \( -2.6 m \) (Tuna), which is deeper than \( -1.5 m \) (Ling).
The correct order from deepest to shallowest is: Ray, Tuna, Ling.
