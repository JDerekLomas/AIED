## Step 1: Understanding the Task
The task is to convert the decimal \(1.4\) into a fraction. This requires an understanding of decimal to fraction conversion, specifically knowing that \(0.4 = \frac{4}{10} = \frac{2}{5}\) and thus \(1.4 = 1 + \frac{2}{5} = \frac{5}{5} + \frac{2}{5} = \frac{7}{5}\) or \(1\frac{2}{5}\).

## 2: Identifying Potential Misconceptions
- A common misconception might be to interpret \(1.4\) as \(1\frac{4}{10}\) directly, which simplifies to \(1\frac{2}{5}\) or \(\frac{7}{5}\), but not as \(1\frac{4}{5}\).
- Another misconception could be misunderstanding the decimal part as a fraction directly without converting it properly.
- Students might also think that because \(1.4 > 1\), it cannot be converted into a simple fraction or might resist the idea of converting decimals greater than 1 into fractions.

- Option C: \(\frac{7}{5}\) or \(1\frac{2}{5}\) is correct.
- Option D: "Not possible as it is greater than \(1\)" is incorrect because decimals greater than 1 can indeed be converted into fractions.
