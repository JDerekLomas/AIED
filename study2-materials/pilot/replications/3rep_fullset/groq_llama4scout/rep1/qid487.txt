## Step 1: Understand the question and the perimeter of a rectangle formula
The perimeter \(P\) of a rectangle is given by \(P = 2l + 2w\), where \(l\) is the length and \(w\) is the width. In this question, the perimeter \(P = 1.6 \, \mathrm{m}\) and one side (let's assume the width \(w\)) is \(0.35 \, \mathrm{m}\). We need to find the length of the side marked with a star, which we'll call \(l\).

## 2: Calculate the length of the side marked with a star
Given \(P = 1.6 \, \mathrm{m}\) and \(w = 0.35 \, \mathrm{m}\), we substitute these into the perimeter formula:
\[1.6 = 2l + 2(0.35)\]
\[1.6 = 2l + 0.7\]
\[2l = 1.6 - 0.7\]
\[2l = 0.9\]
\[l = 0.45 \, \mathrm{m}\]
