## Step 1: Understand the question
The question presents a diagram with two vertical lines crossed by a diagonal line, forming several angles. ## 2: Identify the relevant geometric principle
The key principle here is that when two lines intersect, the vertically opposite angles are equal. However, the diagram shows two vertical lines and one diagonal line. For the angles formed on the same side of the transversal (the diagonal line) and inside the two vertical lines (or parallel lines if we consider the context usually applied), we consider corresponding angles, alternate interior angles, etc., but specifically, for two lines cut by a transversal, the corresponding angles are equal, and the interior angles on the same side of the transversal are supplementary.

## 3: Apply the geometric principle to the given diagram
Given the diagram, the angle marked '70' and the angle '?' are corresponding angles because they are on the same side of the transversal (diagonal line) and in the same relative position with respect to the vertical lines. This implies they should be equal.
