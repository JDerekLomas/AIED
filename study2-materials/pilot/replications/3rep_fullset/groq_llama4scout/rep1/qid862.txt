The question requires students to solve the equation \(4x - 1 = -3\) using a graph of \(y = 4x - 1\). The correct method involves setting \(y = -3\) and finding the corresponding \(x\) value on the graph.

## Step 1: Understanding the Task
Students need to understand that solving \(4x - 1 = -3\) graphically means finding where the line \(y = 4x - 1\) intersects the line \(y = -3\), which translates to finding \(x\) when \(y = -3\).

## 2: Solving the Equation Algebraically for Reference
To solve \(4x - 1 = -3\) algebraically:
\(4x - 1 = -3\)
\(4x = -2\)
\(x = -\frac{2}{4} = -0.5\)

## 3: Analyzing Potential Misconceptions and Errors
- A common misconception might be misreading the graph or not using it correctly.
- Another could be algebraic mistakes if students were to solve it algebraically instead of or in addition to using the graph.
