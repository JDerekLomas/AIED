## Step 1: Understand the question and the misconception it tests.
The question tests the understanding of ratios and proportional relationships. It states that for every 5 marbles Karen has, Marty has three times as many marbles as Karen. This means if Karen has 5 marbles, Marty has \(5 \times 3 = 15\) marbles.

## 2: Determine the correct ratio.
To express the number of marbles Karen and Marty have as a ratio with Karen's part first, we calculate it as 5:15. This ratio can be simplified by dividing both parts by their greatest common divisor, which is 5. So, the simplified ratio is \(5/5 : 15/5 = 1 : 3\).

## 3: Identify potential misconceptions and errors.
A common misconception might be to directly use the given multiplier (three times as many) without understanding it implies Marty's marbles are \(3 \times\) Karen's marbles, not that the ratio of Karen's to Marty's marbles is \(1:3\) directly. Another error could be in simplifying or representing the ratio incorrectly.
