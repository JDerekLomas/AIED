## Step 1: Understand the question and the concept being tested
The question involves the enlargement of a rectangle by a scale factor of 1.5. The original rectangle is labeled P, and the enlarged rectangle is labeled Q. The dimensions of Q are given as width = 9cm and length = 12cm. The task is to find the length of rectangle P, which is marked with a star.

## 2: Recall the process of enlargement in geometry
When a figure is enlarged by a scale factor, each of its dimensions (lengths and widths) is multiplied by that scale factor to get the dimensions of the enlarged figure. Here, the scale factor is 1.5, meaning that the dimensions of P are multiplied by 1.5 to get the dimensions of Q.

## 3: Apply the concept to find the missing length
Given that the length of Q is 12cm and it is obtained by enlarging the length of P by a scale factor of 1.5, we can set up the equation:
\[ \text{length of P} \times 1.5 = 12 \]
Solving for the length of P:
\[ \text{length of P} = \frac{12}{1.5} = 8 \]
