The question involves understanding two key geometric properties: 
1. Exactly two lines of symmetry
2. Rotational symmetry of order 2

A rectangle has exactly two lines of symmetry (its diagonals are not lines of symmetry, but for a rectangle, the lines of symmetry are the ones through the midpoints of opposite sides) and rotational symmetry of order 2 (since it looks the same after a 180-degree rotation).

## Step 1: Understanding the Properties
- A shape with exactly two lines of symmetry could be a rectangle (among other shapes like a kite).
- A shape with rotational symmetry of order 2 looks the same after being rotated by 180 degrees, which applies to rectangles.

## 3: Applying to a Rectangle
