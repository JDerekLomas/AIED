## Step 1: Understand the question and the task
The task is to predict the percentage of students at each ability level (below_basic, basic, proficient, advanced) who would choose each option (A, B, C, D) when rearranging the equation \(g=2r+3\) to make \(r\) the subject.

## 2: Identify the correct solution
To make \(r\) the subject, we start with \(g = 2r + 3\). Subtracting 3 from both sides gives \(g - 3 = 2r\). Dividing both sides by 2 yields \(r = \frac{g - 3}{2}\). This matches option C.

- Option B, \(r = g + 3 \div 2\), incorrectly suggests that the division by 2 only applies to the 3, not to the result of \(g + 3\), and misrepresents the algebraic relationship.
- Option D, \(r = g - \frac{3}{2}\), suggests subtracting \(\frac{3}{2}\) from \(g\) without dividing by 2, which is another algebraic error.
