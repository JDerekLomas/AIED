## Step 1: Understanding Significant Figures
Rounding to one significant figure involves looking at the second digit of the number (after the decimal point if the number is less than 1) and deciding whether to round up or down based on its value. If the second digit is 5 or greater, we round up; if it's less than 5, we round down.

## 2: Applying to \(0.00809\)
The number given is \(0.00809\). When rounding to one significant figure, we look at the first non-zero digit, which is 8 in this case. The rule for significant figures states that if the digit immediately after the one we are keeping is less than 5, we simply keep the first non-zero digit as it is. If it's 5 or greater, we add 1 to it.
