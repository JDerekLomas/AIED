The question asks for the mean of the numbers \(2, 2, 3, 3, 2\). To find the mean, we sum all the numbers and then divide by the count of numbers.

## Step 1: Calculate the mean
The sum of the numbers is \(2 + 2 + 3 + 3 + 2 = 12\).
There are \(5\) numbers.
So, the mean is \(12 / 5 = 2.4\).

## 2: Analyze the options

- C: \(3\)
- D: A little less than \(2\)

Given that the actual mean is \(2.4\), option B is the correct answer because \(2.4\) is indeed a little more than \(2\).

- Another potential error could be not calculating the mean properly or misunderstanding what "a little more than \(2\)" implies.
