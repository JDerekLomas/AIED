## Step 1: Understanding the Question
The question requires students to understand ratios and apply them to find a missing quantity in a mixture problem. The ratio of red paint to white paint is given as \(3:4\), and the amount of red paint is given as \(1200 \, \mathrm{ml}\). Students need to find the amount of white paint.

## 2: Identifying the Ratio Relationship
The total parts in the ratio are \(3 + 4 = 7\) parts. The red paint represents \(3\) parts, and the white paint represents \(4\) parts.

## 3: Calculating the Amount of White Paint
To find the amount of white paint (the star), first, determine the value of one part of the ratio. Since \(3\) parts equal \(1200 \, \mathrm{ml}\), one part equals \(1200 \, \mathrm{ml} / 3 = 400 \, \mathrm{ml}\). Therefore, \(4\) parts (the amount of white paint) equal \(4 \times 400 \, \mathrm{ml} = 1600 \, \mathrm{ml}\).

## 4: Analyzing Potential Student Errors

- Another potential error could be failing to recognize that if \(1200 \, \mathrm{ml}\) represents \(3\) parts, then the calculation for \(1\) part and subsequently \(4\) parts must be correctly scaled.
