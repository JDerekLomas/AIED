The question is about interpreting bar models, which are visual representations used to help solve mathematical problems, especially in algebra. The bar models presented are supposed to represent the expression \(2x\). 

- Tom's bar model shows two segments, one labeled "2" and the other labeled "\(x\)", which might imply \(2 + x\) rather than \(2x\).
- Katie's bar model shows two segments, both labeled "\(x\)", which clearly represents \(x + x = 2x\).

## Understanding the Misconception
The potential misconception here involves understanding that \(2x\) means \(x + x\), not \(2 \times \text{some number}\). Students who might struggle with this could mistakenly think Tom's representation is correct because they misunderstand how \(2x\) should be visually represented.
