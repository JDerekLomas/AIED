## Step 1: Understanding Standard Form
Standard form, also known as scientific notation, is a way of writing very large or very small numbers as a product of a number between 1 and 10 and a power of 10. The general form is \(a \times 10^n\), where \(1 \leq a < 10\).

## 2: Identifying Potential Misconceptions
A common misconception is not correctly placing the decimal point or incorrectly determining the exponent \(n\). For the number 300,200, students need to move the decimal point to the left until they have a number between 1 and 10, which gives them 3.002. They then need to count how many places they moved the decimal point, which is 5 places, resulting in \(3.002 \times 10^5\).
