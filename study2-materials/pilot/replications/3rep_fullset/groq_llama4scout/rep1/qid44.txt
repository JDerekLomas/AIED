1. All angles are right angles (90 degrees).
2. Opposite sides are equal in length.

The question asks about triangle \( \mathrm{BCD} \).

## Step 1: Understanding Triangle BCD
Triangle \( \mathrm{BCD} \) is formed by points B, C, and D. Since \( A B C D \) is a rectangle, angle \( \mathrm{BCD} \) is 90 degrees because it is one of the angles of the rectangle.

## 2: Identifying the Type of Triangle
- A right-angled triangle has one 90-degree angle.
- An isosceles triangle has two sides of equal length.
- A scalene triangle has all sides of different lengths.
- An equilateral triangle has all sides of equal length and all angles equal to 60 degrees.

## 3: Applying Properties to Triangle BCD
Since \( \mathrm{ABCD} \) is a rectangle, \( \mathrm{BC} = \mathrm{AD} \) and \( \mathrm{CD} = \mathrm{AB} \). Also, \( \mathrm{BD} \) is the diagonal of the rectangle. For triangle \( \mathrm{BCD} \), \( \mathrm{BC} \) and \( \mathrm{CD} \) are not necessarily equal (unless the rectangle is a square), but \( \mathrm{BCD} \) is a right angle.
