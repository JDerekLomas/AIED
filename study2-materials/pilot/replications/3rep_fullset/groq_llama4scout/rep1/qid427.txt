## Step 1: Understand the question and the concept of gradient
The question asks for the best estimate of the gradient of a curve at a specific point \( t = 6 \) seconds, given a velocity-time graph of a parachutist. The gradient of a curve at any point represents the rate of change of velocity with respect to time, which in physics is known as acceleration. The graph provides a tangent line at \( t = 6 \) seconds, and two points through which this tangent line passes are given as \( (2, 31) \) and \( (6, 47) \) and also \( (8, 55) \). However, for calculating the gradient at \( t = 6 \), we can use any two points on the tangent line.

## 2: Calculate the gradient using given points
To find the gradient, we use the formula: \( \text{gradient} = \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t} \). Using points \( (2, 31) \) and \( (6, 47) \):
\[ \text{gradient} = \frac{47 - 31}{6 - 2} = \frac{16}{4} = 4 \]

Using points \( (6, 47) \) and \( (8, 55) \) to confirm:
\[ \text{gradient} = \frac{55 - 47}{8 - 6} = \frac{8}{2} = 4 \]
