The question presents a right-angled triangle with an angle labeled as 32 degrees, the side adjacent to this angle labeled as 6cm, and the side opposite to this angle labeled as \( p \). The task is to choose the correct trigonometric ratio to find \( p \).

## Step 1: Recall Trigonometric Ratios
The three basic trigonometric ratios are:
- Sine (Sin) = opposite side / hypotenuse
- Cosine (Cos) = adjacent side / hypotenuse
- Tangent (Tan) = opposite side / adjacent side

## 2: Identify Given and Required Information
Given: angle = 32 degrees, adjacent side = 6cm, opposite side = \( p \) (which we need to find).
The hypotenuse is not given.

## 3: Analyze the Options
- To find \( p \) (the opposite side), we need a ratio that involves the opposite side and the adjacent side or a way to incorporate the hypotenuse if using Sin or Cos.
- Option A (Sin) involves the opposite side and the hypotenuse, but the hypotenuse is not provided.
- Option B (Cos) involves the adjacent side and the hypotenuse, which again, doesn't directly help since the hypotenuse is unknown.
- Option C (Tan) = opposite side / adjacent side. This directly involves \( p \) and the given side (6cm), and it does not require the hypotenuse.

## 4: Consider Common Misconceptions
A common misconception might be confusing which sides are used for each trigonometric function. However, the specific setup here (opposite side and adjacent side given, with the goal to find the opposite side) should guide students towards using Tan since it's the ratio that directly relates these two sides.
