## Step 1: Understand the question
The question presents a descending linear sequence with a constant difference of 22 and states that the 3rd term is 712. The task is to find the value that should replace the star, which presumably is the first term of the sequence.

## 2: Identify the sequence's properties
In a descending linear sequence with a constant difference, each term decreases by the same amount to get to the next term. Here, the difference is 22, meaning each term is 22 less than the preceding term.

## 3: Determine the formula for the nth term
The formula for the nth term of a linear sequence can be given as \(a_n = a_1 + (n-1)d\), where \(a_n\) is the nth term, \(a_1\) is the first term, \(n\) is the term number, and \(d\) is the common difference. For a descending sequence with a difference of 22, \(d = -22\).

## 4: Apply given information
Given the 3rd term (\(a_3\)) is 712, and \(d = -22\), we can substitute these into the formula: \(712 = a_1 + (3-1)(-22)\).

## 5: Solve for \(a_1\)
Simplifying the equation: \(712 = a_1 + 2(-22)\),
\(712 = a_1 - 44\),
\(a_1 = 712 + 44\),
\(a_1 = 756\).
