- Tangent (Tan) = Opposite / Adjacent

Given the triangle with an angle of 52 degrees, the side opposite to this angle is 6cm, and the side adjacent to this angle is \( p \) (which we are trying to find).

## Step 1: Identify the sides in relation to the given angle
The side opposite the 52-degree angle is 6cm, and the side adjacent to it is \( p \).

## 2: Determine the appropriate trigonometric ratio
To find \( p \), we need a ratio that involves \( p \) (the adjacent side) and the 6cm side (the opposite side). The tangent ratio is \( \tan = \frac{opposite}{adjacent} \), which can be rearranged to find \( p \): \( p = \frac{opposite}{\tan(angle)} \) or directly using \( \tan(52^\circ) = \frac{6}{p} \), implying \( p = \frac{6}{\tan(52^\circ)} \).
