## Step 1: Understand the concept of similar shapes
Similar shapes maintain the same proportions between their corresponding sides. This means that the ratio of the lengths of any two corresponding sides in one shape is equal to the ratio of the lengths of any two corresponding sides in the other shape.

## 2: Apply the concept to the given problem
Given that the two triangles are similar, we can set up a proportion based on the side lengths provided. The smaller triangle has sides 5cm and 4cm, and the larger triangle has a corresponding side to the 5cm side that is 15cm, and the side corresponding to the 4cm side is marked with a star.

## 3: Calculate the scale factor
The scale factor from the smaller triangle to the larger triangle can be found by dividing the length of the corresponding side in the larger triangle by the length in the smaller triangle. So, the scale factor is \( \frac{15}{5} = 3 \).

## 4: Determine the length of the star-marked side
Using the scale factor, we can find the length of the side marked with a star. Since the scale factor is 3, we multiply the 4cm side of the smaller triangle by 3 to get \( 4 \times 3 = 12 \)cm.
