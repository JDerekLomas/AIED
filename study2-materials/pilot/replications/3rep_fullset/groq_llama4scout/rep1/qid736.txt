## Step 1: Understanding Isosceles Triangles
An isosceles triangle, by definition, has two sides of equal length. A key property of an isosceles triangle is that it has exactly one line of symmetry, which passes through the vertex where the two equal sides meet and is perpendicular to the base. However, if the triangle is also equilateral (all sides are equal), then it has more than one line of symmetry and also has rotational symmetry of order 3.
