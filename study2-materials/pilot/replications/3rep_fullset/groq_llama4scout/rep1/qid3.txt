## Understanding the Angles
- The diagonals of a rectangle intersect at right angles if and only if the rectangle is a square. For a general rectangle that is not a square, the diagonals intersect but not at right angles.
- The highlighted angles are formed by the intersection of the diagonals. 

## Analyzing the Options
- **Acute (A):** An acute angle is less than 90 degrees. 
- **Obtuse (B):** An obtuse angle is greater than 90 degrees but less than 180 degrees.
- **\( 90^{\circ} \) (C):** A right angle, exactly 90 degrees.
- **Not enough information (D):** This option suggests there's insufficient information to determine the angles.
