The question asks to identify a regular polygon from given quadrilaterals. A regular polygon is a shape with equal sides and equal angles. 

- A regular polygon with 4 sides is a square.
- A square is a special kind of rectangle and rhombus, but not all rectangles or rhombuses are squares.

## Step 1: Understanding the Options
- Option A: Trapezium - This is not a regular polygon because it does not have equal sides or equal angles.
- Option B: Rhombus - While a rhombus has equal sides, it does not necessarily have equal angles, so it's not necessarily a regular polygon.
- Option C: Rectangle - A rectangle has equal angles (all right angles), but its sides are not necessarily equal, so it's not a regular polygon unless it's a square.
- Option D: Square - This has both equal sides and equal angles, making it a regular polygon.

## 2: Analyzing Potential Misconceptions
A common misconception might be that a rhombus or a rectangle could be considered regular polygons because they have some properties of regular polygons (like equal sides for a rhombus or equal angles for a rectangle). However, for a shape to be a regular polygon, it must have both equal sides and equal angles.
