## Step 1: Understanding Trigonometric Ratios
- \( \sin(\theta) = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}} \)
- \( \cos(\theta) = \frac{\text{adjacent}}{\text{hypotenuse}} \)
- \( \tan(\theta) = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}} \)

## 2: Identifying Given and Required Information
The side opposite to \( \theta \) is 10cm, and the side adjacent to \( \theta \) is 7cm. However, the hypotenuse's length is not given.

## 3: Determining the Correct Ratio
To find \( \theta \), we need a ratio that involves the 10cm side (opposite to \( \theta \)) and the 7cm side (adjacent to \( \theta \)), since these are the given sides. The \( \tan \) ratio directly involves these two sides: \( \tan(\theta) = \frac{10}{7} \). This makes \( \tan \) the correct choice.
