**Below Basic** students often rely on "number grabbing," where they take the digits present ($4, 2, 10$) and perform an operation like $10 \times 2$. 

**Basic** students often find the next value in the table ($x=1, y=3$) because it feels "easier" or they forget to divide by the coefficient of $y$. 

**Proficient** students usually understand substitution but frequently fall for the "interchangeable intercept" error, solving for the $x$-intercept ($10 \div 4 = 2.5$) instead of the $y$-intercept.

**Advanced** students should navigate the multi-step rearrangement confidently.
