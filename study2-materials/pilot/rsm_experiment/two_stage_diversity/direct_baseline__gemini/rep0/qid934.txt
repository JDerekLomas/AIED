*   **Choice A (20):** Common among lower-ability students who confuse the operation. They see $4(0)$ as $0$, but then mistakenly multiply $10$ by $2$ instead of dividing.
*   **Choice B (3):** This is a "pattern-seeking" error. Students who don't know how to substitute often look at the numbers present ($4, 2$) and assume the missing number in the sequence $2, 3, 4$ or something similar is $3$.
