In my experience marking this specific order of operations (BIDMAS/BODMAS) question, the primary pitfall is the "Left-to-Right" trap. Students who lack a firm grasp of operation hierarchy naturally want to sum 8 and 12 first on the left side, which leads them to see both sides as identical.

**Analysis of the Distractors:**
*   **The "Equality" Trap (C):** Students at the lower end see $(8+12)$ on both sides and simply ignore the placement of brackets or the hierarchy of division, concluding $20 \div 4 = 20 \div 4$.
*   **The "Greater Than" Calculation (A):** This is the correct path. $8 + (12 \div 4) = 8 + 3 = 11$, whereas $(8+12) \div 4 = 20 \div 4 = 5$. Since $11 > 5$, A is correct.
