In this context, the student is being asked to find the $y$-intercept of a linear equation presented in its implicit form ($ax + by = c$).

### 1. The Misconception: "Operation Detachment / Variable Neglect"
The specific bug is **Variable Neglect resulting in a Simple Calculation**. The student recognizes the numbers 4, 2, and 10 in the equation, but instead of treating the variables as containers for values, they see the equation as a "number sentence" puzzle. They ignore the relationship between $4x$ and $2y$ (multiplication/addition) and instead attempt to reach 10 using only the constants and the coefficient of the variable they are solving for.

Alternatively, a common specific procedural bug for Year 9 is the **"First Number is X" error**, where the student believes the value for $x$ must be the first number seen in the equation ($4$), rather than the value given in the table ($0$).

### 2. The Step-by-Step Procedure
A student with this misconception would follow these steps:
1. **Identify the goal:** Find the value for $y$ when $x$ is 0.
2. **Scan the equation:** Look at $4x + 2y = 10$.
3. **Execute the "Neglect" bug:** Since $x=0$, the student assumes the $4x$ part of the equation simply "disappears" or becomes irrelevant (which is mathematically correct in this specific instance).
4. **Perform the "Single Operation" logic:** They are left with $2y = 10$. Instead of dividing 10 by 2, the student's brain looks for a basic arithmetic bond. They see "2" and "10" and think, "What do I do to 2 to get 10?"
5. **The Error:** Because $4 + 2 + \square = 10$ is a common structure in earlier primary key stages, the student performs **$10 - 2 - 3$** (attempting to use all numbers in the row) or, most likely, assumes the star is the result of the constant 10 minus the coefficient 4 and the coefficient 2: **$10 - 4 - 1 = 5$** or simply **$10 \div (4 \times 1)$**.

**However, the most likely path to a wrong answer in the options provided is the "Calculation reversal" or "Substitution swap":**
* The student substitutes $x = 0$ into the equation correctly: $4(0) + 2y = 10$.
* They simplify to $2y = 10$.
* Instead of dividing 10 by 2, they perform an **Additive Compensation**: they think "$2 + \text{what} = 10$?" or "$2 \times 10 = 20$".
