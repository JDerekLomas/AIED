One specific reason students find this question difficult is the **visual "Grouping Trap"** involving the horizontal fraction bar.

In the original expression \(c \div (h+4)\), the brackets explicitly tell the student that $h+4$ is a single entity. However, when transitioning to algebraic fraction notation (Option A), those brackets disappear because the length of the **vinculum** (the horizontal bar) acts as an "invisible" grouping symbol.

**In short:** The difficulty lies in recognizing that the fraction bar replaces the need for brackets, a notation shift that contradicts the student's earlier training that "grouping always requires parentheses."
