One specific reason why students might find this question difficult is a **"left-to-right processing" visual trap**, where the spatial arrangement of the symbols overrides the rules of PEMDAS/BODMAS.

Specifically, the question presents the addition ($5+4$) in the middle of the expression, flanked by subtraction on the left and multiplication on the right. Many students develop a robust but incorrect heuristic of **"reading the expression like a sentence."**

Because the human eye scans left-to-right, students often intuitively perform the "weaker" operations (subtraction and addition) first simply because they encounter them first, viewing the multiplication as a final "action" to be performed on the total of the preceding string rather than an operation with high precedence that must be isolated.
