## Step 1: Identify the specific misconception or procedural error.
The specific misconception or procedural error that a UK Year 9 student might apply in this context is inverting the operation when solving for a variable in an equation. Specifically, when trying to isolate the variable (in this case, \(\star\)), a common mistake is to forget to invert the operation when dividing or multiplying both sides of the equation by a number. However, another prevalent misconception related to operations with fractions and negative numbers is the incorrect handling of negative signs.

To solve \( -\frac{3}{4} \times \star = -1 \), a correct approach would be to divide both sides by \( -\frac{3}{4} \), which can also be represented as multiplying both sides by the reciprocal \( -\frac{4}{3} \). 1. \( -\frac{3}{4} \times \star = -1 \)
2. Multiply both sides by \( -\frac{4}{3} \), but incorrectly apply it as \( \star = -1 \times -\frac{3}{4} \) (which actually results from a correct inversion but let's see how it's misapplied).
