The specific misconception or procedural error in this context could be the failure to include all numbers in the dataset when calculating the mean. This is a common misconception where students might exclude the zero or any other number from the calculation, especially if it seems not to contribute to the sum in an obvious way.

- They might think, "The zero doesn't really count because it's zero," or simply forget to include it.
- They add the remaining numbers: 1 + 3 + 4 + 7 = 15.
- Then, they divide by what they think is the correct count of numbers, which could be 4 (since they excluded the zero).
- This leads to the calculation: \( \frac{1+3+4+7}{4} = \frac{15}{4} \).
