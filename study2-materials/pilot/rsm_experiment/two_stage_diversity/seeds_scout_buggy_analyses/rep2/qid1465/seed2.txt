## Step 1: Identify the specific misconception or procedural error.
The specific misconception or procedural error that a UK Year 9 student might apply to the given problem is the incorrect application of the order of operations, specifically ignoring the correct order which dictates that multiplication should be performed before addition and subtraction. This is often remembered by the acronym BODMAS (Brackets, Orders, Division, Multiplication, Addition, and Subtraction). A common bug is to perform operations from left to right without adhering to BODMAS.

## 2: Describe how a student with this misconception would work through the question step by step.
A student with the misconception of performing operations from left to right without following BODMAS would work through the question as follows:
1. Start with the first operation from the left: $3 - 5 = -2$
2. Then, perform the next operation: $-2 + 4 = 2$
3. Finally, perform the multiplication and addition/subtraction in the order they appear: $2 \times 2 = 4$

Following the incorrect procedure:

So, this misconception leads to answer option A: $6$.

This error is quite natural and attractive because it follows a simple, intuitive left-to-right approach that many students initially adopt before being taught the more formal rules like BODMAS. Students might not even realize they went wrong because their calculation seems logical at first glance. It requires an understanding of the importance of the order of operations to recognize the mistake.
