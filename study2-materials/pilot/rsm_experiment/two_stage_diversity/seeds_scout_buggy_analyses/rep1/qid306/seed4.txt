## Step 1: Identify the specific misconception or procedural error.
The specific misconception or procedural error to identify involves understanding the order of operations, often remembered by the acronym BIDMAS (Brackets, Indices, Division, Multiplication, Addition, and Subtraction) in the UK. A common misconception is not following this order correctly, especially when it comes to addition and subtraction or multiplication and addition/subtraction.

## 2: Describe how a student with this misconception would work through the question step by step for Problem P and Problem Q.
For Problem P: \( 4 - 5 + 6 = \), a student with the misconception might think that because subtraction and addition have the same precedence, they should be performed from left to right but might incorrectly prioritize operations based on their appearance or mistakenly perform operations in a wrong order due to misremembering the rules. However, the correct approach is to perform the operations from left to right since they have equal precedence: \( 4 - 5 = -1 \) then \( -1 + 6 = 5 \).

For Problem Q: \( 4 + 5 \times 6 = \), the student should perform multiplication before addition according to BIDMAS. However, someone with a misconception might incorrectly add first and then multiply: \( 4 + 5 = 9 \) then \( 9 \times 6 = 54 \), which is incorrect.
