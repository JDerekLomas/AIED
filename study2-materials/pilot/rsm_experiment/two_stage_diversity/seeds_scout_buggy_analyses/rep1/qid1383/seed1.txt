The specific misconception or procedural error that a UK Year 9 student might apply in this context is the mistaken belief that when a negative number is raised to an even power, the result is negative. This error stems from a misunderstanding of exponent rules, particularly for even exponents. The correct rule is that any non-zero number raised to an even power results in a positive value.
