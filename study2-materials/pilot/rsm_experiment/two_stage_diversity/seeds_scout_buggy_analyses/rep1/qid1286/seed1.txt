## 3: Step-by-step work through with the misconception
Given \( V = \frac{4}{3} \pi r^3 \), to solve for \( r \), one should:
1. Multiply both sides by \( \frac{3}{4} \) to get \( \frac{3V}{4} = \pi r^3 \).
2. Divide both sides by \( \pi \) to isolate \( r^3 \): \( \frac{3V}{4\pi} = r^3 \).
3. Take the cube root of both sides: \( r = \sqrt[3]{\frac{3V}{4\pi}} \).
