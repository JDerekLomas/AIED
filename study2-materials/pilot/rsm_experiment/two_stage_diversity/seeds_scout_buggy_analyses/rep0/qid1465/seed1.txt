## Step 1: Identify the specific misconception or procedural error.
The specific misconception or procedural error that a UK Year 9 student might apply is the incorrect application of the order of operations, specifically ignoring the correct sequence or misunderstanding the hierarchy of operations (often remembered by the acronym BODMAS/BIDMAS/PEDMAS, which stands for Brackets, Orders (or Indices), Division, Multiplication, Addition, and Subtraction). A common mistake is to perform operations from left to right without regard to the hierarchy.

2. Perform the operations from left to right:
