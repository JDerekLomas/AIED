## Step 1: Identify the specific misconception or procedural error.
The specific misconception or buggy procedure that a UK Year 9 student might apply is the incorrect application of the order of operations, specifically ignoring the multiplication before addition and subtraction rule (often remembered by the acronym BIDMAS/BODMAS: Brackets, Indices, Division, Multiplication, Addition, and Subtraction). A common error is to perform operations from left to right without considering the precedence of multiplication and division over addition and subtraction.

## 2: Describe how a student with this misconception would work through the question step by step.
A student with this misconception might work through the question as follows:
1. Start with the first operation from the left: \(3 - 5\)
2. Perform the subtraction: \(3 - 5 = -2\)
3. Then, perform the multiplication: \(4 \times 2 = 8\)
4. Finally, add the result of the multiplication to the previous result: \(-2 + 8 = 6\)

## 3: Determine which answer option this misconception leads to.
Following the steps described, the student would arrive at the answer:
\[3 - 5 + 4 \times 2 = -2 + 8 = 6\]
So, this misconception leads to answer option A) \(6\).

This error is quite natural and attractive because it involves performing operations in a straightforward, linear fashion without considering the specific rules that dictate the order of operations. Many students might not realize they went wrong because the steps seem logical and easy to follow, especially if they have not been thoroughly taught or have not practiced applying the order of operations consistently.
