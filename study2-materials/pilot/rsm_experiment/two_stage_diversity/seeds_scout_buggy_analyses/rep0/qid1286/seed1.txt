## Step 1: Identify the correct formula for the volume of a sphere
The correct formula for the volume of a sphere is \( V = \frac{4}{3} \pi r^3 \), where \( V \) is the volume and \( r \) is the radius.

## 2: Solve the correct formula for \( r \) to find the radius
To find \( r \), we rearrange the formula:
\[ V = \frac{4}{3} \pi r^3 \]
\[ V \times \frac{3}{4} \pi^{-1} = r^3 \]
\[ r = \sqrt[3]{\frac{3V}{4\pi}} \]
This means the correct answer is \( \sqrt[3]{\frac{3 V}{4 \pi}} \mathrm{cm} \), which corresponds to option C.
