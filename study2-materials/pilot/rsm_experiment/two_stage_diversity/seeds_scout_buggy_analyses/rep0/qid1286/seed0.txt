1. Multiply both sides by \( \frac{3}{4} \) to get \( \frac{3V}{4} = \pi r^3 \).
2. Divide both sides by \( \pi \) to isolate \( r^3 \), yielding \( \frac{3V}{4\pi} = r^3 \).
3. Take the cube root of both sides to solve for \( r \), resulting in \( r = \sqrt[3]{\frac{3V}{4\pi}} \).
