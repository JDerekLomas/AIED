The specific misconception or procedural error that a UK Year 9 student might apply in solving the equation \(10=\frac{5}{p}\) is inverting both sides of the equation incorrectly or mishandling the algebraic manipulation. A common mistake is to multiply both sides by \(p\) but then not properly isolate \(p\), or to incorrectly apply the rule for solving equations involving fractions.

\[10p = 5\]
Then, they might divide both sides by 10:
\[p = \frac{5}{10}\]
\[p = 0.5\]

However, the error lies in not checking if this manipulation leads to a sensible solution in the context of the original equation.
