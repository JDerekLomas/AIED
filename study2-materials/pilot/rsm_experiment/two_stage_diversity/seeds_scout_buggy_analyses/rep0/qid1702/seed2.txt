Let's assume the misconception involves not rearranging the equation properly to solve for \(x\) when \(y = 0.5\). A student might:
1. Write down \(10x - 6y = 30\).
2. Substitute \(y = 0.5\): \(10x - 6(0.5) = 30\).
3. Calculate: \(10x - 3 = 30\).
4. However, the student might then incorrectly add 3 to both sides and then divide by 10, but let's say they incorrectly solve it as \(10x = 30 - 3 = 27\), then \(x = 27 / 10 = 2.7\).
