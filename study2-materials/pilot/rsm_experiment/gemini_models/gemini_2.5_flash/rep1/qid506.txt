Thinking Process:

1.  **Understand the Question:** The question requires solving a simple linear equation involving a fraction: `3p = 1/12`. The goal is to find `p`. The correct operation is to divide both sides by 3.

2.  **Correct Calculation:**
    `3p = 1/12`
    `p = (1/12) / 3`
    `p = 1/12 * 1/3` (remember dividing by a number is multiplying by its reciprocal)
    `p = 1/36`

    So, B is the correct answer.

3.  **Analyse Each Option and Predict Errors:**

    *   **A) `p = 1/4`**
        *   How to get this?
            *   **Error 1: Multiplication instead of division.** The most common attractive error. Instead of dividing by 3, they might multiply `(1/12) * 3`. This is a classic misinterpretation of 'getting rid of' the coefficient. `(1/12) * 3 = 3/12 = 1/4`.
            *   **Who makes this error?** A very common trap for basic/below-basic students who are weak on inverse operations and fractional arithmetic. Proficient students *should* catch this, but might make it if rushing or panicking.

    *   **B) `p = 1/36`**
        *   This is the correct answer.
        *   **Who gets this?** Students who correctly apply inverse operations (divide by 3) and perform fraction division correctly (`(1/12) / 3 = 1/12 * 1/3`). This will be primarily proficient and advanced students, and some careful basic students.

    *   **C) `p = 4`**
        *   How to get this?
            *   **Error 1: Inverse of reciprocal?** This suggests an incredibly muddled understanding. Perhaps `12 / 3` but somehow dropping the `1/` from `1/12`? Or thinking `3p = 12` (instead of 1/12), then `p = 4`. This is a very fundamental misunderstanding of the fraction.
            *   **Error 2: Inverse of division / multiplication applied to only part of the fraction.** e.g., `p = 12 * 3` or `p = 12/3` after somehow moving the `1`.
            *   **Who makes this error?** Primarily below-basic students, who have little understanding of fraction operations or inverse operations with fractions. This is a very poor calculation.

    *   **D) `p = 1/9`**
        *   How to get this?
            *   **Error 1: "Cross-multiplication" misconception.** This error sometimes comes from trying to "do something" with the numbers directly. `3 * 3` gives 9, so perhaps they tried to "undo" `12` in a weird way? This feels less systematic.
