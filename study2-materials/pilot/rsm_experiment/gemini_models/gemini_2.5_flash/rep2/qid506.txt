**Correct Answer:** The correct answer is B.
To solve $3p = \frac{1}{12}$, you need to divide both sides by 3.
$p = \frac{1}{12} \div 3$
$p = \frac{1}{12} \times \frac{1}{3}$
$p = \frac{1}{36}$

**Analysis of Incorrect Options:**

*   **A) $p = \frac{1}{4}$**
    *   This comes from multiplying $12 \times 3$ and putting it in the denominator ($1 / (12 \times 3) = 1/36$), but then somehow getting confused with the reciprocal or thinking $12 \div 3 = 4$. Or, a very common error: $3p = 1/12 \Rightarrow p = 3/12 \Rightarrow p = 1/4$. This implies multiplying the numerator by 3, not dividing. This is a *very* attractive distractor for students who don't fully grasp fractions and division.

*   **C) $p = 4$**
    *   This is from *inverting* the operation entirely and multiplying $12 \times 3$. Some students see $3p$ and $1/12$ and think "get rid of the fraction, multiply both sides by 12". So, $3p \times 12 = 1 \Rightarrow 36p = 1 \Rightarrow p = 1/36$. No, this is likely from simply looking at $12$ and $3$ and deciding they should be multiplied, giving $36$, then getting the reciprocal of the actual fraction $1/36$ which is $36$, or simply thinking $12 \div 3 = 4$. Students sometimes just look at numbers and pick operations they *can* do, rather than the *correct* one. $12 \div 3 = 4$ is too tempting.
    *   Alternatively, $3p = 1/12 \rightarrow p = 12 \div 3 = 4$. This is a major misconception about the reciprocal and how fractions work in equations.

*   **D) $p = \frac{1}{9}$**
    *   This one is less common, but could come from a student mistakenly thinking $3 \times 3 = 9$ and somehow trying to simplify $1/12$ in relation to $3$, or making an error like $3 \times 1/3 = 1$ and so $12 \times 3/1 = 36$ but then confusion leading to $1/9$. Or maybe they tried to do $1/12$ divided by 3, but simplified it as $1/(12/3)$ or something similarly muddled with the inverse of the number not the reciprocal of the operation. This error feels less systematic. Perhaps from attempting to simplify $3p = 1/12$ by multiplying by 3 to get $p = 3/12 \rightarrow 1/4$, but making a further mistake or getting distracted by $3 \times 3 = 9$.
