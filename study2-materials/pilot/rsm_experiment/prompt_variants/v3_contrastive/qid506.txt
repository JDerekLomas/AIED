The most common error for students at the basic and below-basic levels is a confusion between multiplication and division of the denominator. Students see the 3 and the 12 and perform $12 \div 3 = 4$, leading them to Option A. Higher ability students occasionally fall for the "inverse" trap (Option C) if they mistakenly multiply by 3, but the primary distractor is Option A.
