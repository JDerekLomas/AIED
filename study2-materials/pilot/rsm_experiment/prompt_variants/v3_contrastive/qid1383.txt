This question tests two distinct concepts: index notation (powers) and the rules for multiplying negative numbers. 

The most common trap here is the "even power" rule. Many students incorrectly believe that if you start with a negative number, the result must remain negative (leading to option C). Others fall into the trap of multiplying the base by the exponent (leading to options B and D).

**Analysis by level:**
*   **Below Basic:** These students often confuse powers with multiplication ($3 \times 4$) and struggle with negative signs. D and B will be very attractive.
*   **Basic:** These students usually recognize $3^4 = 81$, but they have a strong "carry the sign" misconception, where they believe if the input is negative, the output must be negative. Option C is the primary distractor here.
*   **Proficient:** Most students at this level understand that an even number of negatives creates a positive result, but a significant minority will still make a "surface-level" error and choose -81 by habit or lack of care with brackets.
*   **Advanced:** These students will correctly identify $(-3) \times (-3) \times (-3) \times (-3) = 81$ almost instinctively.
