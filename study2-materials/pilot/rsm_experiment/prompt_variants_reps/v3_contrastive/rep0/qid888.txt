The primary distractors focus on two common errors:
1. **Option A (The Difference Error):** Students subtract 5 from 7 (or vice versa) to get -2, rather than adding -5 and -7. 
2. **Option D (The Product Error):** Students correctly calculate $-5 - 7 = -12$ but then think that a "minus and a minus make a minus" when multiplying, resulting in -35 instead of +35. This is the most common error among intermediate students.
3. **Option C (The Sign Flip):** Students often associate the two negatives in the brackets with a "positive result" and simply change all signs to plus.

**Difficulty analysis:** This is a mid-tier difficulty question for double bracket expansion because of the double negative. It is significantly harder than $(m+5)(m+7)$.
