The most common error is the "operation confusion." Students see the numbers -1 and -4 and, even though they know they are supposed to "expand" (multiply), their brains default to the additive relationship, leading them to $-5$. This question is actually "easier" than a full expansion because it focuses on a single step, but the specific numbers used are designed to trigger the two most common misconceptions in algebra: $ (-1) \times (-4) = -4 $ (sign error) and $ (-1) + (-4) = -5 $ (operation error).
