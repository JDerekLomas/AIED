## Step 1: Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions for easier calculation
First, let's convert the given mixed numbers into improper fractions to facilitate the addition. The mixed numbers are \(2 \frac{7}{10}\) and \(2 \frac{1}{4}\). Converting them into improper fractions:
\(2 \frac{7}{10} = \frac{(2 \times 10) + 7}{10} = \frac{20 + 7}{10} = \frac{27}{10}\)
\(2 \frac{1}{4} = \frac{(2 \times 4) + 1}{4} = \frac{8 + 1}{4} = \frac{9}{4}\)

## 2: Find a common denominator for the improper fractions
To add these fractions, we need a common denominator. The least common multiple (LCM) of 10 and 4 is 20.
\(\frac{27}{10} = \frac{27 \times 2}{10 \times 2} = \frac{54}{20}\)
\(\frac{9}{4} = \frac{9 \times 5}{4 \times 5} = \frac{45}{20}\)

## 3: Add the fractions
Now, add the fractions:
\(\frac{54}{20} + \frac{45}{20} = \frac{99}{20}\)

## 4: Convert the sum back to a mixed number
Convert \(\frac{99}{20}\) back to a mixed number:
\(\frac{99}{20} = 4 \frac{19}{20}\)
