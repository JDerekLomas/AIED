## 2: Identify the correct solution
To find the gradient, we first need to rearrange the equation into the slope-intercept form, \(y = mx + b\), where \(m\) is the gradient. Rearranging \(5x + 3y = 15\) gives \(3y = -5x + 15\), and then \(y = -\frac{5}{3}x + 5\). Thus, the gradient of the line is \(-\frac{5}{3}\).

C) Choosing C: A student might choose \(\frac{5}{3}\) if they mistakenly consider the ratio of the coefficients of \(x\) to \(y\) but forget the negative sign and the proper rearrangement of the equation.

D) Choosing D: This is the correct answer, \(-\frac{5}{3}\), which we've already determined.
