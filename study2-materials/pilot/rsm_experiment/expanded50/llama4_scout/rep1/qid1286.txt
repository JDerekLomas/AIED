## Step 1: Understand the formula for the volume of a sphere
The volume \(V\) of a sphere is given by the formula \(V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3\), where \(r\) is the radius of the sphere.

## 2: Solve the volume formula for \(r\)
To find the radius \(r\) in terms of \(V\), we rearrange the formula:
\[V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3\]
\[V \cdot \frac{3}{4\pi} = r^3\]
\[r = \sqrt[3]{\frac{3V}{4\pi}}\]

Option A is \(\sqrt[3]{\frac{4V\pi}{3}}\). A student might choose this by mistakenly trying to isolate \(r\) but incorrectly manipulating the formula, possibly by multiplying both sides by \(\pi\) and then trying to solve for \(r\), or simply misplacing the terms.

## 4: Analyze option B
Option B is \(\sqrt[3]{\frac{4V}{3\pi}}\). This would be chosen by a student who incorrectly rearranges the formula, possibly forgetting to multiply by \(\pi\) or incorrectly handling the fraction. For example, they might solve it as \(r^3 = \frac{4}{3} \cdot \frac{V}{\pi}\), which does not correctly derive from the volume formula.

## 5: Analyze option C
Option C, \(\sqrt[3]{\frac{3V}{4\pi}}\), is the correct formula for the radius of the sphere given its volume. This would be chosen by students who correctly derive \(r\) from the volume formula.

## 6: Analyze option D
Option D is \(\frac{3\sqrt[3]{V}}{4\pi}\). This option might be chosen by a student who attempts to solve for \(r\) but mistakenly takes the cube root of \(V\) separately and then applies the rest of the formula incorrectly, not properly accounting for \(r^3 = \frac{3V}{4\pi}\).
