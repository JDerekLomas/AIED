## Step 1: Solve the simultaneous equations to find the correct solution.
To solve the given pair of simultaneous equations:
\[4x+y=7\]
\[3x+2y=4\]
We can use either substitution or elimination method. Let's use elimination here:
Multiplying the first equation by 2 gives:
\[8x + 2y = 14\]
Subtracting the second equation from this result:
\[8x + 2y - (3x + 2y) = 14 - 4\]
\[5x = 10\]
\[x = 2\]
Substituting \(x = 2\) into the first equation:
\[4(2) + y = 7\]
\[8 + y = 7\]
\[y = -1\]
So, the correct solution is \(x = 2, y = -1\).
