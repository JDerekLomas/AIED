## Step 1: Factorisation Analysis
To factorise the expression \(8m - 10\), we need to find the greatest common factor (GCF) of 8 and 10. The GCF of 8 and 10 is 2. So, the expression can be written as \(2(4m - 5)\).

## 2: Error Analysis
A) \( \begin{array}{l}{\bigstar}=8 \\ \Delta=1\end{array} \): This would imply that \(8m - 10 = 8(m - 10)\), which does not factorise the expression correctly. A student might choose this by incorrectly assuming the GCF is 8 and not adjusting the terms inside the parenthesis accordingly.

B) \( \begin{array}{l}{\bigstar}=2 \\ \Delta=4\end{array} \): This option suggests \(8m - 10 = 2(4m - 5)\), which is actually correct but let's verify if it matches the given equation \(8m - 10 \equiv {\bigstar}(\triangle m-\bigcirc)\). Here, \({\bigstar}=2\), \(\triangle=4\), and \(\bigcirc=5\). So, this seems like a potentially correct answer.

C) \( \begin{array}{l}{\bigstar}=5 \\ \Delta=3\end{array} \): Choosing this would mean \(8m - 10 = 5(\frac{8}{5}m - 2)\), which does not factorise the expression correctly. A student might pick this by mistakenly trying to force a factor of 5 into the expression.

D) \( \begin{array}{l}{\bigstar}=4 \\ \Delta=2\end{array} \): This implies \(8m - 10 = 4(2m - 2.5)\), which again does not correctly factorise the given expression. The student might choose this by incorrectly determining the factors.

## 3: Detailed Error Explanation
- A: Incorrectly identifies the GCF as 8 instead of 2.
- B: Correctly identifies the GCF as 2 and correctly factorises the expression.
- C: Incorrectly identifies both the GCF and the coefficient of \(m\).
- D: Incorrectly identifies the GCF as 4 and does not properly adjust the expression.
