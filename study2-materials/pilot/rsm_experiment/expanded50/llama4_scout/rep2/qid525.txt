## 2: Find a common denominator for the improper fractions
To add these fractions, we need a common denominator. The least common multiple (LCM) of 10 and 4 is 20. So, we convert both fractions to have a denominator of 20:
- \(\frac{27}{10} = \frac{27 \times 2}{10 \times 2} = \frac{54}{20}\)
- \(\frac{9}{4} = \frac{9 \times 5}{4 \times 5} = \frac{45}{20}\)

## 3: Add the fractions
Now, add the two fractions:
\(\frac{54}{20} + \frac{45}{20} = \frac{54 + 45}{20} = \frac{99}{20}\)

## 4: Convert the improper fraction back to a mixed number
