In Year 9, many students can recall (or are provided with) $V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$. However, the rearrangement requires three distinct steps: multiplying by 3, dividing by $4\pi$, and cube-rooting the result.

**The calculation errors leading to distractors:**
*   **B is the "Fraction Fumble":** Students know they need to "get rid" of the $\frac{4}{3}$, but they fail to multiply by the reciprocal. They simply move the $4/3$ to the other side without flipping it, resulting in the constants remaining in their original positions.
*   **A is the "Operation Confusion":** Students often move $\pi$ to the other side via multiplication rather than division, or simply group all "known" symbols together at the top of a fraction.
*   **D is the "Incomplete Root":** This is a classic "Basic to Proficient" transition error. The student identifies the need for a cube root but only applies it to the $V$ or fails to recognize that the entire expression must be contained within the radical.
