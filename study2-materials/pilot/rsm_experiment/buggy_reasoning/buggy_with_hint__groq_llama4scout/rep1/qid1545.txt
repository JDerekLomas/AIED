## Step 1: Analyze the given problem and the options.
The problem requires comparing the results of two expressions: \(8+12 \div 4\) and \((8+12) \div 4\). To solve this, we must follow the order of operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS: Parentheses/Brackets, Exponents/Orders, Multiplication and Division from left to right, Addition and Subtraction from left to right).

## 2: Solve the expressions correctly.
First expression: \(8 + 12 \div 4\). Following BODMAS, we do division before addition: \(12 \div 4 = 3\), then \(8 + 3 = 11\).

Second expression: \((8+12) \div 4\). Following BODMAS, we do the operation in the parentheses first: \(8 + 12 = 20\), then \(20 \div 4 = 5\).

## 3: Compare the results and determine the correct symbol.
Since \(11 > 5\), the correct symbol to use is \(>\).

Option D (\(\geq\)) could be chosen by a student who thinks the expressions could be equal or the first one is greater but isn't sure, possibly due to a calculation error or not fully understanding the order of operations.
