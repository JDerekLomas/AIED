## Step 1: Understand the question and the formula for the volume of a sphere
The volume \(V\) of a sphere is given by the formula \(V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3\), where \(r\) is the radius of the sphere. To find the radius \(r\) in terms of \(V\), we need to rearrange this formula.

## 2: Rearrange the volume formula to solve for \(r\)
Starting with \(V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3\), we can multiply both sides by \(\frac{3}{4\pi}\) to get \(\frac{3V}{4\pi} = r^3\). Then, taking the cube root of both sides gives \(r = \sqrt[3]{\frac{3V}{4\pi}}\).

## 3: Identify the correct answer
From the rearranged formula, the correct answer is \(C: \sqrt[3]{\frac{3V}{4\pi}} \, \text{cm}\).
