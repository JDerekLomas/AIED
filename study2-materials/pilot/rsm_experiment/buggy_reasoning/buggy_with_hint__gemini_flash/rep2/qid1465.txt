Hello, I am happy to help. This question is a classic test of **BIDMAS/BODMAS** (Order of Operations) competency, which is a common stumbling block in Key Stage 3 and even for many GCSE foundation students.

The correct answer is **A ($6$)**, following the steps: $4 \times 2 = 8$, then $3 - 5 + 8 = 6$.

Here is my diagnostic breakdown of the errors and student logic.

---

### 1. Analysis of Wrong Answer Options

#### **Option B: 4 (The "Left-to-Right" Misconception)**
*   **Buggy Reasoning:** As specified, the student ignores operation priority and calculates strictly as they read the page: 
    1. $3 - 5 = -2$
    2. $-2 + 4 = 2$
    3. $2 \times 2 = 4$
*   **Attractiveness:** **Very High.** This is the most instinctive way for a human to process information (reading like a book). It requires no cognitive effort to determine "priority." It is highly attractive to students who have a procedural memory of the rules but lack the habit of scanning the whole expression before starting.

#### **Option C: -12 (The "Addition Before Subtraction" Misconception)**
*   **Buggy Reasoning:** The student follows "B-I-D-M-**A**-**S**" too literally, believing Addition must *always* come before Subtraction:
    1. $4 \times 2 = 8$
    2. $5 + 8 = 13$
    3. $3 - 13 = -12$
*   **Attractiveness:** **Medium-High.** This is a common "learned" error. In many UK classrooms, students are taught the acronym but not the nuance that addition and subtraction are equal-priority (as are multiplication and division). A student trying to be "correct" and "diligent" by following the letters of the acronym exactly will land here.

#### **Option D: -10 (Left-to-Right + Sign Confusion)**
*   **Buggy Reasoning:** The student reads left-to-right but makes an integer error (or processes the numbers without the signs):
    1. $3 - 5 = -2$ (or perhaps just focuses on the $5+4$ because it's "easier")
    2. $5 + 4 = 9$, then $3 - 9 = -6$... actually, the most common route to **-10** is usually: $3 - (5+4) \times 2 \Rightarrow 3 - 18$, but more likely a student does: $(3 - 5 + 4) \times (-2)$ or forgets the sign during a jump.
    3. **Alternative Path (More likely for D):** Student does $3 - 5 = -2$; then ignores the $+4$ and just multiplies the $-5 \times 2$. This is often a "desperation" answer or a confusion of where the bracket belongs: $3 - (5 + 8) \Rightarrow -10$.
*   **Attractiveness:** **Low-Medium.** This requires multiple missteps. It is often chosen by students who have very weak grasp of negative numbers combined with order of operations.

---

### 2. Analysis of Difficulty Levels

*   **Below Basic:** These students treat math as "arithmetic reading." Option B is the "correct" way to read a sentence to them.
*   **Basic:** These students know BIDMAS exists but misinterpret the order of the letters (Option C) or struggle with the negative number calculation ($3-5$) involved in Option A.
*   **Proficient:** These students scan for the multiplication first. Most will get A, but under time pressure, a few may fall into the Addition-Subtraction trap (Option C).
*   **Advanced:** This is a trivial "sanity check" question. Errors would only occur through extreme carelessness or a "slip of the pen."
