This question targets a common area of confusion in linear equations: the order of inverse operations. 

Tom's method is the standard "balance" method (adding 5 to both sides first). Katie's method is the classic "multiplication trap." To multiply by 3 correctly, Katie would need to multiply *every* term, resulting in $x - 15 = 36$. Many students, however, mistakenly believe that multiplying by 3 simply "cancels" the denominator of the fraction without affecting the other terms on that side of the equation.
