**The Context:**
*   **The "Zero Effect":** Many students see $x=0$ and mentally "cancel out" the entire left side of the equation, leading them to see only the numbers 4, 2, and 10. 
*   **The Divider:** Those who struggle with formal algebra often perform a single operation (10 รท 2 or 10 รท 4) without considering the full structure.
*   **The Confused:** A subset of students will multiply the first two numbers they see ($4 \times 5 = 20$).

**Why:** These students will likely choose **C (5)**. Their thought process is: "I see $2y = 10$, so $y$ must be 5." This is actually the correct logic, but many will guess **D (2.5)** because they see the 4 and the 10 and try to divide them ($10 \div 4 = 2.5$). **Why:** This group is the most likely to trip up on the "star." Many will correctly identify that if $x=0$, $2y=10$, therefore $y=5$ (**C**). However, a significant number will overcomplicate it or solve for when $y=0$ instead of $x=0$. Others might look at the options and pick **B (3)** because they are trying to find a "pattern" in the $y$ row before they've even calculated the first term.
