As an experienced UK maths teacher, I see this specific type of question frequently in Year 9 assessments. It sits in a "sweet spot" of cognitive conflict where students often feel confident but fall into specific sign-processing traps.

### **DIFFICULTY: 4/10**

**Rationale:**
Technically, this is a simple "reciprocal" question. However, for a typical Year 9 student, it involves managing two distinct rules simultaneously: 
1. How to make a product equal to 1 (multiplicative inverse).
2. How to handle the negative sign. 

This isn't a "random guess" question; it is a **high-misconception** question. The "confidently wrong" factor is very high here because students often remember "flip the fraction" but misapply the "change the sign" rule from perpendicular gradients or simply forget the sign altogether.
