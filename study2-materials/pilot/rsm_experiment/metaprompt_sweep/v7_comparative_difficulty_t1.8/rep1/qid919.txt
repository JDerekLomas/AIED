As an experienced UK maths teacher, I see this as a classic "diagnostic" question. On the surface, it is a simple substitution. However, it tests three distinct skills: index notation (BIDMAS/BODMAS), squaring decimals, and place value.

### DIFFICULTY: 6/10
For a Year 9 class, this sits slightly above the middle of the scale. While "completing the square" (10) is a complex multi-step process, this question is a **"trap" question**. It isn't difficult because the math is advanced; it’s difficult because it triggers high-confidence misconceptions regarding decimals and the order of operations.

### Analysis of the Misconceptions

*   **Distractor B ($0.27$): The High-Confidence Error.**
    This is the most dangerous answer. Students correctly identify that $0.3 \times 0.3 = 0.09$, but they then perform $3 \times 0.09$. Because they are used to "moving the decimal," many will see $3 \times 9 = 27$ and conclude the answer must be $0.27$ (mistakenly thinking there is only one decimal place involved in the final product). It is a "logical" wrong answer.

*   **Distractor A ($0.81$): Squaring the Whole Expression.**
    These students perform $(3x)^2$ instead of $3(x^2)$. They do $3 \times 0.3 = 0.9$, then $0.9^2 = 0.81$. This is a classic BIDMAS error where they multiply before dealing with the index.

*   **Distractor C ($1.8$): The Linear Error.**
    Typically seen in lower sets, students confuse $x^2$ with $2x$. They calculate $3 \times (0.3 \times 2) = 1.8$.

*   **Correct Answer D ($0.27 \times 10 = 2.7$):**
    The calculation required is $3 \times (0.3 \times 0.3) = 3 \times 0.09 = 0.27$. **Wait—** Looking closely at the options provided in your prompt:
    *   $3 \times 0.09 = \mathbf{0.27}$.
    *   **Note:** In your list, **B is 0.27 and D is 2.7**. In a real-world classroom, if $0.27$ is available (B), many Proficient students will choose it and actually be correct. However, if they struggle with the number of decimal places, they will gravitate toward **D (2.7)** because "3 times something should make it look bigger." 

**Correction on Logic:** If the correct calculation is $3(0.09) = 0.27$, then **B** is actually the correct answer. In my experience, Year 9s find the decimal place $0.09$ very difficult, often thinking $0.3 \times 0.3 = 0.9$ or $0.3$. This makes getting to $0.27$ significantly harder than it looks.
