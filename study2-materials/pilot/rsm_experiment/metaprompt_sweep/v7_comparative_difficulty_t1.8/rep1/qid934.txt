As a UK maths teacher, I find this question fascinating because it targets a specific "weak point" in the Year 9 curriculum: moving from explicit equations ($y = mx + c$) to implicit ones ($ax + by = c$).

### **DIFFICULTY: 6/10**

While the arithmetic is simple ($10 \div 2$), the conceptual load is high for Year 9. Most students are trained to see a table and "plug in" $x$ to get $y$ immediately. This question requires a multi-step algebraic process: substituting $x=0$, simplifying to $2y=10$, and then solving for $y$.

### **Predicting Response Distributions**

**Key Misconception Activation:**
*   **The "First Number" Trap:** Many students see $4x$, see $x=0$, and ignore that term, but then focus on the $10$ or the $4$.
*   **The Operation Reversal:** Students often see $4x + 2y = 10$ and mistakenly perform $10 \div 4$ (leading to D) because they associate the $x$ coefficient with the primary division step.
*   **Random Association:** Students might add $2$ and $3$ to get $5$ simply because they are the numbers visible in the equation.
