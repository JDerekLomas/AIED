As a UK maths teacher, I find this question fascinating because it targets one of the most persistent misconceptions in secondary education: the "Hierarchy of Operations" trap (specifically within BIDMAS/BODMAS).

### Difficulty Rating: 6/10
While the arithmetic itself is Year 4 level, the conceptual logic required to navigate Problem P correctly is a major stumbling block even for Year 11s. It is a high "misconception activation" question. Students don't guess; they choose an incorrect answer with absolute confidence.

**The "BIDMAS Trap":** 
Most UK students are taught "Addition then Subtraction" because A comes before S in the acronym. They fail to realise that Addition and Subtraction possess equal priority and must be solved left-to-right. Therefore, many will insist you must do $5+6$ first in Problem P.

**Advanced:** (Top Sets/Grade 8-9 trajectory)
These students understand that Addition/Subtraction and Multiplication/Division are inverse pairs with equal priority.

*   *Logic:* They see the trap immediately. Only minor "silly mistakes" (reading the options too fast) would lead them away from B.
