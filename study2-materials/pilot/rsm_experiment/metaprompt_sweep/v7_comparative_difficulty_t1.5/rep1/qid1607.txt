This is a classic "lure" question that tests structural understanding of inverse operations. For a Year 9 student, this falls into the category of a **high-misconception question.**

Students often perform the correct operation (multiplying or subtracting) but apply it to the wrong side of the equals sign or fail to maintain the balance of the equation.

### DIFFICULTY: 4/10
While the algebra itself is simple, the "trap" in Step 2 is highly effective. It is significantly easier than completing the square, but it catches students who are working on autopilot rather than checking their logic.

**Below Basic:** Students at this level often struggle with fractions in equations. They may see "divide by 4" and "add 1" and just combine numbers randomly, or they might think Isaac must be right because "it looks like maths."

### Teacher's Note on Misconception Activation:
This is a **High Misconception** question. Students rarely guess randomly here; they "confidently get it wrong." The jump from $60 = 1 + k$ to $61 = k$ is a very common "reflex error" where the brain sees the $60$ and the $1$ and performs the easiest operation (addition) regardless of the equals sign.
