In this Year 9 mock, students frequently trip up on the order of operations when rearranging equations. 

**Tom's method** represents the standard "undo the subtraction first" approach, which most students recognize as the safest path.
**Katie's method** represents a common misconception regarding multiplying through by a denominator. Students often forget that *every* term (including the -5) must be multiplied by 3 if they choose to do that first.

- **Below Basic** students often guess C because they assume both "maths sounding" statements must be valid paths, or B because "multiplying is what you do for fractions."
- **Basic** students are often taught "do the same to both sides." They see Tom added 5 (correct) and Katie multiplied by 3 (but miss that she didn't multiply the -5). This is the most common trap for this cohort.
