1.  **Hierarchy:** Multiplication comes before Addition.
2.  **Associativity/Direction:** Addition and Subtraction have equal priority and should be worked left-to-right.

**Teacher's Note on "The D Trap":** 
In UK classrooms, **Option D** is the most common "sophisticated" error. Students who are taught BIDMAS as a rigid hierarchy often calculate $4 - (5+6) = -7$ instead of $(4-5) + 6 = 5$. Distinguishing between the Proficient and Advanced levels usually comes down to whether they understand that **AS** (Addition/Subtraction) and **DM** (Division/Multiplication) are tied pairs.
