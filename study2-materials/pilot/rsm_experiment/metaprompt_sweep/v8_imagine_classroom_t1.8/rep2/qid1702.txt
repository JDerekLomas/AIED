The main hurdle isn't the algebra; it's the decimal and the negative sign. When students see $y=0.5$, a large group will instinctively "half" something because $0.5$ is a half. When they see $-6y$, they often lose the negative during the transposition or add $3$ to $30$ instead of dealing with the fact that $6 \times 0.5$ is $3$.
