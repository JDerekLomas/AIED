As a UK maths teacher, I have seen thousands of students tackle powers and directed numbers. The "trap" here isn't the calculationâ€”it's the interplay between the negative base and the even exponent.

While an advanced student knows that a negative number raised to an even power is positive, a basic or "below basic" student often ignores the index rule and performs multiplication ($3 \times 4 = 12$) or correctly identifies the magnitude ($3^4 = 81$) but keeps the negative sign because the input was negative.

Crucially, many students who use a calculator will type $-3^4$ without brackets, which results in $-81$. This leads even "proficient" students to choose C more often than one might expect.
