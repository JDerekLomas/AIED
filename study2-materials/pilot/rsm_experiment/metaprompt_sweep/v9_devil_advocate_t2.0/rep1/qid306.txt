As a UK maths teacher, I have seen thousands of students grapple with the Order of Operations (BIDMAS/BODMAS). 

The primary "trap" in this question lies in Problem P. Many students are taught "Addition before Subtraction" because 'A' comes before 'S' in the acronym. They fail to realise that addition and subtraction have equal priority and should be solved left-to-right. Therefore, they will mistakenly choose "5+6" as the first step for Problem P. In Problem Q, most students recognise that multiplication comes before addition, but lower-ability students still tend to work strictly left-to-right.

**Rationale:**
*   **Below Basic:** These students often work strictly left-to-right regardless of the operators (Option A). Some may have a vague memory of BIDMAS but apply it incorrectly.
*   **Basic:** This group is split. Many have been taught BIDMAS but adhere to it literally, thinking Addition **must** happen before Subtraction, leading them to Problem P: 5+6 (Options C and D).
*   **Proficient:** These students usually get Problem Q right (multiplication first), but the "Addition before Subtraction" myth is incredibly persistent even among mid-set students, leading many to Option D when they should choose B.
*   **Advanced:** Most will correctly identify that P is left-to-right and Q is multiplication first (Option B).
