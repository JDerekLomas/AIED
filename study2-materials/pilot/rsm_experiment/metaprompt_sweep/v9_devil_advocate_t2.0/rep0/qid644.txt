Based on my experience teaching GCSE maths, this question is a classic "sign error" trap. While it appears simple (basic multiplication), students frequently struggle with negative numbers and confuse the rules of addition with the rules of multiplication.

**Below Basic:** These students often struggle with the "negative times a negative" rule. They are highly likely to see -1 and -4 and mentally "combine" them to get -5, treating the operation as -1 + (-4) or simply adding the digits and keeping the sign.

**Basic:** These students usually know that $1 \times 4 = 4$. However, many still fail the sign rule, incorrectly believing that a negative and a negative remain negative when multiplied. Some will still add the numbers instead of multiplying.

**Advanced:** These students have internalized the FOIL/Grid method and the signs involved. They are very unlikely to add the numbers, but a very small minority might still make a momentary slip with a sign.
