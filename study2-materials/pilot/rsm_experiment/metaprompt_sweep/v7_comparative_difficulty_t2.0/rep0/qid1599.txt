### Teacher's Analysis of Misconceptions

*   **Choice A (Correct):** Students recognize that the expression following the division sign becomes the denominator in its entirety.
*   **Choice B (The "Symmetry" Trap):** Popular with **Below Basic** students. They see " $+ 4$ " in the brackets and feel it looks more "balanced" or "algebraic" if there is a " $+ 4$ " on top as well. This is a pure pattern-matching error.
*   **Choice C (The BODMAS/Order of Ops error):** This is a high-activation misconception. Students think the division only applies to the $h$. They essentially ignore the brackets, reading the expression as $c \div h + 4$. Even some **Basic** and **Proficient** students might slip up here if they are rushing and forget that the fraction bar acts as a bracket.
*   **Choice D (The Reverse Trap):** These students know it becomes a fraction but have the numerator and denominator backwards. In Year 9, a common misconception is "the bigger number (or the one with more terms) goes on the bottom," or they simply process the expression from right to left.
