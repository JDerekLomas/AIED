This is a classic "bread and butter" topic for the KS3 curriculum. Expanding double brackets is typically introduced in Year 8 and solidified in Year 9.

### DIFFICULTY: 4/10

While it requires multiple steps (FOIL or the Grid Method), the algebra itself is linear. The primary challenge here isn't the process of multiplication, but **directed number arithmetic**. Year 9 students often "confidently get it wrong" because they remember the algebra steps but slip up on the signsâ€”specifically, the "double negative" for the constant term and the "addition of negatives" for the coefficient of $m$.

### Predicted Response Distributions

**Key Misconceptions:**
*   **A ($m^2 - 2m - 35$):** This represents a student "finding the difference" between 7 and 5 for the middle term and failing the negative multiplication rules for the end.
*   **C ($m^2 + 12m + 35$):** The student knows that a negative times a negative is a positive, but wrongly applies that logic to the *addition* of $-5m$ and $-7m$.
*   **D ($m^2 - 12m - 35$):** The most common error. The student adds the negatives correctly for the middle term but fails the "negative $\times$ negative = positive" rule for the constant.

---

#### below_basic: A=25% B=15% C=30% D=30%
*These students are likely guessing or applying incorrect sign rules across the board. They often think $-5 + -7$ is $+12$ or that $-5 \times -7$ must be negative because there are "so many minus signs."*

#### basic: A=15% B=45% C=10% D=30%
*These students have the "FOIL" or "Grid" method down but are prone to a single sign error. Option D is a very "sticky" distractor here; they get the $-12m$ but lapse on the final $-5 \times -7$.*

#### proficient: A=5% B=85% C=0% D=10%
*These students are fluent in double bracket expansion. Most errors will be due to rushing or a "slip of the pen" on the final sign. Option C is almost never picked here as they know the middle term must be negative.*

#### advanced: A=1% B=98% C=0% D=1%
*At this level, the process is automated. Only an extreme lapse in concentration leads to an incorrect answer. They will often perform this expansion mentally in seconds.*

---

**Correct Answer: B**
$(m \times m) + (m \times -7) + (-5 \times m) + (-5 \times -7)$
$= m^2 - 7m - 5m + 35$
$= m^2 - 12m + 35$
