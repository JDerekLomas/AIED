### Teacher's Analysis of Misconceptions:

1.  **Distractor D ($\frac{h+4}{c}$): The "Division is Commutative" Myth.** This is the most common error for weaker students. They often place the "larger" looking part of the expression on top or simply forget that order matters in division ($a \div b \neq b \div a$).
2.  **Distractor C ($\frac{c}{h} + 4$): The BIDMAS/Grouping Failure.** These students don't realise that the vinculum (the horizontal fraction bar) acts as a grouping symbol. They apply the division to the $h$ and "add 4" at the end, essentially treating it as $c \div h + 4$.
3.  **Distractor B ($\frac{c+4}{h+4}$): The Over-extender.** Rare in Year 9, but seen in students who think they must "balance" or apply an operation to both the numerator and denominator unnecessarily.

**Summary:** For a Year 9 class, this is an excellent "Check for Understanding" diagnostic question at the start of a lesson on algebraic fractions. If more than 15% of the class chooses D or C, it indicates a need to revisit the definition of the vinculum before proceeding.
