This is a classic "subject-manipulation" question that bridges the gap between Geometry and Algebra. For a typical Year 9 student, this is significantly more difficult than it looks, primarily because it requires multiple steps of algebraic inversion involving fractions and a non-standard root.

### DIFFICULTY: 8/10
While Year 9s are taught the volume of a sphere formula $V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$, they rarely practice rearranging it to make $r$ the subject. Most Year 9 practice involves substituting values *into* the formula. Converting a "word formula" into an abstract algebraic manipulation is a High Level 6/Low Level 7 skill in the UK curriculum.

*   **Common Pitfall:** The most frequent error is multiplying by 4 and dividing by 3 (retaining the fraction's original orientation), rather than multiplying by 3 and dividing by 4.
