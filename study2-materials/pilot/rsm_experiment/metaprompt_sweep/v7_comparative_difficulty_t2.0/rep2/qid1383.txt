### DIFFICULTY: 6/10
While the operation is simple (it's not long division or complex algebra), it scores a 6 because it is a **high-misconception** task. Even students who understand indices often trip over the negative sign. In a UK classroom, this is a classic "Grade 4" GCSE-style foundation/higher crossover question.

### Analysis of the "Confidence in Error"
This is not a "random guess" question. Students will be very confident in their wrong answers. 
*   **The Sign Trap:** Many believe that if the input is negative, the output must be negative. 
*   **The Operation Trap:** Weaker students see a small 4 and a big 3 and multiply them.
*   **The Calculator Trap:** If they use a non-CAS calculator and type $-3^4$, it will give $-81$ (due to BIDMAS), reinforcing the misconception.
