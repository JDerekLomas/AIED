This is a staple question for the UK Year 9 curriculum. It tests two distinct skills: the algebraic structure of expanding double brackets (FOIL/Grid method) and the application of directed number rules (integer arithmetic).

### **DIFFICULTY: 4/10**

While expanding brackets is a "Grade 4" (GCSE foundation) skill, the "double negative" makes this a classic trap. Students in Year 9 often grasp the "method" of multiplication quickly but stumble on the final constant term $(-5 \times -7)$.

**The Misconception Profile:**
*   **High Confidence/High Error:** This is not a "random guess" question. Students who get this wrong usually do so with high confidence because they successfully performed three out of the four multiplications correctly but failed the signs.
*   **The "Double Negative" Trap:** Option D is the most "attractive" wrong answer for middle-ability students who correctly sum $-5$ and $-7$ to get $-12$ but believe that "a negative and a negative stay negative" when multiplying.
