### The Mathematical Thinking behind the options:
*   **A (20):** Operational error. The student multiplies all visible numbers $(4 \times 5 \text{ or } 2 \times 10)$ or performs $4 + 10 + 2 + 4$.
*   **B (3):** Calculation error/Inverse error. Student correctly identifies $x=0$, simplifies to $2y=10$, but then calculates $10 - 2 + 0$ or makes a systematic error in division. Or, more likely, they guess a small integer because it "looks right" in a sequence.
*   **C (5):** **Correct answer.** Substitution of $x=0$ leading to $2y=10$, therefore $y=5$.
*   **D (2.5):** Reverse substitution/Confusion. The student thinks they need to solve for $x$ when $y=0$ (finding the x-intercept instead of the y-intercept) or incorrectly divides 10 by 4.
