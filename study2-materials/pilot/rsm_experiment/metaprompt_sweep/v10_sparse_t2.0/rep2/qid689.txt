### Teacher Analysis of Distractors:
*   **A (0.5):** The Correct Answer ($5 \div 10$).
*   **B (50):** The **Inverse Error.** The student sees a fraction (division) and tries to undo it by multiplying the two visible numbers ($10 \times 5$). 
*   **C (2):** The **Commutative Error.** The student assumes division is commutative ($10 \div 5$) or follows the pattern "Big $\div$ Small." This is the most common error for students who lack confidence in algebra.
*   **D (No solution):** Chosen by students who feel $5$ is "too small" to be divided into 10 without knowing about fractions/decimals.
