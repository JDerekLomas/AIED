### The Mathematical Analysis
*   **Correct Logic:** Substitute $x = 0$ into $4(0) + 2y = 10$, resulting in $2y = 10$, so $y = 5$. (**Answer C**)
*   **Common Misconception 1 (A):** The "Operation Switch." The student sees $x=0$ and adds the coefficients $4+2$ then doubles it or performs $4 \times 0$ but then mistakenly calculates $(10 \times 2)$. 
*   **Common Misconception 2 (B):** Guessing or "Pattern Making." Students often assume $y$ decreases by 1 each time, or they pick a "maths-looking" small number like 3 if they get stuck on the algebra.
*   **Common Misconception 3 (D):** The "Divide Everything" error. Students see 10 and 4 and perform $10 \div 4 = 2.5$.
