**The Logic Behind the Wrong Answers:**

*   **Option D (The "Double Negative" Trap):** This is where most of my "Basic" and lower "Proficient" students will fall. They correctly add $-5$ and $-7$ to get $-12m$, but in their heads, they are thinking "negative, negative... my answer must be negative." They forget that multiplying two negatives produces a positive. They see the minus signs in the brackets and reflexively look for an answer with minus signs.
*   **Option A (The "Difference" Error):** This is my "Below Basic" group. Instead of seeing two negatives to be added, they find the difference between 7 and 5. They do $7 - 5 = 2$ and attach the middle term to it. They usually also get the sign of the constant wrong for the same reasons as above.
*   **Option C (The "Ignore the Signs" Group):** These students have the mechanics of FOIL/Grid Method down, but they find negatives "annoying" so they subconsciously ignore them.
