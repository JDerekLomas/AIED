In my experience, the acronym **BIDMAS** (or BODMAS) is a double-edged sword. While students remember that 'M' and 'D' come before 'A' and 'S', many take the linear order of the acronym literally. This leads to the most common misconception in Year 9: believing that Addition *must* be performed before Subtraction simply because the letter 'A' precedes 'S' in the mnemonic.

**below_basic: A=10% B=10% C=30% D=50%**
These students (about 6 in the class) often hyper-fixate on the acronym. For Problem P, they see 'Addition' comes before 'Subtraction' in BIDMAS, so they stubbornly look for the addition first (\(5+6\)). For Problem Q, they know multiplication is 'stronger' than addition, but their cognitive load is high, and a few will still default to reading left-to-right.

**basic: A=05% B=30% C=20% D=45%**
These students (about 10 in the class) are getting better at spotting the multiplication in Problem Q. However, Problem P is their undoing. They have been taught BIDMAS so rigidly in Primary school that they find it physically uncomfortable to do a subtraction before an addition, even when moving left-to-right is the correct mathematical convention.

**proficient: A=00% B=70% C=05% D=25%**
These students (about 9 in the class) are starting to understand that Addition and Subtraction have equal priority. They remember me telling them "they are a team" and to work left-to-right.
