## Step 1: Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions for easier calculation

## 2: Find a common denominator for the fractions
To add \(\frac{27}{10}\) and \(\frac{9}{4}\), we need a common denominator. The least common multiple of 10 and 4 is 20. So, we convert both fractions: \(\frac{27}{10} = \frac{27*2}{10*2} = \frac{54}{20}\) and \(\frac{9}{4} = \frac{9*5}{4*5} = \frac{45}{20}\).

## 3: Add the fractions
Now, we add \(\frac{54}{20} + \frac{45}{20} = \frac{99}{20}\).

## 4: Convert the improper fraction back to a mixed number
\(\frac{99}{20}\) can be converted to a mixed number: \(4 \frac{19}{20}\) because \(99 \div 20 = 4\) remainder \(19\).
