## Step 1: Understanding the formula for the volume of a sphere
The volume \(V\) of a sphere is given by the formula \(V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3\), where \(r\) is the radius of the sphere. To find the radius \(r\) given the volume \(V\), we need to rearrange this formula to solve for \(r\).

## 2: Deriving the formula for the radius of a sphere
Starting with \(V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3\), we can solve for \(r\):
\[r^3 = \frac{3V}{4\pi}\]
Taking the cube root of both sides gives:
\[r = \sqrt[3]{\frac{3V}{4\pi}}\]
