## Step 1: Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions for easier calculation
To solve the given problem \( 2 \frac{7}{10}+2 \frac{1}{4} \), first convert the mixed numbers into improper fractions. \( 2 \frac{7}{10} = \frac{(2*10) + 7}{10} = \frac{20 + 7}{10} = \frac{27}{10} \) and \( 2 \frac{1}{4} = \frac{(2*4) + 1}{4} = \frac{8 + 1}{4} = \frac{9}{4} \).

## 2: Find a common denominator for the improper fractions
To add \( \frac{27}{10} \) and \( \frac{9}{4} \), find a common denominator. The least common multiple (LCM) of 10 and 4 is 20. So, convert both fractions: \( \frac{27}{10} = \frac{27*2}{10*2} = \frac{54}{20} \) and \( \frac{9}{4} = \frac{9*5}{4*5} = \frac{45}{20} \).

## 3: Add the fractions
Now add them: \( \frac{54}{20} + \frac{45}{20} = \frac{99}{20} \).

## 4: Convert the sum back to a mixed number
To convert \( \frac{99}{20} \) into a mixed number: \( \frac{99}{20} = 4 \frac{19}{20} \).
