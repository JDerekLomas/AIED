In my experience marking these papers, Problem P is a notorious "BIDMAS trap." Many students over-rely on the acronym and assume Addition *must* come before Subtraction, rather than treating them with equal priority from left to right. Problem Q is a standard test of Order of Operations.

**Calculation Errors & Traps:**
*   **The "Addition Priority" Trap (Problem P):** This leads students to A or C. They see the 'A' before the 'S' in BIDMAS and mistakenly believe they must do $5+6$ first, often leading them to an incorrect answer of $4-11=-7$.
*   **The "Read Left-to-Right" Trap (Problem Q):** This leads students to A or B. Students ignore operation hierarchy and simply process the numbers as they appear ($4+5=9$, then $9 \times 6$).
*   **Advanced Students:** They recognize that for P, you work left-to-right (Subtraction and Addition are equal priority), and for Q, Multiplication takes precedence.
