While the operation is simple (division by 3), the fraction $1/12$ triggers a common cognitive shortcut where students perform $12 \div 3$ instead of $\frac{1}{12} \div 3$.

**Analysis of the Distractors:**
*   **Option A ($1/4$):** This is the most common error. Students see the 3 and the 12 and immediately think "3 fits into 12 four times," essentially calculating $1/(12 \div 3)$ or confusing the operation with multiplication by 3.
*   **Option C ($4$):** This is chosen by students who ignore the fraction structure entirely or perform $12 \div 3$ and move the result to the numerator.
*   **Option D ($1/9$):** A rarer error where students might misread $1/12$ as $1/3$ or perform $3 \times 3$ erroneously.

Below basic students will largely gravitate towards A and C because they recognize the 3-4-12 number bond. Basic students often know they need to divide, but struggle with the mechanics of dividing a fraction by an integer.
