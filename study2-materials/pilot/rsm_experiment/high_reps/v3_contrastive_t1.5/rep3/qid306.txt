This question is a classic test of the "Order of Operations" (BIDMAS/BODMAS) and uncovers two very specific developmental hurdles in secondary school mathematics.

**Problem P ($4-5+6$)** tests the common misconception that Addition must always be performed before Subtraction because 'A' comes before 'S' in BIDMAS. In reality, they have equal priority and should be done left-to-right. This is the primary "trap" for mid-to-high ability students who have learned the acronym but not the nuanced rule.
**Problem Q ($4+5 \times 6$)** tests the standard hierarchy of Multiplication over Addition. This is generally better understood than the P-addition trap.
