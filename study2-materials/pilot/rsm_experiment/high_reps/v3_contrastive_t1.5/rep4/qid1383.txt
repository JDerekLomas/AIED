This question tests two distinct concepts: index notation (the power of 4) and the application of even powers to negative bases.

**Analysis:**
1.  **The Negative Sign Trap:** This is the primary hurdle. Students often calculate $3^4 = 81$ and then reflexively re-attach the negative sign, or they believe that because the input is negative, the output must be negative.
2.  **Multiplication vs. Exponents:** Lower-ability students frequently confuse $k^4$ with $k \times 4$.
3.  **Calculation Error:** $3^4$ is $3 \times 3 \times 3 \times 3$. Some students lose track and calculate $3 \times 3 \times 3 = 27$ or make a arithmetic slip.

The choice of $k=-3$ is particularly effective because $3^4$ is large enough to invite errors, and the "even power" rule is one of the most common misconceptions in GCSE Algebra.
