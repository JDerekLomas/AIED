As an experienced teacher, I evaluate this question as one that tests a student's fluency with dividing fractions, specifically the interaction between a whole number coefficient and a fractional constant.

The common pitfall here is the "intuitive" operation. Many students at the lower ability levels see '3' and '12' and immediately think of the relationship $12 \div 3 = 4$. This leads them to either A ($1/4$) or C ($4$). Option A is particularly dangerous because it "looks" like a fraction, which fits the context of the question. Option D is a less common error involving squaring the coefficient or confusion with the number 3 being the square root of 9.

High-ability students will correctly identify the operation as $p = \frac{1}{12} \div 3$, which is $\frac{1}{12} \times \frac{1}{3} = \frac{1}{36}$.
