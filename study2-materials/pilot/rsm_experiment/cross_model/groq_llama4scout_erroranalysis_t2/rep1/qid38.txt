## Step 1: Understand the question and the common errors
The question asks students to simplify the expression \(\frac{3}{2x} + \frac{4}{x}\) into a single fraction. The correct approach involves finding a common denominator, which in this case would be \(2x\), and then adding the fractions.

## 2: Identify the correct solution
To add these fractions, we convert \(\frac{4}{x}\) into \(\frac{4 \times 2}{x \times 2} = \frac{8}{2x}\). Then, \(\frac{3}{2x} + \frac{8}{2x} = \frac{3 + 8}{2x} = \frac{11}{2x}\). So, the correct answer is A) \(\frac{11}{2x}\).

- Option D) \(\frac{7}{2x}\): This could result from students adding the numerators \(3 + 4 = 7\) without considering the denominator \(2x\) for the first fraction, effectively treating it as \(\frac{3}{2} + 4\).
