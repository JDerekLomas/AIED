## Step 1: Understand the question
The question requires students to compare two expressions: \(8+12 \div 4\) and \((8+12) \div 4\), and determine which symbol (\( <, >, =, \geq \)) belongs in the box to make the statement true.

## 2: Solve the expressions correctly
First, solve \(8+12 \div 4\). Following the order of operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS: Parentheses/Brackets, Exponents/Orders, Multiplication and Division from left to right, Addition and Subtraction from left to right), we get:
\(8 + 12 \div 4 = 8 + 3 = 11\).

Then, solve \((8+12) \div 4\):
\((8 + 12) \div 4 = 20 \div 4 = 5\).

## 3: Compare the expressions
Since \(11 > 5\), the correct symbol to use is \(>\).

## 4: Analyze potential errors for each option
- Option A (\( > \)) is correct based on the calculation.
- Option B (\( < \)) would be chosen by students who incorrectly think \(5 > 11\) or mistakenly calculate the expressions.
- Option C (\( = \)) would be chosen by students who mistakenly calculate both expressions to be equal, possibly due to incorrect application of operations or misunderstanding of the order of operations.
- Option D (\( \geq \)) would be considered by students who think the expressions could be equal or the first one is greater, but not exclusively.
