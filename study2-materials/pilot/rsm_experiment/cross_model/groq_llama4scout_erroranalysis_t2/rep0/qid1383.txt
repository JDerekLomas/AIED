## Step 1: Understand the question and the possible errors
The question requires students to substitute \( k = -3 \) into the equation \( T = k^4 \) to find \( T \). The correct calculation is \( T = (-3)^4 = 81 \). We need to consider common errors that could lead to the incorrect answers provided.

## 2: Analyze option A
Option A is \( 81 \), which is the correct answer. Students who understand the order of operations and know that an even power of a negative number is positive will choose this.

## 3: Analyze option B
Option B is \( 12 \). This could result from a calculation error where a student incorrectly applies the power, such as \( (-3)^4 = -3 \times 4 = -12 \) or mistakenly uses \( (-3)^2 = 9 \) and then \( 9 \times -3 = -27 \) but incorrect substitution or an error in mental math could plausibly lead to incorrect values. However, directly getting 12 seems less likely without specific miscalculation like confusing \( k^4 \) with \( 4k \) implicitly.

## 4: Analyze option C
Option C is \( -81 \). This is an attractive error because students might forget that raising to an even power negates the negative sign, thus incorrectly calculating \( (-3)^4 = -81 \) directly without applying the rule that \( (-a)^{even} \) yields a positive result.

## 5: Analyze option D
Option D is \( -12 \). This error could arise from a mistaken application of the index, possibly through a confusion with \( k^1 \) or another power and sign error combined.
