## Step 1: Understand the question
The question asks for the radius of a sphere given its volume \(V \mathrm{~cm}^{3}\). The formula for the volume of a sphere is \(V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3\), where \(r\) is the radius.

## 2: Recall the formula and solve for \(r\)
To find \(r\), we rearrange the formula: \(r^3 = \frac{3V}{4\pi}\). Taking the cube root of both sides gives \(r = \sqrt[3]{\frac{3V}{4\pi}}\).

## 4: Identify the correct option
The correct formula for \(r\) is \(\sqrt[3]{\frac{3V}{4\pi}}\), which matches Option C.
