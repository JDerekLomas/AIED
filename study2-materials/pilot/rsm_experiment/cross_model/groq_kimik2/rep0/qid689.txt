Below basic students often do not reverse the operation correctly for equations such as 10 = 10 ÷ 5 , or treat only the digits before/after the equals sign.  
Average working on this item is “what times what is ten when five is involved?”, which is why B is attractive; very few manage to cross-multiply p·10 and get p = 2.
