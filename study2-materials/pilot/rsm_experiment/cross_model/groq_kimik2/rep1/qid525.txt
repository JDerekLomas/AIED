1. Need to handle different denominators (10 and 4) which don't divide conveniently
2. Need to find LCM of 20
3. The fraction parts sum to 7/10 + 1/4 = 7/10 + 2.5/10 = no, wait... they'd need to find common denominator
4. Must give answer as a mixed number, not improper fraction
5. Option A gives the right fraction but wrong format (improper), tricking those ignoring the instruction
6. Option C uses wrong denominator when adding numerators directly
7. Option D adds both numerators and denominators - very common misconception

The biggest trap in this specific question is that students will likely add 1/4 + 7/10, struggle with the common denominator (would need 9/20) and may give up. However, the fraction parts alone work out cleanly to 9/20, which is just under a whole, so there's no carrying complexity that trips students up.
