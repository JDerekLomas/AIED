**Analysis of the Question:**

This question tests understanding of linear equations and the ability to substitute values into an equation to find a missing value. The specific equation is 4x + 2y = 10. Students need to substitute x = 0 and solve for y. While the underlying concept is reasonably straightforward, there are a couple of potential pitfalls:

*   **Misinterpretation of the Table:** Some students may struggle to correctly identify where to substitute the `x` value.
*   **Algebraic Manipulation Errors:** Even if they substitute correctly, some students may make errors when rearranging the equation to isolate `y`.
*   **Arithmetic Errors:** Simple arithmetic mistakes can occur during the calculation.
*   **Thinking ahead rather than working with what is provided:** A student could try to find a general solution for y in terms of x, rather than focus on the case where x = 0.
