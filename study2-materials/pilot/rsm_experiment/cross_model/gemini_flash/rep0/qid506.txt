As an experienced teacher, I evaluate this question as one that specifically tests "fractional fluency" rather than just algebraic manipulation. While the step required (dividing by 3) is basic, the interaction with the fraction $\frac{1}{12}$ is a significant hurdle.

Many students at lower ability levels will instinctively multiply $3 \times 12$ but place the result in the numerator (Option C) or perform a "matching" operation where they think $3$ into $12$ goes $4$ times (Option A). The core misconception being tested is whether the student understands that dividing a fraction by an integer results in a smaller fraction (multiplying the denominator).

**Option Analysis:**
- **A ($1/4$):** Common for students who think "3 goes into 12 four times" and keep the reciprocal structure.
- **B ($1/36$):** The correct answer ($\frac{1}{12} \div 3 = \frac{1}{36}$).
- **C ($4$):** Extremely common for weaker students who ignore the fraction structure entirely and just calculate $12 \div 3 = 4$.
- **D ($1/9$):** A rarer "guess" where students might mistakenly try to square or subtract.
