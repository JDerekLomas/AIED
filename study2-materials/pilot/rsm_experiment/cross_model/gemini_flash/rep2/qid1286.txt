This question tests a student's ability to rearrange a formula involving a cube root, a fraction, and the constant $\pi$. While the formula for the volume of a sphere ($V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$) is usually provided or memorized, the algebraic manipulation required to isolate $r$ is a common stumbling block.

**Analysis:**
1.  **The Fraction Flip:** The most common error is failing to correctly multiply by the denominator (3) and divide by the numerator (4). This leads students to Option B ($\frac{4}{3\pi}$).
2.  **The Placement of $\pi$:** Students often struggle with whether $\pi$ is in the numerator or denominator. Those who treat $\frac{4}{3}\pi$ as a single term to be "divided away" often incorrectly end up with versions like Option A if they rush the reciprocal.
3.  **Algebraic Order:** Option D represents a fundamental misunderstanding of the order of operations, where the cube root is applied only to $V$ before the coefficients are dealt with.
4.  **Target (C):** This is the correct rearrangement: $3V = 4\pi r^3 \Rightarrow \frac{3V}{4\pi} = r^3 \Rightarrow \sqrt[3]{\frac{3V}{4\pi}} = r$.
