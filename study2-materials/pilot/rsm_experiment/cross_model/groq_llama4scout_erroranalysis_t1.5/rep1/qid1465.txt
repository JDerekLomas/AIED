## Step 1: Understand the question and the order of operations
The question requires the evaluation of the expression \(3 - 5 + 4 \times 2\). According to the order of operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS), multiplication should be performed before addition and subtraction. So, the correct calculation is \(3 - 5 + 8 = 6\).

## 2: Identify the wrong answers and the calculation errors leading to them
- Option A: \(6\) is the correct answer.
- Option B: \(4\) could result from \(3 - 5 + 4 = 2 \times 2\), but more likely from incorrect order of operations, such as \(3 - (5 + 4) \times 2 = 3 - 9 \times 2 = 3 - 18 = -15\) or simply adding/subtracting from left to right without considering multiplication first, leading to \(3 - 5 = -2\), then \(-2 + 4 = 2\), and \(2 \times 2 = 4\). However, a more straightforward mistake would be \(3 - 5 = -2\), then \(-2 + 4 = 2\), and multiplying \(2 \times 2 = 4\), but this does not directly lead to B without additional incorrect steps.
- Option C: \(-12\) could come from \(-5 + 4 \times 2 = -5 + 8 = 3\), then \(3 - 5 = -2\), no, more likely \(-5 \times 4 \times 2 = -40\) or simply performing operations out of order, such as \(4 \times 2 = 8\), then \(3 - 5 + 8 = 6\), not \(-12\). A mistake could be \(3 - (5 + 4 \times 2)\) or similar, yielding \(3 - (5 + 8) = 3 - 13 = -10\), not \(-12\).
