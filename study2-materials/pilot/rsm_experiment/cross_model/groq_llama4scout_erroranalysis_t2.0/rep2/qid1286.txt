## Step 1: Understanding the Question
The question requires students to find the radius of a sphere given its volume \(V\). The formula for the volume of a sphere is \(V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3\), where \(r\) is the radius. To find \(r\), the formula needs to be rearranged.

## 2: Rearranging the Volume Formula
Rearranging the volume formula to solve for \(r\):
\[V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3\]
\[V \cdot \frac{3}{4\pi} = r^3\]
\[r = \sqrt[3]{\frac{3V}{4\pi}}\]
