PREREQUISITES:
1. **Basic English Sentence Structure:** Understanding that a sentence needs a subject and a verb (even if not explicitly taught as 'subject' and 'verb').
2. **Noun Recognition:** Identifying "apples" and "basket" as things (nouns).
3. **Quantifier Vocabulary:** Knowing the *meaning* of "some" and "any" – this is the core concept. Crucially, understanding "some" implies a positive quantity, even if small.
4. **"Some" vs. "Any" - Positive vs. Negative/Question Context:** Understanding that "some" is generally used in affirmative statements (there *are* apples), while "any" is often used in negatives (there are *not* apples) or questions (are there *any* apples?). This is a subtle but vital distinction.
5. **Reading Comprehension (Basic):** Ability to decode the sentence and understand its literal meaning – that it's describing the presence or absence of apples.
6. **Ability to follow instructions:** Understanding the command "Choose the correct word."
7. **Visual Discrimination:** Being able to visually distinguish between "some" and "any" (important for students still developing reading fluency
