## Analysis of Question Difficulty: "some/any" in Grade 6 English (Indian Government Schools)

This question, while seemingly simple, has a surprisingly nuanced difficulty level for Grade 6 students in Indian government schools. Here's a breakdown:

**What could go wrong?**

* **L1 Interference:** Many students in Indian government schools come from linguistic backgrounds where a direct equivalent of "some/any" doesn't exist, or the usage differs significantly. They might attempt a literal translation from their L1, leading to incorrect choices.
* **Rule Memorization vs. Conceptual Understanding:** Students might be taught rules about "some" with countable nouns and "any" with uncountable nouns *without* understanding the underlying concept of quantity and affirmation/negation. This leads to rote application that fails when the context isn't perfectly aligned with the rule.
* **Contextual Understanding:** The question relies on understanding that the presence of apples (even a few) implies *some* quantity. Students might overthink and look for a more complex grammatical structure.
